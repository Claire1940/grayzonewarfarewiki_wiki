## Understanding the Quest System and Vendors

Before heading into the field, you must understand how to interact with the three primary vendors. Each vendor provides unique rewards and specializes in different types of gear. Progressing their individual questlines is the primary method for leveling up your character and gaining access to better attachments, medical supplies, and armor.

| Vendor | Specialization | Primary Quest Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Handshake** | Logistics & Security | Reconnaissance and eliminations |
| **Gunny** | Weapons & Tactical Gear | Item retrieval and marking locations |
| **Lab Rat** | Medical & Science | Finding medical supplies and biological data |

To accept a task, open your **Tab** menu, navigate to the **Vendors** tab, select a vendor, and click the **Tasks** button. It is crucial to check your in-game messages after accepting certain tasks, as items like trackers or keys are often sent directly to your inbox rather than appearing in your inventory automatically.

> **💡 Tip:** Always move quest-critical items like trackers or unique keys into your **Safe Lockbox**. If you die with these items in your pockets or backpack, you will lose them and may be forced to restart the objective.

## Phase 1: The Initial Reconnaissance

The first set of tasks introduces you to the local town near your faction's base. These missions are designed to familiarize you with the Landing Zone (LZ) mechanics and basic navigation.

### First Recon (Handshake)
This task requires you to visit three distinct landmarks in the starting town:
1.  **Gas Station:** Located in the southeast corner of the town. Expect 3-5 AI guards in this area.
2.  **Convenience Store:** Situated north of the lumber yard.
3.  **Demolished Building:** Found in the northern sector of town, easily identifiable by a large yellow excavator.

### Meds Wanted (Lab Rat)
Head to the town's water tower—a massive structure visible from almost anywhere in the village. Directly beneath the tower is a medical site characterized by white tents and blue logos. 
*   **Objective:** Enter the building in the northwest corner of the medical site.
*   **Item:** Retrieve the **White Medical Box** from the shelves in the back room.

### Cash Retrieval (Gunny)
This is your first mission requiring trackers. Retrieve the trackers from your messages and place them on two specific red-topped wooden crates:
*   **Lumberyard:** Inside a small shed on the east side of the yard.
*   **Warehouse:** In a small exterior room on the west side of the warehouse complex.

## Phase 2: Search and Rescue Operations

Once you have completed the initial reconnaissance, the vendors will assign more dangerous tasks that require venturing further from the safety of the initial Landing Zones. This **gray zone warfare all quests complete guide** emphasizes the importance of unlocking new LZs during these runs.

### Little Bird Down (Handshake)
This quest involves locating a crashed helicopter and searching for survivors.
*   **Location:** South of the main gas station, typically near the Charlie 3 LZ.
*   **Steps:** Approach the burnt-out chopper in the woods to trigger the first objective. Then, head to the nearby house to the north to find the bodies of the crew. Clearing the house of AI is mandatory before you can safely confirm the status of the crew.

### Lost and Found (Lab Rat)
You are tasked with finding a missing clerk at the local construction shop.
*   **Location:** The construction shop building is located just north of the lumber yard.
*   **Objective:** Find the body on the floor inside the shop.
*   **Item:** Pick up the **Necklace** found between the clerk's hands. Immediately place this in your lockbox.

### No Man Left Behind (Gunny)
Similar to the Cash Retrieval mission, you must locate a specific body and mark it for extraction.
*   **Location:** Look for the body in the alleyways/backyards just south of the Town Hall.
*   **Action:** Use a tracker on the body to complete the objective.

## Advanced Starter Tasks and Key Management

As you progress through the **gray zone warfare all quests complete guide**, you will encounter tasks that rely on RNG (Random Number Generation) for completion, specifically regarding keys.

### Restoring Order (Handshake)
This is a straightforward elimination task. You must kill **10 enemies** within the town limits. While simple, use this opportunity to loot every body you drop. Enemies in the starter town have a high chance of carrying keys needed for future quests.

### Medical Detective (Gunny)
This quest is often a bottleneck for new players because it requires the **KV Dock Key**.
*   **Objective:** Find missing medical supplies in the medical building at grid **141 166**.
*   **Requirements:** You cannot enter the room without the specific key, which drops randomly from AI enemies in the town. 

> **Warning:** If you die while attempting to retrieve a quest item (like the necklace or medical box), the item will despawn from your inventory. You must return to the original location to pick it up again.

## Tactical Extraction and Death Mechanics

Success in Gray Zone Warfare is not just about completing the objective; it is about getting home alive. When you finish a task, open your map (**M**) and locate the nearest LZ. Click the LZ and select **Call Transport**. A helicopter will arrive within a few minutes to take you back to base.

If you die during a quest:
1.  **Your body remains:** All your gear and non-protected quest items stay on your corpse.
2.  **The "Second Death" Rule:** If you die a second time before reaching your original body, the first body (and all its loot) will **despawn**.
3.  **Squad Recovery:** If playing with friends, they can right-click your body and "Drop All" to move your loot to the ground. This prevents the loot from despawning if you die again on your way back.

## FAQ

**Q: Which faction should I choose in Gray Zone Warfare?**

A: Your choice of faction (Lamang Recovery Initiative, Mithras Errant Force, or Crimson Shield International) determines your starting location on the map and which players you can squad up with. Ensure you and your friends pick the same faction, as you cannot change this without a character reset.

**Q: How do I find specific quest locations if the map doesn't show them?**

A: Many tasks in the **gray zone warfare all quests complete guide** rely on grid coordinates (e.g., 141 166). Zoom in on your map to see the grid numbers. For descriptive quests (like "near the gas station"), look for landmarks like water towers or smoke plumes.

**Q: What happens if I lose a quest item like a tracker?**

A: If you lose a tracker or a key provided by a vendor, check your messages again. If it is not there, you may need to purchase a replacement from the vendor's trade menu or find one in the world.

**Q: Can I complete quests in PVE mode?**

A: Yes, Gray Zone Warfare allows you to progress through all tasks in both PVEVP and pure PVE modes. Your progress, loot, and vendor reputation carry over between both modes.
