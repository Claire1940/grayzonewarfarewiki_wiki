Navigating the dense jungles and war-torn settlements of Lamang Island requires more than just a steady aim; it demands keen investigative skills. The **fire and water gray zone warfare** mission is a primary example of how developer MADFINGER Games integrates environmental storytelling into its tactical gameplay. Assigned by the vendor Gunny, this task sends operators into a volatile region northeast of the Sawmill to uncover the truth behind a brutal skirmish between local factions.

Completing the **fire and water gray zone warfare** objective is essential for players looking to boost their reputation with Gunny and unlock higher-tier equipment in 2026. Unlike standard "search and destroy" missions, this task focuses on intelligence gathering within a "burned village" atmosphere that is as haunting as it is dangerous. In this guide, we will break down the exact coordinates, the specific items you need to interact with, and the tactical precautions necessary to survive the aftermath of the firefight.

## Fire and Water Task Overview

Before heading out to the field, it is vital to understand the parameters of the mission. Gunny has intercepted reports of a prolonged firefight that ended in total silence. Your job is to determine who was involved and what the outcome was. This mission was introduced as part of the significant Spearhead 0.4 update, which expanded the lore surrounding the LLA (Lamang Liberation Army) and their internal conflicts.

## Navigating to the Firefight Site

The area of interest is located at a captured LLA outpost. On your tactical map, look specifically to the northeast of the Sawmill landmark. The coordinates for the primary objectives are clustered around the **144, 151** and **145, 151** grids. 

The terrain here is treacherous. The village is divided into a relatively intact section and a "burnt-down" area separated by a small wooden bridge. Expect AI resistance in the surrounding woods, as the LLA frequently patrols the perimeter of the Sawmill to protect their supply lines.

### Objective 1: Finding the Unknown Soldier's Notes

The first part of the investigation leads you to the main building of the outpost at coordinates **144, 151**. This structure serves as the entrance to the village and is easily identifiable by its large front porch and elevated staircase.

Follow these steps to secure the notes:
1. Approach the main building from the southwest to avoid being spotted by snipers in the burnt village.
2. Ascend the wooden staircase onto the front porch.
3. Move through the front room into the back office/living area.
4. Look for a **red book** (the Unknown Soldier's Diary) resting on a surface.
5. Interact with the book to "read" it; your task list should update immediately.

## Objective 2: LLA Chosen Propaganda

Once you have documented the soldier's notes, you must move deeper into the devastation. The second objective is located in the burnt-down section of the village at coordinates **145, 151**. This area is characterized by charred remains of houses and a thick layer of ash on the ground.

To find the propaganda:
1. Exit the main building and head east toward the wooden bridge.
2. Cross the bridge into the scorched residential area.
3. Search for a **burned soldier's corpse** lying amidst the rubble.
4. Underneath or directly beside the corpse, you will find the **LLA Chosen Propaganda** note.
5. Read and report the note to complete the investigation phase.

## Tactical Recommendations for 2026

The Sawmill and its surrounding outposts are notorious for "bush wookies"—AI or enemy players hiding in dense foliage. When attempting the **fire and water gray zone warfare** mission, consider the following loadout and tactical advice:

*   **Optics:** Use a 1x/4x variable scope. The village requires close-quarters clearing, but the approach from the Sawmill requires long-range spotting.
*   **Suppression:** A suppressor is mandatory. Engaging AI loudly will draw reinforcements from the Sawmill, potentially pinning you down in the burnt village with no cover.
*   **Extraction:** The nearest Landing Zone (LZ) can be hot. Always pop smoke or wait for a clear perimeter before calling in your Little Bird for extraction.
*   **Pace:** Do not rush the bridge. The bridge between the two village sections is a fatal funnel. Use grenades or flashbangs if you suspect enemies are lurking in the burnt ruins.

## Narrative Significance: Who are the "Chosen"?

The documents found during this mission provide a glimpse into the splintering factions of Lamang. The "LLA Chosen Propaganda" suggests a fanatical sub-sect within the Lamang Liberation Army that believes in a scorched-earth policy. This explains the "Fire" aspect of the mission title—the total destruction of the village was likely a message sent from one faction to another. Gunny's interest in this "unstable area" hints at future missions involving these extremist groups as you progress deeper into the heart of the island.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I see the red book in the main building?**

A: If the item isn't appearing, ensure you have officially accepted the task from Gunny in the vendor menu. If it's still missing, another player might have recently interacted with it; wait a few moments for the world state to refresh or try a different server instance.

**Q: What happens if I die after reading the notes but before finding the propaganda?**

A: In **fire and water gray zone warfare**, dying while the task is active results in a failure. You will lose reputation with Gunny and must wait for the 20-minute cooldown before the task resets. You will likely need to re-read the first set of notes upon returning.

**Q: Is there a specific faction requirement for this mission?**

A: No, this is a standard side task available to all three factions (Mithras Security Systems, Lamang Recovery Initiative, and Crimson Shield International) once they have progressed sufficiently with Gunny's questline.

**Q: Can I complete this mission at night?**

A: Yes, but it is significantly more difficult. The burnt village has very few light sources, making it hard to spot the charred corpse containing the propaganda. Using Night Vision Goggles (NVGs) is highly recommended for night-time operations in this sector.
