## Essential Starter Quests and Objectives

When you first arrive at base camp, your priority should be accepting the initial tasks from Handshake, Gunny, and Labrat. These early **gray zone warfare quests** are designed to familiarize you with the local town and the mechanics of extraction. By grouping these tasks together, you can complete multiple objectives in a single deployment, saving valuable time and resources.

| Vendor | Quest Name | Primary Objective | Key Location |

| **Handshake** | First Recon | Survey three town landmarks | Gas Station, Store, Ruin |

## Finding the Secret Vendor: Vulture

One of the most unique aspects of the current 2026 meta is the inclusion of Vulture, a secret vendor who does not appear in your base menu by default. Unlike standard vendors, Vulture is a scavenger who resells gear lost by PMCs on the battlefield. To unlock him, you must physically locate him at one of the "Major Cops" (locations marked with three black dots on your map) around Lamang.

Vulture operates on a weekly rotation, typically alternating between two major locations such as **Harrison** and **Titan**. To interact with him, your faction must first capture the location. Once the main computer at the site turns yellow, Vulture will become available in your vendor list for the duration of his stay at that post.

### Why Unlock Vulture?
Vulture is essential for players looking to optimize their stash. While he sells repaired PMC weapons, his high-level rewards are the real draw. At higher reputation levels, he sells specialized storage safes that are vital for managing your wealth and high-value loot.

| Item | Level Requirement | Capacity | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Small Safe** | Vulture Level 3 | 48 Slots | Stores Money & Valuables |
| **Large Safe** | Vulture Level 4 | 108 Slots | Maximum Storage Efficiency |

## Strategies for Leveling Up Vendors

Leveling up your vendors is just as important as completing the **gray zone warfare quests** themselves. Higher reputation levels unlock better armor, suppressors, and high-magnification optics like the ACOG. While most vendors level up through direct task completion, Vulture requires a different approach.

To level Vulture, you must sell him specific military equipment and high-tier valuables. While he often pays less than other vendors (sometimes 50% of the standard rate), the XP gain is necessary to reach Level 4. Focus on farming high-tier areas like **Midnight Sapphire** to find military electronics and rare items that Vulture accepts.

### Efficient Quest Routing
1. **The Town Sweep:** Start at the Water Tower for *Meds Wanted*, move Southeast to the Lumberyard for *Cash Retrieval*, and finish at the Gas Station for *First Recon*.
2. **The Extraction Play:** After finishing the Gas Station objective, head further South to unlock the **Charlie 3 LZ**. This provides a faster exit and sets you up perfectly for the *Little Bird Down* quest.
3. **The Body Retrieval:** For quests like *No Man Left Behind* or *Lost and Found*, keep the quest items (like the necklace) in your secure lockbox. If you die before extracting, you won't have to return to the dangerous town center to loot them again.

## Advanced Quest Mechanics: Keys and Tools

As you progress through the **gray zone warfare quests**, you will encounter locked doors that require specific keys. These are not guaranteed rewards; instead, they must be looted from the bodies of fallen AI enemies within the mission zone.

| Key Name | Associated Quest | Location |
|---|---|---|
| **KV Dock Key** | Medical Detective | Medical Building (141 166) |
| **Construction Key** | Lost and Found | Construction Shop Office |
| **Warehouse Office Key** | Cash Retrieval | West Town Warehouse |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Keys are lost upon death if they are in your standard pockets or backpack. Always keep your quest-essential keys in your secure container to avoid being locked out of mission progress.

## Final Preparations for Success

Before embarking on any major **gray zone warfare quests**, ensure your kit is optimized for the distance. The early M4 builds benefit significantly from an ACOG 4x scope, which can be purchased from Gunny once you've completed a few initial tasks. This allows you to clear AI from a distance, reducing the risk of being overwhelmed in tight urban corridors like those found in the town center.

Always bring at least two bandages and a splint, as the AI in 2026 is remarkably accurate at hitting limbs. By following a disciplined questing path and focusing on vendor reputation, you will quickly transition from a struggling recruit to a well-equipped professional operator.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the trackers for Gunny's quests?**

A: Trackers are sent via the in-game messaging system. Open your "Messages" tab, click on Gunny, and "Accept" the items. They will then appear in your character's stash, from where you must move them to your inventory.

**Q: Does Vulture stay in the same place forever?**

A: No, Vulture moves between "Major Cops" (3-dot locations) on a weekly rotation. You must recapture the new location each week to regain access to his trade menu.

**Q: What happens if I lose a quest item like the necklace in "Lost and Found"?**

A: If you die while carrying a unique quest item in your backpack or pockets, it will despawn. You will need to re-enter the deployment zone and go back to the original location to pick it up again.

**Q: How do I complete gray zone warfare quests that require 10 kills?**

A: These "elimination" quests, like *Restoring Order*, simply require you to kill any AI enemies within the specified town limits. You do not need to loot them or interact with specific objects to progress the counter.
