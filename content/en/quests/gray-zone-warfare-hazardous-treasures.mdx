Navigating the treacherous jungles of Lamang Island requires more than just a steady aim; it demands logistical precision and endurance. One of the most challenging multi-stage missions players encounter is the **gray zone warfare hazardous treasures** task assigned by Handshake. This mission is a grueling test of a PMC's ability to clear high-threat areas and extract extremely heavy mission items across six different points of interest. 

Completing the **gray zone warfare hazardous treasures** quest involves traveling to locations like the Sawmill, Hunter's Paradise, and the YBL-1 bunker. At each stop, you must eliminate at least 10 hostiles before locating and extracting a heavy metal container. Because these containers weigh 30kg each and occupy significant backpack space, players must plan their routes carefully to avoid being caught out while over-encumbered. This guide provides the exact coordinates, key requirements, and tactical advice needed to survive this marathon task in 2026.

## Preparation and Mission Requirements

Before heading out, you must understand that this is not a single-deployment mission. Unless you are operating with a highly coordinated 4-man squad and multiple helicopters, you will likely need to tackle these locations one by one. The containers you are hunting are not only heavy but also bulky, ranging from 2x3 to 4x4 in slot size.

### Recommended Loadout
*   **Weaponry:** A long-range rifle with a high-magnification scope (e.g., 1-6x or 4x) is essential for clearing the Sawmill and Airfield from a distance.
*   **Ammunition:** Bring at least 4-5 spare magazines. You are required to kill 60 enemies total across all phases.
*   **Backpack:** A large backpack (like the Rush24 or better) is mandatory. Some containers will not fit in smaller tactical bags.
*   **Keys:** Several locations are locked. While you can sometimes find doors already opened by other players, having the specific keys in your safe container is much more reliable.

## Phase 1: The Sawmill and Blue Lagoon

The first leg of the **gray zone warfare hazardous treasures** task takes you to the Sawmill. This area is notorious for AI snipers and roaming patrols. Approach from the Echo 2 landing zone, as there is a prominent mound that overlooks the main work yard, allowing you to pick off the 10 required hostiles from relative safety.

Once the area is clear, head to the office building at **139, 144**. You will need the **MS OS TRG Key** to enter the specific room containing the metal container. 

> **Warning:** The Sawmill container is a 4x4 item weighing 30kg. Once you pick this up, your stamina regeneration will drop significantly. Ensure your path to the LZ is clear before grabbing it.

After turning in the Sawmill container at base, Handshake will send you to the Blue Lagoon. This area is more condensed. Clear the 10 hostiles around the restaurant and pier area. The container is located in the blue building's back office at **204, 117**. Unlike the Sawmill, this container is a slightly smaller 2x3, but it remains just as heavy.

## Phase 2: Hunter's Paradise and Ban Pa

The third location for **gray zone warfare hazardous treasures** is Hunter's Paradise. This POI is split into the firing range, the hotel, and the weapon storage area. Focus your 10 kills on the hostiles patrolling the firing lanes and the main entrance. 

To find the container, head to the weapon storage building at **159, 168**. You will need the **HP WEAP Key** to gain access. The container is tucked in the corner behind a stack of crates. It is a 3x3 item, so ensure your backpack is organized before attempting to pick it up.

Next is Ban Pa, the fishing village. This area is densely packed with foliage, making it easy for AI to ambush you. After securing your 10 kills, head to the Elder's House. You will need the **BP Elder Room Key**. The container sits directly in front of the desk in the back room.

## Phase 3: Pha Lang Airfield and YBL-1

As you reach the final stages of the **gray zone warfare hazardous treasures** mission, the difficulty spikes. The Pha Lang Airfield is wide open, leaving you vulnerable to fire from the hangars. 

1.  **Eliminate 10 Hostiles:** Use the perimeter fence for cover and pick off guards near the runway.
2.  **Locate the Remote Hangar:** Move to the hangar at **182, 156**.
3.  **Unlock Storage:** Use the **PLA Storage Key** to enter the small office within the hangar.
4.  **Extract:** Grab the 3x3 container and head to the nearest LZ.

The final objective is the YBL-1 bunker. This is arguably the most dangerous part of the quest due to the close-quarters nature of the underground tunnels. You do not need a key for this container, but you must fight your way down to the second level. Look for the conference room with wood-paneled walls; the container is located in the back corner of the adjoining office.

## Managing Over-Encumbrance

The defining characteristic of the **gray zone warfare hazardous treasures** task is the weight of the objectives. Every container weighs 30kg. In [Gray Zone Warfare](https://grayzonewarfare.com/), being over-encumbered drains your stamina rapidly while walking and prevents you from sprinting.

To manage this, consider dropping non-essential gear before picking up the container. If you are playing in a squad, have one person carry the container while the others act as scouts and point-men. If you are solo, you may need to "leapfrog" your gear—moving the container a short distance, dropping it, clearing the path ahead, and then returning for the container.

> **💡 Tip:** Always call for your extraction helicopter *before* you pick up the container if the LZ is close. This minimizes the time you spend standing still and vulnerable while waiting for transport.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the keys for the Hazardous Treasures task?**

A: Keys like the MS OS TRG and HP WEAP are random drops from hostiles in those specific areas. If you are struggling to find them, check the bodies of the 10 enemies you are required to kill for the mission, as they have a higher chance of carrying local keys.

**Q: Can I extract multiple containers in one trip?**

A: While theoretically possible with a large enough backpack and a squad, it is not recommended. The weight of two containers (60kg) plus your gear will likely make you unable to move or regenerate stamina, making you an easy target for AI and rival PMCs.

**Q: What happens if I die while carrying a Hazardous Treasures container?**

A: If you die, the container will remain on your body. You must return to your corpse to retrieve it. However, because these items are part of the **gray zone warfare hazardous treasures** quest, other players cannot turn them in for their own progress, though they can take them to grief you.

**Q: Do I have to kill the 10 hostiles in the same raid I find the container?**

A: Yes, the objectives are usually linked. You must satisfy the kill requirement at the specific POI before the container extraction will count toward your progress for Handshake. Always check your task log to ensure the "Eliminate Hostiles" objective is checked off before leaving the area.
