<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival First: Health Management and Injury Treatment">
In Gray Zone Warfare, taking the **first hit** can lead to a cascade of injuries that quickly incapacitate you. Effective health management is not just about carrying medkits; it's about understanding the various injury types and knowing how to treat them under pressure. Ignoring injuries like bleeding or fractures can lead to critical conditions such as coma or even death.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Injury">
    After taking damage, quickly assess the injury type. This could range from light bleeding to fractures or even organ damage. The HUD provides visual cues for your current status.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Stop Bleeding Immediately">
    Bleeding, even light, can quickly drain your blood volume. Use bandages or combat tourniquets to stop the blood loss. Prioritize this as low blood volume can lead to a coma.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Treat Fractures and Pain">
    Fractures impair movement and accuracy. Use splints to stabilize them. Pain can also affect your aiming and focus, so use painkillers or morphine to mitigate its effects.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Hydration and Energy">
    Low hydration and energy significantly impact your recovery and overall performance. Always carry water and food. Dehydration and starvation can cause hazy vision and prevent blood regeneration.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Utilize Secure Lockbox for Meds">
    Place essential medical supplies in your Secure Lockbox. This ensures you won't lose them if you die, allowing for a faster recovery and re-entry into the action.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success">
A well-stocked medical pouch can turn the tide of a losing fight. Always carry at least one combat tourniquet, a splint, painkillers, and an IFAK (Individual First Aid Kit) for comprehensive treatment.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Tactical Positioning and Movement">
Your positioning and movement are critical factors in gaining the advantage during your **gray zone warfare first hit**. Simply running and gunning will often lead to early death. Utilize cover, understand line of sight, and move strategically to control engagements. The map of Lamang Island offers diverse terrain, from dense jungle to urban areas, each requiring a different tactical approach.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Utilizing Cover">
    - **Hard Cover**: Concrete walls, large rocks, and thick trees provide complete ballistic protection. Always prioritize these.
    - **Soft Cover**: Bushes, thin wooden fences, or light foliage can obscure your silhouette but offer minimal ballistic protection. Use them for concealment, not defense.
    - **Angles**: Use angles to expose minimal body surface to enemies while maintaining a clear shot.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Strategic Movement">
    - **Slow & Deliberate**: Avoid sprinting unnecessarily. Slow movement reduces noise and makes you harder to spot.
    - **Flanking**: If possible, maneuver to the side or rear of an enemy to gain a positional advantage.
    - **Disengagement**: Know when to break contact. Sometimes, repositioning is better than fighting a losing battle.
    - **Helicopter Use**: While noisy, helicopters provide a safe insertion/extraction and a 4-second invulnerability window upon disembarking, useful for tactical repositioning.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="info">
The sounds you make are just as important as the shots you fire. Footsteps, reloads, and even breathing can alert AI and other players to your presence.
</Callout>
