## Understanding the Vendor System and Mission UI

Before heading into the field, you must understand how to interact with the various Private Military Company (PMC) vendors. Each vendor specializes in specific types of equipment, and completing their tasks is the only way to increase your reputation and unlock better items. 

To access your tasks, press the **Tab** key while at base camp and navigate to the "Vendors" tab at the top of the screen. From here, you can select individual vendors to view their available assignments.

| Vendor | Specialization | Why Level Them? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Handshake** | Armor, Rigs, and Basic Supplies | Essential for survival and body protection. |
| **Gunny** | Firearms, Ammunition, and Attachments | Unlocks high-powered optics like the ACOG. |
| **Lab Rat** | Medical Supplies and Consumables | Provides advanced healing items and surgery kits. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your "Messages" tab after accepting a task. Vendors often send quest-critical items, such as GPS trackers, via in-game mail that must be manually collected.

## The Essential Gray Zone Warfare Task Tracker: Managing Your Items

One of the most unique mechanics in the game is the physical requirement of a **gray zone warfare task tracker** item for specific marking missions. Unlike other shooters where "marking" is a simple button press, here you must have a physical tracker in your inventory to interact with the objective.

### How to Obtain and Protect Trackers
When you accept missions like "Cash Retrieval" or "Leave No Man Behind," Gunny will send you trackers through the messaging system. 
1. Open the **Messages** menu.
2. Click on the vendor (e.g., Gunny).
3. Collect the item shipment.
4. Move the trackers from your Stash to your **Safe Lock Box**.

By placing the tracker in your lock box, you ensure that you do not lose the item if you are KIA (Killed In Action). If you do happen to lose your trackers, you can purchase replacements from Gunny's trade menu for $50 each. Simply scroll to the bottom of his inventory to find them.

## Phase 1: The Initial Town Incursion

The first set of tasks usually sends you to the town nearest your faction's starting base. To make the most of your deployment, you should accept "First Recon," "Cash Retrieval," and "Meds Wanted" simultaneously. This allows you to hit multiple objectives in a single "batch" run.

| Task Name | Vendor | Objective | Location Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First Recon** | Handshake | Visit 3 Locations | Gas Station, Convenience Store, Demolished Building. |
| **Cash Retrieval**| Gunny | Mark 2 Caches | Warehouse (West) and Lumber Yard (East). |
| **Meds Wanted** | Lab Rat | Retrieve Med Box | Medical site directly under the Water Tower. |

### Meds Wanted Walkthrough
Head toward the giant Water Tower, which is visible from almost anywhere near the town. Look for the white tents with blue logos. The medical supplies are located in the building in the Northwest corner of this site. Open the door on the right and grab the white box from the shelf. 

### Cash Retrieval Walkthrough
For this mission, you must use your trackers. 
*   **Lumber Yard:** Locate the small shed on the east side. Interact with the red crate inside to place the tracker.
*   **Warehouse:** Circle the large warehouse to find a small exterior room. Inside, you will find a second red crate for your second tracker.

## Phase 2: Expanding the Map and Search Operations

Once you complete the initial set, return to base via the Landing Zone (LZ). Successfully extracting from the southern part of the town often unlocks the **Charlie 3 LZ**, which is vital for the next phase of your **gray zone warfare task tracker** journey.

### Little Bird Down
This task from Handshake requires you to find a crashed helicopter. If you have unlocked Charlie 3, the crash site is located just north of the LZ in the treeline. It appears as a burnt-out husk. After "discovering" the chopper, head to the nearby house to the north to find the bodies of the crew, which completes the objective.

### Lost and Found
Lab Rat will task you with finding a missing clerk. Head to the construction shop located just north of the Lumber Yard. Inside the main building, you will find a body on the floor. Look closely between the hands of the corpse to find a necklace. Pick this up and immediately put it in your **Safe Lock Box**.

### Leave No Man Behind
Gunny requires you to mark a specific body located in the backyards near the Town Hall (East side of town). This area is heavily patrolled by AI, so approach with caution. Once you find the body in the alleyway, place your tracker to finish the mission.

## Combat and Key-Based Progression

As you progress through the [official Gray Zone Warfare](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2479810/Gray_Zone_Warfare/) task list, missions transition from simple discovery to combat-focused objectives.

### Restoring Order
This is a straightforward elimination task from Handshake. You must neutralize 10 enemies within the town limits. While doing this, be sure to loot every body you drop. AI enemies in this game have a chance to carry specific keys required for later tasks.

### Medical Detective
This Gunny task is often a "bottleneck" for players. It requires you to find missing supplies in a medical building at grid **141 166**. However, the door is locked and requires the **KV Dock Key**. 
*   **How to find the key:** There is no fixed spawn. You must farm AI enemies in the town until one drops the key.
*   **Strategy:** Combine this with "Restoring Order" to kill two birds with one stone.

> **Warning:** Do not discard any keys you find, even if you don't recognize the name. Store them in your locker at base camp; many will be required for high-level tasks later in the 2026 campaign.

## Tactical Tips for Task Completion

To ensure your **gray zone warfare task tracker** stays updated without constant setbacks, follow these tactical guidelines:

1.  **Optics are King:** Before heading into town, buy an ACOG mount and a 4x scope from Gunny. The AI often engages from long distances, and the standard iron sights or red dots are insufficient for clean headshots.
2.  **Batch Your Tasks:** Never head out for just one objective. Always check if you have overlapping missions in the same area to minimize the number of helicopter rides you need to take.
3.  **The "Lock Box" Rule:** Any item with a "Quest" tag (like the clerk's necklace or medical folders) should be placed in your secure container immediately.
4.  **LZ Safety:** When calling in a transport helicopter, stay hidden until the bird is on the ground. LZs are high-traffic areas and common spots for PVP ambushes.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I get more trackers if I lose mine?**

A: You can purchase additional trackers from Gunny for $50. They are located at the very bottom of his "Trade" inventory. Always try to keep at least two in your stash for backup.

**Q: Why isn't my gray zone warfare task tracker updating when I reach a location?**

A: Some locations require you to stand in a very specific spot, often the center of a room or right next to a specific prop (like the yellow digger at the demolished building). If it doesn't tick off, try walking around the perimeter of the objective.

**Q: Can I complete tasks in a squad?**

A: Yes, but certain "item retrieval" tasks (like the necklace in Lost and Found) may only spawn one item per instance or require each player to pick it up. Marking tasks with trackers can usually be done by one person to progress the mission for the whole squad if they are on the same step.

**Q: What happens if I die with a quest item?**

A: If the item is in your standard inventory (backpack, rig, pockets), it will stay on your corpse. If you cannot retrieve your body, the item is lost, and you must restart the quest by visiting the spawn location again. Items in your Safe Lock Box are never lost.
