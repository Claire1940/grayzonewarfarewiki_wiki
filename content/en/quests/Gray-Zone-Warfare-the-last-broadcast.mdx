Mastering early-to-mid game progression on Lamang Island requires completing key faction missions, and the **Gray Zone Warfare the last broadcast** task is one of the most critical routing bottlenecks you will encounter. Issued by Handshake, this mission tasks operators with locating a local radio transmitter that has been broadcasting militia propaganda, retrieving the recorded data, and extracting safely.

Before attempting this mission, you must complete the initial starter town tasks, including First Recon and Meds Wanted. Handshake requires you to travel to a high-altitude radio tower located just outside your faction's starting town. Because the radio tower sits on high ground, approaching the area makes you highly visible to local rebel forces who patrol the hillsides.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Step-by-Step Walkthrough for Gray Zone Warfare the last broadcast">

To complete this mission efficiently, follow these steps to secure the area, locate the target intel, and extract back to base.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Extraction Point and Flight Path">
    Deploy from the FOB and request a helicopter to the closest landing zone (LZ) near your starter town. Ensure you have a clear route to the hill. Keep your weapon raised during the 4-second invulnerability window immediately after stepping off the chopper.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear the Hillside Perimeter">
    Slowly ascend the hill leading to the radio mast. Do not sprint, as doing so drains your stamina and increases weapon sway. Spot and eliminate the 3 to 5 patrolling militia soldiers guarding the outer fence. Use silenced weapons to avoid alerting the entire compound.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locate the Main Control Room">
    Identify the main concrete building at the base of the metal radio tower. Check the entrance for any remaining hostile AI. The building has an open door, so no keys are required for this step.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Retrieve the Broadcast Intel">
    Enter the building and look for a desk with radio transmitter equipment. Locate the objective item, which appears as a small cassette tape or folder labeled as the broadcast log. Interact with the item to pick it up, and immediately move it to your Secure Lockbox to protect it in case you die before extraction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Extract and Turn in the Quest">
    Move down the hill using a different path to avoid respawned AI. Call in a transport helicopter at your nearest LZ. Once back at the FOB, open your vendor menu, select Handshake, and turn in the task to claim your rewards.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Secure Lockbox Rule">
Always place task items like documents, keys, and recording tapes directly into your Secure Lockbox. If you get eliminated on the way to the extraction point, the item remains safe in your secure inventory, allowing you to hand it in to Handshake without running the mission again.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Preparation and Recommended Loadout">

Update 0.4 Spearhead introduces significant changes to weapon handling, ergonomics, and ballistics. Weapon weight now directly affects stamina drainage, sway, and aim down sight (ADS) speed. When climbing the steep hills to complete the **Gray Zone Warfare the last broadcast** task, running a heavy, unoptimized loadout will leave you fatigued and vulnerable. 

Because the AI at the radio towers are lightly armored, you do not need expensive high-penetration armor-piercing rounds. Instead, prioritize weapon ergonomics and high-velocity ammunition.

| Gear Category | Selection | Tactical Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Weapon** | AK-74M or M4A1 | Versatile rifle platforms with controllable recoil |
| **Ammunition** | 5.45x39mm PP / 5.56x45mm M855 | Excellent flesh damage with enough penetration for basic vest protection |
| **Armor Class** | Class 2+ (Gen II Carrier) | Lightweight protection that preserves stamina during hill climbs |
| **Medical Supplies** | IFAK, Tourniquet, Splints | Essential for treating bleeding and leg fractures caused by SKS rounds |
| **Hydration/Energy** | 1x Water Bottle, 1x MRE | Prevents dehydration haze and ensures active blood regeneration |

<Callout type="tip" title="Sustain Management">
Stamina regeneration stops entirely if your hydration drops below 50 percent. Because the ascent to the radio tower requires significant climbing, drink a full bottle of water at the LZ before starting your uphill approach.
</Callout>
