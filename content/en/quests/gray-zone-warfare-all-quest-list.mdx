## Getting Started with Gray Zone Warfare Tasks

Before heading into the field, players must accept tasks from the Vendor tab in the base camp menu. Each vendor specializes in different types of support: Handshake focuses on tactical reconnaissance, Gunny manages firearms and logistics, and Lab Rat handles medical supplies and recovery.

When you first spawn, you should accept all available tasks simultaneously. This allows you to "stack" objectives, completing multiple requirements in a single deployment to the nearby town.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Always place quest-critical items, such as Trackers or recovered jewelry, into your **Lockbox**. This ensures that even if you are KIA during extraction, you won't lose the item and have to restart the task.

## Handshake Task List: Tactical Foundation

Handshake’s early missions are designed to familiarize you with the local town's landmarks. These are "mirrored" tasks, meaning the specific town name changes based on your faction, but the building types and layouts remain consistent.

### First Recon
This task requires you to scout three specific locations. You do not need to retrieve items; simply walking into the proximity of the building will trigger the objective completion.
*   **Gas Station:** Located on the outskirts of town. Expect 2-3 AI guards.
*   **Convenience Store:** A large central building near the lumber yard.
*   **Demolished Building:** Found on the northern edge, identifiable by a large yellow excavator.

### Little Bird Down
After completing the initial recon, Handshake will task you with locating a downed helicopter. This mission is best approached by flying into the **Charlie 3 LZ**, which puts you within walking distance of the crash site.
1.  Locate the charred remains of the helicopter in the treeline.
2.  Search the nearby house to the north to find the remains of the crew.
3.  Clear the area of AI before attempting to "inspect" the bodies.

## Gunny and Lab Rat: Logistics and Recovery

While Handshake wants intel, Gunny and Lab Rat require physical interaction with the environment. These tasks often involve using **Trackers**, which must be retrieved from your in-game messages after accepting the task.

### Gunny's Cash Retrieval
You must mark two specific caches using trackers. If you lose your trackers, you must return to base to retrieve new ones from your stash.
*   **Lumber Yard Cache:** Located in a small shed on the east side of the lumber yard. Interact with the red crate inside.
*   **Warehouse Cache:** Located in a warehouse north of the water tower. Look for a small side room with an external door.

### Lab Rat's Meds Wanted
This task sends you to the medical tents located directly beneath the town's giant water tower. 
1.  Identify the white tents with blue medical logos.
2.  Enter the building in the Northwest corner of the medical site.
3.  Retrieve the **White Medical Box** from the back shelves.
4.  **Warning:** If you die while carrying this box (and it isn't in a lockbox), it will despawn.

## Advanced Progression: The Secret Vulture Vendor

As you progress through the **gray zone warfare all quest list**, you will eventually encounter a unique vendor known as **Vulture**. Unlike the static vendors at your base, Vulture is a "secret" merchant who appears at different major Command Posts (COPs) on a weekly rotation.

Vulture does not require a specific quest to unlock, but his inventory is dynamic. He scavenges gear from PMCs who died and failed to recover their loot. This makes him an excellent source for high-tier attachments, such as 20-inch M4 barrels or 60-round magazines, that you might not have unlocked through traditional leveling yet.

### Leveling Vulture
To increase your reputation with Vulture, you cannot sell him standard weapons or armor. He only accepts **High-Value Loot** (watches, jewelry, rare electronics). 
*   **Trade-off:** Vulture pays significantly less for these items than Turncoat does. 
*   **Benefit:** Leveling him to Rank 2 unlocks the **MP7 A2**, and reaching Rank 4 grants access to the powerful **AK-308**.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Vulture's stock is server-dependent. If another player on your server buys a rare item from him, it may be out of stock until the next refresh.

## Essential Tips for Completing the Gray Zone Warfare All Quest List

1.  **Optics Matter:** Before heading into town for the first time, buy an **ACOG mount and a 4x scope** from Gunny. The AI in Gray Zone Warfare can engage from significant distances, and iron sights will put you at a severe disadvantage.
2.  **LZ Management:** Don't just use the closest Landing Zone. Sometimes landing further away and flanking into a town provides better cover and prevents "LZ camping" from rival factions.
3.  **Key Farming:** Tasks like "Medical Detective" require specific keys (e.g., the KV Dock Key). These are random drops from AI enemies in that specific region. If you can't find a key, try grouping with other players in your faction who might already have the door unlocked.
4.  **The Message System:** Many players forget that quest items like trackers and rewards are sent via the **in-game messaging system**. Check your messages frequently to ensure you have the tools required for your next deployment.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the gray zone warfare all quest list in-game?**

A: You can view your active and completed tasks by pressing **Tab** and navigating to the **Vendors** tab. Click on an individual vendor and select "Tasks" at the top left to see your current progress.

**Q: What happens if I lose a quest item like the Clerk's Necklace?**

A: If the item was not in your lockbox and you died, it will despawn from your inventory. You must redeploy to the mission location (in this case, the Construction Shop) and pick the item up again.

**Q: How do I find the Vulture vendor this week?**

A: Vulture moves between two major COPs every Monday. Because his location is the same for all players regardless of faction or server type (PvE/PvP), checking community hubs or Discord servers is the fastest way to find his current coordinates.

**Q: Are the quests different for each faction?**

A: The objectives and mechanics are identical, but the locations are mirrored. For example, if a Mithras player goes to the town of Nam Thaven, a Crimson Shield player will perform the same tasks in their respective starting town. The **gray zone warfare all quest list** remains functionally the same for everyone.
