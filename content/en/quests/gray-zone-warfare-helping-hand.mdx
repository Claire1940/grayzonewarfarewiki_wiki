Completing the **gray zone warfare helping hand** quest is a vital milestone for players looking to build a solid reputation with Handshake. As one of the early-to-mid-game tasks, it requires a mix of tactical navigation, key hunting, and successful extraction. In the unforgiving environment of Lamang, knowing exactly where to go and what tools you need can save you from unnecessary gear loss. This **gray zone warfare helping hand** tutorial provides a comprehensive breakdown of the objectives, regardless of which faction you have joined. Whether you are navigating the dense streets of Nam Thaven or the outskirts of Pha Lang, this guide ensures you find the intelligence folder and return it to base safely.

## Quest Overview and Objectives

The "Helping Hand" mission is assigned by Handshake, the primary vendor for weapons and tactical gear. The narrative involves recovering sensitive intelligence left behind in a specific building within your faction's starting town. Unlike some of the earlier "kill and clear" missions, this task is an extraction-based objective. You must enter a hostile area, locate a locked room, retrieve a physical item (an intelligence folder), and successfully extract it to your Forward Operating Base (FOB).

| Objective | Requirement | Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Locate Intelligence** | Find the specific folder | Faction Starter Town |
| **Retrieve Folder** | Physical item in inventory | Locked Upper Floor |
| **Safe Extraction** | Survive to the LZ | Nearest Landing Zone |
| **Hand over Intel** | Return to Handshake | FOB Vendor Menu |

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you die while carrying the intelligence folder, it will be lost. You will need to return to the building to retrieve it again, though you will not need to find a new key if you still have yours in your safe container.

## Necessary Keys for Helping Hand

The most significant hurdle for the **gray zone warfare helping hand** quest is acquiring the correct key. Depending on your faction and the specific building layout in your town, you will typically need either the **Attic Key** or the **Restaurant Storage Key**. These keys are not sold by vendors; they must be looted from the environment.

### How to Find the Keys
The most reliable way to find these keys is by eliminating AI combatants within your starter town. Every AI enemy has a random chance to drop a key relevant to the local area. 

1.  **Farm AI Hubs:** Focus on areas with high enemy density like marketplaces or construction sites.
2.  **Check Every Corpse:** Even if you didn't get the kill, check bodies left behind by other players.
3.  **Check Jackets and Containers:** Residential buildings often have lootable clothing that may contain keys.
4.  **Ask Faction Mates:** Since keys are physical items, a friendly player in your faction might be willing to drop one for you or open the door if they are on the same mission.

| Key Name | Primary Town | Common Drop Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attic Key** | Nam Thaven / Kiu Vongsa | Local AI Soldiers |
| **Restaurant Storage Key** | Pha Lang / Kiu Vongsa | Local AI Soldiers |

## Faction-Specific Locations

While the objective remains the same, the geographical location of the **gray zone warfare helping hand** mission changes based on your faction. Each faction (Mithras Security Group, Lamang Recovery Initiative, and Crimson Shield International) operates out of a different starter town.

### Mithras Security Group (Nam Thaven)
For Mithras players, the objective is located in Nam Thaven. You are looking for a large, multi-story house often characterized by a T-shape on the map. It is usually situated near the western edge of the town, past the construction site. Use Landing Zone **Bravo 1** for the quickest approach.

### Crimson Shield International (Kiu Vongsa)
CSI players will head to Kiu Vongsa. The target building is a prominent restaurant-style structure. It is often identified by its blue fencing and proximity to the town center. Landing Zone **Delta 1** or **Delta 2** provides the best access points while avoiding the most heavily patrolled main roads.

### Lamang Recovery Initiative (Pha Lang)
LRI members must navigate Pha Lang. The intelligence is located in a residential attic or a storage room above a local business. Focus on the northern sector of the town. Landing Zone **Lima 1** is the standard insertion point for this operation.

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough

Follow these steps to ensure a successful run for the **gray zone warfare helping hand** task:

### Step 1: Gear Preparation
Before heading out, ensure you have the required key in your **Safe Folder** (Secure Container). This prevents you from losing the key if you are eliminated. Bring a suppressed weapon if possible, as the towns are crawling with AI that will swarm your position if they hear unsuppressed gunfire.

### Step 2: Infiltration
Land at your designated LZ and move toward the town cautiously. Avoid the main roads where AI snipers often have clear lines of sight. Move through backyards and alleyways until you reach the target building.

### Step 3: Entering the Building
Once you arrive at the house or restaurant, clear the ground floor first. AI often hide in corners or behind counters. Locate the staircase leading to the upper level. 
*   **For the Attic:** The staircase is often located at the back of the house or even an external set of stairs.
*   **For the Restaurant:** Look for a door behind the kitchen area that leads upward.

### Step 4: Using the Key and Collecting Intel
Approach the locked door. If the door is already open (from a previous player), you can walk right in. If not, interact with the door to use your key. Inside the room, look for a wooden chair or a desk. The "Intelligence Folder" will be sitting there. Interact with it to place it in your inventory.

> **💡 Tip:** The Intelligence Folder takes up a 1x2 or 2x2 slot depending on the current game patch. Ensure you have enough room in your backpack or rig before entering the room.

### Step 5: Extraction
This is the most dangerous part of the **gray zone warfare helping hand** quest. With the intel in hand, backtrack to your nearest LZ. Do not sprint blindly; players often camp LZs or wait for runners. Once the helicopter arrives, board it immediately to return to the FOB.

## Tactical Advice for Solo Players

Completing the **gray zone warfare helping hand** mission as a solo player requires a different approach than squad play. Without teammates to watch your back, stealth and patience are your best friends.

*   **Patience is Key:** If you hear heavy gunfire at the target building, wait. Let other players clear the AI for you. You might even find the door already unlocked, saving you the trouble of finding a key.
*   **Night Operations:** If you have access to Night Vision Goggles (NVGs), performing this mission at night can significantly reduce the AI's detection range, allowing you to slip in and out of the building with minimal resistance.
*   **Suppressors:** Always use a suppressor. The sound of a single unsuppressed shot can draw AI from three blocks away, making your extraction nearly impossible.
*   **Check the Map:** Use high-quality community maps like those found on [Official Gray Zone Warfare community hubs](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2477940/Gray_Zone_Warfare/) to identify the exact "T-shape" or "Blue Fence" buildings before you land.

| Strategy | Benefit | Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stealth** | Avoids large firefights | Slower progression |
| **Aggressive Clear** | Secures the area quickly | Draws more AI/Players |
| **Co-op** | Shared security | Shared loot/Noise |

## Rewards for Completion

After returning to the FOB, open your menu and navigate to Handshake. Hand over the intelligence folder to receive your rewards. These rewards are essential for early-game progression, providing you with better gear and more currency.

1.  **Experience Points (XP):** Boosts your overall character level.
2.  **Vendor Reputation:** Increases your standing with Handshake, unlocking higher-tier weapons and attachments.
3.  **Currency:** A significant amount of USD to fund future raids.
4.  **Gear Pieces:** Often includes armor or specialized ammunition.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I complete the gray zone warfare helping hand quest without the key?**

A: Yes, but only if another player has already unlocked the door during your current session. If you arrive and the door is closed/locked, you must have the specific Attic or Restaurant Storage key to proceed.

**Q: What happens if I lose the Intelligence Folder?**

A: If you are killed before extracting, the folder will drop with your body. You must go back to the town and pick it up again. If your body is looted or despawns, the folder will respawn in the original locked room.

**Q: Does the key break after one use?**

A: No, keys in Gray Zone Warfare currently have multiple uses or are permanent (depending on the specific key type), allowing you to help your friends or faction members complete the mission after you are done.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm the Attic Key?**

A: The best place to farm any town-specific key for the **gray zone warfare helping hand** mission is the area with the highest concentration of AI, such as the local marketplace or the main entrance to the town. AI "Leaders" or those with better equipment often have a slightly higher chance of carrying keys.
