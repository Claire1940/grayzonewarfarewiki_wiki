## Essential Mechanics for Blueprint Collection

Before heading out to the coordinates listed below, you must understand two critical mechanics that often trip up new contractors. First, the interaction system for blueprints is unique. When you find a blueprint, you cannot simply click it once. You must **double-tap the interaction button**. The first interaction usually triggers an "inspect magazine" animation or a general check; the second interaction actually places the item into your inventory.

Second, blueprints are treated as quest items with high stakes. If you die while carrying a blueprint before turning it in to a trader, the quest typically fails. In some 2026 builds, players have reported bugs where lost blueprints do not respawn immediately, potentially locking out weapon unlocks for extended periods. Always prioritize a direct extraction once a blueprint is in your secure container or backpack.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never attempt to "clear one more building" after picking up a blueprint. The risk of losing the item to a stray AI bullet or a rival player is too high. Head to the nearest LZ immediately.

### High-Tier Assault Rifles: MCX and DDM4

The **MCX** is a premier choice for players who prefer suppressed, close-quarters builds. Chambered in .300 Blackout, it offers incredible stopping power without the noise signature of standard 5.56 rifles. To find it, infiltrate the Midnight Sapphire Hotel. Navigate to the ground floor and look toward the back right-hand side of the main building. 

The **DDM4**, conversely, is the gold standard for mid-range versatility. This modern AR platform is located deep within the hostile territory of Fort Narith. You must enter the middle barrack at coordinates 141 131, head up the stairs, and follow the hallway to the very end. The blueprint sits plainly on a table, but expect heavy resistance from elite AI guards patrolling the barracks.

### Long-Range Dominance: M700 and SVD

For marksmen, the **M700** and **SVD** are essential unlocks. The M700 is a bolt-action rifle firing 7.62x51 NATO, capable of dropping targets at extreme distances. It is conveniently located at the same POI as the MCX (Midnight Sapphire), but you must climb the main stairs to the rooftop. Traverse to the far right end of the roof to secure the schematic.

The **SVD** offers a semi-automatic alternative for rapid follow-up shots. It is located in the high-danger zone of Tiger Bay. You must fight your way into the Tiger Bay Mall and locate the bookstore. Behind the register, there is a door leading to a back storage room; the blueprint is located on a shelf to the right.

## The Secret Vendor: Vulture

While most **gray zone weapon blueprints** are found in the world, some specialized gear is tied to the secret vendor known as **Vulture**. Unlike standard traders who remain at your base camp, Vulture is an "in-person" merchant who rotates his location weekly.

Vulture typically appears at specific combat outposts, such as **Winchester** or **Titan**. His location resets every Monday at 8:00 AM EST. To access his inventory, you must physically travel to the outpost where he is currently stationed. Once inside the perimeter, his shop will unlock in your vendor tab.

## Tactical Tips for Blueprint Hunting

1. **Go in Light, Leave Heavy**: When hunting blueprints in Fort Narith or Tiger Bay, bring only what you need to survive the initial breach. Leave space in your secure container for the blueprint.
2. **The "Coffee Pot" Secret**: The AK308 blueprint in Fort Narith is notoriously hard to spot. It is tucked directly underneath a coffee pot in the back room of the jail. You must kick the door open to enter.
3. **Pillow Talk**: The AK&M blueprint at 195 153 is hidden inside a small shack. Look specifically next to the pillow on the floor; it is easily missed if you are just scanning the tables.
4. **Use PvE for Scouting**: If you are struggling to reach a blueprint location due to player interference, consider switching to a [PvE-only server](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2479810/Gray_Zone_Warfare/) to learn the AI patrol patterns and room layouts without the threat of snipers.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I pick up the blueprint even though I'm looking right at it?**

A: You must double-tap the interaction key. The first press often triggers an inspection of the weapon's magazine or a general "check" of the item. Press it again immediately to add it to your inventory.

**Q: Do gray zone weapon blueprints disappear if I die?**

A: Yes. If the blueprint is in your backpack or rig and you die before extracting, you will lose it. If you have space, always place the blueprint in your Secure Container to ensure it stays with you even if you are KIA.

**Q: How often does the secret vendor Vulture move?**

A: Vulture rotates his location every Monday at 8:00 AM EST. He typically alternates between two major combat outposts. You must physically be at his current location to trade with him.

**Q: Can I find all blueprints in a single raid?**

A: Theoretically, yes, but it is highly discouraged. The locations are spread across the entire map, and the risk of losing multiple blueprints to a single death is too high. It is much safer to target one or two blueprints per deployment.
