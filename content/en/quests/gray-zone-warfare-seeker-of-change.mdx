As players progress through the tactical landscape of Lamang Island, the **gray zone warfare seeker of change** task stands out as one of the more atmospheric and hazardous missions provided by Lab Rat. This task requires operators to infiltrate the heavily fortified and eerie corridors of Fort Nar to retrieve biological samples from an infected individual. Unlike standard fetch quests, this mission demands precise timing and environmental awareness, as the objective is located within a radiation-heavy zone that can quickly compromise an unprepared squad.

Completing the **gray zone warfare seeker of change** mission is essential for players looking to boost their reputation with Lab Rat and unlock high-tier medical supplies. Because the task must be performed under the cover of night, it introduces a layer of tactical complexity that requires night vision goggles (NVGs) and a solid understanding of Fort Nar’s layout. In this guide, we will break down the preparation, the infiltration route, and the extraction process to ensure you survive the radioactive depths of the infirmary.

## Task Overview and Requirements

Before heading out to the field, you must ensure you have accepted the task from Lab Rat at your base of operations. She will provide you with a specific item required for the mission: the Sample Collection Kit. This is a quest-critical item; if you lose it or forget to bring it, you will be unable to interact with the objective.

| Task Attribute | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Vendor** | Lab Rat |
| **Location** | Fort Nar (Infirmary/ICU) |
| **Time Constraint** | Must be performed between **2200 and 0500** |
| **Primary Objective** | Collect a biological sample from an infected body |
| **Required Item** | Sample Collection Kit (Provided by Lab Rat) |
| **Hazard Level** | High (Radiation and Elite AI) |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Lab Rat only provides one Sample Collection Kit at the start. If you die and lose the kit, you may need to check your messenger or repurchase it from her inventory if available. Always double-check your secure container before deploying.

## Preparation and Gear Selection

In 2026, the meta for Fort Nar involves high-penetration ammunition and robust radiation protection. Since the **gray zone warfare seeker of change** task occurs at night, your gear loadout is non-negotiable. You will be facing elite AI guards who often carry suppressed weapons and high-tier armor.

### Essential Loadout Table

| Category | Recommended Item | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vision** | Gen 3 Night Vision Goggles | Necessary for the 2200-0500 time window. |
| **Medical** | Potassium Iodide (KI) Pills | Essential for mitigating radiation damage in the infirmary. |
| **Weapon** | Suppressed 5.56x45 or 7.62x39 | Stealth is key to avoiding unnecessary reinforcements. |
| **Armor** | Class 4+ Plate Carrier | Fort Nar AI are accurate and use armor-piercing rounds. |

The radiation in the infirmary is not a mere suggestion—it is a lethal environmental hazard. While the exposure is relatively low compared to the Ground Zero zones, it will cause your character to cough, breathe heavily, and lose health over time. Using anti-radiation medication like KI pills (often referred to by players as "the key") will give you the window needed to secure the sample without collapsing.

## Infiltration Route: Reaching Fort Nar

The most efficient way to reach the objective for the **gray zone warfare seeker of change** task is via **LZ Delta 3**. This landing zone places you within striking distance of the southern entrance of Fort Nar. 

1.  **LZ Delta 3 Arrival:** Once you touch down, move north toward the training course.
2.  **The Perimeter Fence:** Look for the training course fence. You can hop over this or find a breach to bypass the main gates, which are usually heavily guarded by AI.
3.  **Approaching the Infirmary:** The infirmary is a large, distinct building within the Fort Nar complex. Look for the medical symbols (red crosses or caduceus signs) near the entrances.
4.  **Entry Points:** Avoid the main lobby if possible. Use side entrances or windows to enter the building to minimize your profile.

## Navigating the Infirmary and Finding the Sample

Once inside the infirmary, the atmosphere shifts. You will notice radiation warning signs on several doors. Your objective is located on the **second floor** within the ICU (Intensive Care Unit) area.

### Step-by-Step Collection Guide

*   **Ascend to the Second Floor:** Locate the central stairwell or the elevator shafts (which are non-functional but climbable in some builds). 
*   **Locate the ICU:** Move toward the back corner rooms of the second floor. You are looking for a room that appears heavily contaminated, often marked with biohazard or radiation symbols.
*   **Identify the Body:** Inside one of the corner rooms (often the one furthest back in the ICU wing), you will find a body on a gurney or the floor. This individual is the source of the "change" Lab Rat is investigating.
*   **Extract the Sample:** With the Sample Collection Kit in your inventory, interact with the body. A progress bar will appear. Once completed, the sample will be placed in your inventory.

> **💡 Tip:** If you begin coughing or your screen starts to blur, your radiation levels are peaking. Take your KI pills immediately and finish the interaction. Do not linger in this room once the sample is secured.

## Extraction and Completion

After securing the sample for the **gray zone warfare seeker of change** task, your priority is a safe exit. Do not get greedy for loot in Fort Nar; the weight of extra gear will slow you down, making you an easy target for AI or rival players camping the LZs.

1.  **Retrace your steps:** Exit the infirmary through the same side path you entered.
2.  **Check for Campers:** LZ Delta 3 is a high-traffic area. Use your NVGs to scan the treeline before calling in your Little Bird.
3.  **Return to Base:** Once back at the FOB, open your menu, navigate to the Vendors tab, select Lab Rat, and hand over the sample.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I complete the gray zone warfare seeker of change task during the daytime?**

A: No. The task specifically requires the sample to be collected between 2200 and 0500 hours in-game time. If you attempt to interact with the body outside of this window, the prompt may not appear, or the sample will be "invalid."

**Q: What happens if I forget the Sample Collection Kit?**

A: You will be able to find the body, but you will not have the "Interact" prompt to collect the sample. You must return to base and ensure the kit is in your backpack or secure container before trying again.

**Q: Is the radiation in Fort Nar permanent?**

A: No, the radiation is localized to the infirmary and specific "hot zones" within Fort Nar. Once you leave the building and move toward the LZ, your radiation sickness will slowly dissipate, though using KI pills or other medical supplies will speed up the recovery process.

**Q: Are there multiple bodies to choose from?**

A: While there are many bodies in the infirmary, only one is the correct quest objective for the **gray zone warfare seeker of change** task. Look for the body in the back corner of the ICU on the second floor; it is usually the only one that provides a UI interaction prompt.
