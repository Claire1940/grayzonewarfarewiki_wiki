## Quest Overview and Objectives

The Fire and Water quest is centered around the Water Treatment Plant, a location teeming with hostile AI scavengers. Your primary goal is to verify the status of the facility and retrieve specific data or items that prove the facility's viability. In the 0.4 update of Gray Zone Warfare, environmental storytelling has become even more prominent, making the "search and report" mechanics more immersive but also more dangerous.

| Objective | Location | Task Type |
|---|---|---|
| **Locate Water Treatment Plant** | Pha Lang / Nam Thaven / Kiu Vongsa | Exploration |
| **Inspect the Main Pump** | Facility Interior | Interaction |
| **Retrieve Maintenance Log** | Control Room | Looting |
| **Survive and Extract** | Nearest Landing Zone (LZ) | Survival |

> **💡 Tip:** Before heading out, ensure you have accepted the task in your "Tasks" menu. If you enter the area without the quest active, your progress will not count toward completion.

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough: Completing Fire and Water

### 1. Reaching the Landing Zone
Depending on your faction (Mithras, Crimson, or Lamang), your starting town will differ, but the layout of the **gray zone warfare fire and water quest** remains functionally identical. Call in a transport helicopter to the LZ closest to the industrial sector of your town.

### 2. Clearing the Perimeter
The Water Treatment Plant is usually fenced in. AI patrols the exterior and often sits in elevated positions or behind sandbags. Use the "lean" mechanic (Q and E keys) to peek corners before committing to a push. If you are using the shotgun strategy, wait for the AI to move toward you or close the distance using bushes as concealment.

### 3. Locating the Maintenance Log
Once inside the main facility, navigate to the control room. This is typically a small office elevated above the main pump floor. Look for a physical folder or a laptop that prompts an "Interact" (F) action. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** The sound of your footsteps on metal grates inside the facility is very loud. AI will converge on your position if you run. Move at a crouch or "slow walk" pace to maintain the element of surprise.

### 4. Capturing the COP (Optional but Recommended)
In the 0.4 update, capturing a nearby COP (Combat Outpost) like **Atlas** or **Harrison** can provide a vital fallback point. If the **gray zone warfare fire and water quest** takes longer than expected, having a captured COP allows you to resupply at vendors or even interact with the secret vendor, **Vulture**, if he is on rotation at that location.

## Dealing with Injuries and Survival
The Water Treatment Plant is a "hot" zone. You are likely to take fire. Understanding the 2026 health system is paramount. If you see "Double Drips" on your health UI, you have a heavy bleed. You must use a **CT (Tourniquet)** immediately.

*   **Light Bruises:** Will heal over 2-3 minutes without intervention.
*   **Organ Damage:** Requires a **Surgery Kit (Surkit)** to repair yellow or red status on lungs or stomach.
*   **Dizziness:** This usually indicates low blood volume. Use a **Blood Bag** once you are in a safe spot.

## Advanced Tactics: The Vulture Connection
While performing the **gray zone warfare fire and water quest**, keep an eye out for high-value military equipment. Items like the 60k military trophy found near bosses can be sold to the secret vendor **Vulture**. Even though he buys items at a lower price than other vendors, selling to him is the only way to level his reputation. Maxing out Vulture allows you to purchase **Large Safes**, which provide 108 slots of secure storage for your money and rarest loot—a literal game-changer for 2026 players.

## FAQ

**Q: Where exactly is the Maintenance Log for the Fire and Water quest?**

A: The log is located in the primary office of the Water Treatment Plant. Look for a desk with a blue folder. If it's not there, another player might have grabbed it recently; wait a few minutes for the item to respawn or check if it's already in your quest inventory.

**Q: Can I complete the gray zone warfare fire and water quest in a squad?**

A: Yes, playing in a squad is highly recommended. Only one person needs to interact with the objective for the "Locate" portion, but every player must individually pick up the quest item (Maintenance Log) to complete their own task.

**Q: What happens if I die after picking up the quest item?**

A: If the item is in your physical backpack or rig, you will lose it upon death. However, if you place quest items in your **Safe Locket**, they will persist even if you are KIA. Always prioritize moving quest-critical items to your secure container immediately.

**Q: Is the Water Treatment Plant a PvP hotspot?**

A: In Warfare mode, yes. Because multiple factions have similar early-game quests in these industrial sectors, you are very likely to encounter enemy PMCs. Always listen for the distinct sound of non-suppressed gunfire or the arrival of enemy helicopters.
