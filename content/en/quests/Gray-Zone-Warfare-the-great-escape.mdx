<Section icon={Target} title="Gear Setup for a Smooth Extraction">
Your loadout plays a critical role in your ability to make "the great escape." Mobility, survivability, and utility items are paramount. Overloading yourself with unnecessary gear can slow you down and make you an easy target.

<Callout type="success" title="Lightweight Gear for Mobility">
Prioritize lighter armor and a compact weapon setup if your main goal is quick extraction after securing a high-value item. A faster sprint and lower stamina drain can save your life.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Movement & Stamina">
    Equip lighter armor (e.g., Recon Chest Rig over heavy plate carriers) and consider a primary weapon with good ergonomics. Carry energy drinks and water to maintain hydration and energy, which directly impacts stamina. Low hydration (below 50%) leads to no blood regeneration, and low energy (below 50%) also stops blood regeneration.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Essential Medical Supplies">
    Always carry combat tourniquets, bandages, and painkillers. Untreated bleeding and pain can quickly incapacitate you. If blood drops below 50 percent, you risk entering a coma, and squadmates have only about 180 seconds to revive you.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Secure Lockbox Management">
    Place all high-value task items and irreplaceable loot into your Secure Lockbox immediately. Items inside are protected even if you die. Quest items sometimes survive when placed in secure storage, but prioritize quick hand-ins.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Navigation Tools">
    A reliable compass and a physical map (or mental map knowledge) are invaluable. Digital maps can be accessed, but understanding your surroundings without constant menu-checking is a survival advantage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Communication (Squad Play)">
    If playing in a squad, coordinate your extraction. Designate a rally point, cover each other's flanks, and communicate enemy sightings or changes in LZ status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Considerations for Safe Exfils">
Extraction is rarely a simple walk to the helicopter. It often involves navigating hostile territory, avoiding patrols, and potentially engaging other players or AI.

<Callout type="info" title="Helicopter Immunity Window">
Players are immune to damage while onboard the helicopter. After stepping off, there is a 4-second invulnerability window. Use this to your advantage for quick repositioning or to avoid immediate threats upon landing.
</Callout>

**Key Tactical Elements:**

| Tactic           | Description                                                                 | Application for Extraction                                          | Risk/Reward                                          |
| :--------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------- |
| **Stealth Movement** | Utilizing cover, low-profile movement, and avoiding direct engagements.     | Ideal for solo players or when carrying high-value loot. Minimizes exposure. | Low risk, but slower. May miss opportunistic kills.  |
| **Flanking & Diversion** | Drawing enemy attention away from the main extraction route.                | A squadmate can lay suppressive fire while others push the LZ.        | High risk for the diverter, high reward for successful extraction. |
| **LZ Scouting**  | Approaching the LZ cautiously, checking for enemies before calling the heli. | Prevents ambushes at the extraction point.                           | Slightly slower exfil, but significantly safer.      |
| **Combat Breakout** | Engaging and eliminating threats directly to clear the path to the LZ.      | Necessary when an LZ is heavily contested or your path is blocked.   | High risk, high reward. Requires good combat skills and gear. |
| **Emergency Exfils** | Having a backup LZ or alternative route planned if the primary is compromised. | Crucial for adapting to dynamic situations on the fly.                | Saves your run when primary plans fail.              |

Remember, the helicopter sound also acts as a tactical distraction for both AI and other players. Use this to mask your movements or to bait enemies into a specific area.

</Section>
