<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Tasks for Efficient Progression">
Tasks are the lifeblood of progression in Gray Zone Warfare, offering not just experience and cash, but also crucial vendor reputation and item unlocks. An efficient exodus strategy involves prioritizing tasks that streamline your access to better gear and resources, reducing the time spent in dangerous areas.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Early Vendor Tasks">
    Focus on tasks for Handshake, Gunny, and Lab Rat. These early missions often involve your starting town and provide essential reputation for basic gear, medical supplies, and weapons. For example, "Meds Wanted" for Lab Rat quickly unlocks better healing items.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify Key-Gated Missions">
    Many tasks are blocked by specific keys. Understanding which keys open which doors, and where to find them, is crucial. The "Helping Hand" task for Handshake is an early example, requiring a specific restaurant storage key.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Plan Multi-Objective Runs">
    Group tasks that are in close proximity or along a similar route. This minimizes travel time and exposure. For instance, tasks around YBL-1 can often be combined with initial scouts towards Ban Pa.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Leverage Task Rewards Strategically">
    Task rewards aren't just cash. They can include containers, specific weapons, or even unlock new vendors like Turncoat after "The Shadow Over Ban Pa." Use these rewards to upgrade your loadout or secure valuable items.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success">
Completing early tasks not only boosts your reputation but also provides a solid foundation for your economy, making subsequent raids more sustainable.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Optimizing Your Economy and Vendor Relations">
A sustainable exodus is built on a strong economy. Knowing how to earn money, what to sell, and how to leverage vendors will keep you well-supplied for every run. Effective trading and vendor reputation management are just as important as combat skills.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Focus on Paid Tasks">
    Early tasks are a reliable source of cash and reputation. Prioritize these to build your initial capital. "Meds Wanted" and "Helping Hand" are examples of tasks that offer good monetary rewards early on.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Prioritize High-Value Loot Containers">
    When looting, prioritize weapon containers (CSI Firearms Box, UN Firearms Box) as they often contain valuable guns, magazines, and weapon parts that fetch good prices.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Sell to the Best Vendor">
    Not all vendors pay the same for items. While you can sell to any vendor, consult the wiki or your knowledge to find the vendor who offers the highest price for specific valuable loot. This maximizes your profit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Convert Cash to Vendor Access">
    Your earned money and task completions directly contribute to vendor reputation. Higher reputation unlocks better gear, more powerful weapons, and essential medical supplies. For example, completing "Native Negotiations" for Lab Rat unlocks Artisan quests.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
The recent Update 0.4 rebalanced rewards to place a greater emphasis on money rather than items for many tasks, reinforcing the importance of a robust economic strategy.
</Callout>
