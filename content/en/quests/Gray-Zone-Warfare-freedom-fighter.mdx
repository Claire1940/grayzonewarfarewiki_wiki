<Section icon={Target} title="Essential Early Game Tasks for Freedom Fighters">
The core of early game progression in Gray Zone Warfare revolves around completing tasks for your vendors. As a budding **Gray Zone Warfare Freedom Fighter**, mastering these initial missions is paramount for gaining experience, reputation, and crucial gear. Many early tasks are designed to familiarize you with your starting town and introduce you to the game's mechanics, while simultaneously providing valuable rewards that kickstart your economy and unlock better vendor stock.

Focus on tasks that build reputation with Handshake, Gunny, and Lab Rat early on. Handshake provides general gear and containers, Gunny offers weapons and ammo, and Lab Rat is your source for essential medical supplies. Efficiently tackling these tasks will ensure you're well-equipped for the challenges ahead.

<Callout type="warning" title="Task Bottlenecks">
Be aware that some tasks, like "Helping Hand," are key-gated. This means you'll need to find a specific key to access a required area, which can temporarily halt your progression. Prioritize key-finding as soon as these tasks appear.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="First Recon (Handshake)">
    This is one of the initial route-setting tasks. It teaches you the landmarks of your starter town and helps you get a feel for navigation.
    **Objectives**: Check the shack on the outskirts, check the town's lumberyard, check the area around the gas station.
    **Known Locations**: Demolished Building - 141 165, Convenience Store - 141 162, Gas Station - 144 161.
    **Rewards**: **400 EXP**, +25 Reputation with Handshake, **$1,700 USD**, 1x Modular Operator Carrier Gen II.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Cache Retrieval (Gunny)">
    An early weapon-progression task that keeps you in the safe starter loop while building Gunny reputation.
    **Objectives**: Find and mark the cache in a shed at the town's lumberyard.
    **Known Locations**: Warehouse Location - 140 163, Lumber Yard Location - 141 161.
    **Rewards**: **400 EXP**, +100 Reputation with Gunny, **$1,700 USD**, 1x Scorpion OCP Convoy Pack.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Meds Wanted (Lab Rat)">
    Crucial for unlocking better medical supplies. Players constantly search for this task to improve their sustain and med access.
    **Objectives**: Find the UNLRA medical supplies at the town's water tower.
    **Known Locations**: Medical Supplies - 140 161.
    **Rewards**: **400 EXP**, +100 Reputation with Lab Rat, **$2,200 USD**, 1x IFAK.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Helping Hand (Handshake)">
    This task is a common key-gated progression point. You'll need a specific key to retrieve intel.
    **Objectives**: Retrieve the intel, bring the intel from the restaurant to Handshake.
    **Known Locations**: Restaurant attic in the starter town, Kiu Vongsa restaurant attic door - 141:164.
    **Rewards**: **400 EXP**, +100 Reputation with Handshake, **$2,300 USD**, 1x Leather Key Holder.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Shadow Over Ban Pa (Handshake)">
    A major progression breakpoint that pushes players out of the starter phase and unlocks the Turncoat vendor.
    **Objectives**: Scout the main northern entry point to Ban Pa, scout the main southern entry point to Ban Pa.
    **Known Locations**: Staging area: YBL-1, Ban Pa sits north of the Tiger Bay shopping center and marina.
    **Rewards**: **1,000 EXP**, +150 Reputation with Handshake, **$4,700 USD**, 2x Recon Chest Rig, Unlock access to Turncoat vendor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Managing Your Economy and Gear as a Freedom Fighter">
A successful **Gray Zone Warfare Freedom Fighter** understands that survival isn't just about combat prowess; it's also about effective resource management. Your economy is directly linked to your ability to complete tasks, loot efficiently, and make smart selling decisions. Early on, focus on low-risk loot routes and prioritize items that offer high value-to-slot ratios.

The game's economy is designed to reward disciplined looting and strategic vendor interactions. Selling valuables to the right vendor can significantly boost your cash flow, allowing you to restock and upgrade your gear without constantly risking high-value runs.

<Section icon={Skull} title="Staying Alive: Health and Survival Basics">
The harsh environment of Lamang Island can be unforgiving. As a **Gray Zone Warfare Freedom Fighter**, understanding the health system and basic survival mechanics is paramount to avoiding unnecessary deaths and preserving your hard-earned gear. Injuries, bleeding, hydration, and energy levels all play a critical role in your combat effectiveness and overall survivability.

Ignoring these elements can lead to a swift and frustrating end to your deployment. Always carry adequate medical supplies and monitor your character's status closely.

### Critical Survival Mechanics:

| Mechanic           | Effect of Neglect                                                              | Treatment/Prevention                                                            |
| :----------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Hydration**      | Below 50%: Thirsty (no blood regeneration); 1%: Dehydrated (hazy vision).      | Carry water bottles; consume drinks regularly.                                  |
| **Energy**         | Below 50%: Hungry (no blood regeneration); 1%: Starving (hazy vision).         | Carry food items; consume food regularly.                                       |
| **Bleeding**       | Light, medium, severe bleeding leads to continuous blood loss.                 | Apply bandages (light), tourniquets (medium), or surgical kits (severe).        |
| **Coma**           | Blood below 50% or destroyed organs (lungs/liver) can induce coma.             | Squadmates have ~180 seconds to revive; use stimulants to prevent/treat.        |
| **Invulnerability**| 4 seconds of invulnerability after disembarking helicopter.                    | Use this window to assess threats and find cover immediately after landing.     |

<Callout type="info" title="Revival Protocol">
If a squadmate enters a coma, you have approximately 180 seconds to revive them before their death becomes permanent for that run. Always prioritize reviving teammates to preserve valuable gear and progression.
</Callout>
