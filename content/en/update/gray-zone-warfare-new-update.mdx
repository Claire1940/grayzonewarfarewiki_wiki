## Redesigned Task and Reputation System

The task system has seen the most significant structural change in this **gray zone warfare new update**. To move away from a linear, often stagnant progression, Madfinger has introduced a regional unlock system tied to a three-tier task hierarchy.

### The Three Pillars of Tasks
Tasks are now divided into three specific categories to provide clarity on lore versus progression:
1.  **Main Tasks:** These drive the narrative of your PMC and the mysteries of "The Event."
2.  **Side Tasks:** Optional objectives that flesh out the local history of Lamang.
3.  **Contracts:** Simpler, repeatable objectives designed for resource gathering and reputation farming.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Death now carries a heavier penalty. Failing a task upon death results in a **10% reputation loss** with the specific vendor and puts the task on a cooldown before it can be re-accepted.

### Regional Progression
The map is now divided into regions. You no longer have access to every quest at once. Instead, you must earn reputation within a specific region to "unlock" the next tier of difficulty and rewards. This RPG-lite progression ensures that players are properly geared before heading into high-threat zones occupied by elite AI factions.

## Combat Mechanics and AI Improvements

Combat in Gray Zone Warfare has always been a "pendulum" between AI that feels like gods and AI that feels brain-dead. The Spearhead update attempts to find the middle ground by reworking detection and hit reactions.

### AI Behavior Tuning
The "360-no-scope" behavior from 100 meters away has been significantly toned down. AI now have a "ramp-up" period where they become aware of your presence, shout warnings, and then engage. Furthermore, they now react dynamically to non-lethal hits—stumbling, holding limbs, or falling over—which provides vital visual feedback that your shots are connecting.

### The Health and Ballistics Rework
A common frustration in previous versions was the "unkillable AI" phenomenon. This was often caused by the interaction between the zeroing of high-magnification scopes (LPVOs) and the game's organ-based health system.

*   **The Neck Hitbox Issue:** The game focuses on internal organs (brain, heart, lungs). There are no vital organs in the neck or collarbone. 
*   **The Zeroing Trap:** Many scopes are zeroed for 100m. In close-quarters (under 50m), shots aimed at the head often land in the neck/collarbone area. Because there are no vital organs there, AI can tank multiple rounds.
*   **The Solution:** The update adds a color-coded "paper doll" UI to help you diagnose injuries and improves hit registration clarity.

| Combat Element | Update 0.4 Improvement |
|---|---|
| **Movement** | **Tactical & Burst Sprint** added; stamina scales with weight. |
| **Detection** | AI no longer track players perfectly through thick foliage. |
| **Hit Reactions** | New animations for **organ vs. bone hits**. |
| **Audio** | **Spatial audio overhaul** for better directional tracking. |

## Economy and Vendor Specialization

The **gray zone warfare new update** has completely overhauled how you interact with vendors like Handshake and Artisan. Gone are the days of a "one-size-fits-all" stock; each vendor now serves a specific tactical niche.

*   **Handshake:** Focuses almost exclusively on Western military gear and high-tier attachments.
*   **Artisan:** Handles civilian-grade equipment, AK platforms, and—most importantly—**secure containers** and stash expansions.
*   **Unlimited Stock:** All items sold by vendors now have unlimited stock, removing the frustration of global timers, though prices have been adjusted upward for high-end gear.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the new "Marketplace" features on community sites to track trade deals, as world loot now contains high-value "chase items" that cannot be bought from vendors.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the gray zone warfare new update include a stash wipe?**

A: Yes, Update 0.4 "Spearhead" included a full wipe of player progress, stashes, and reputation to accommodate the massive changes to the task and vendor systems.

**Q: How do I unlock new regions in the 0.4 update?**

A: You must complete tasks and trade with vendors within your current region to increase your reputation. Once you reach specific reputation milestones, the next geographical region and its associated tasks will unlock.

**Q: Can I still play solo in the new update?**

A: Absolutely. While the update adds features to promote team play—like coma timers and teammate healing—the AI tuning and new stealth-friendly biomes make solo play more viable than in previous versions.

**Q: What happened to the "M4 Meta" in Update 0.4?**

A: Madfinger Games has rebalanced vendor stocks and pricing to make Western gear more expensive. Additionally, many high-tier attachments are now world-loot only, encouraging players to use a wider variety of platforms found in the field.
