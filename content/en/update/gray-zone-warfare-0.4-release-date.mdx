## Revamped Task System and Progression

One of the most significant changes arriving with **Gray Zone Warfare 0.4** is the complete redesign of the task system. The developers have moved away from a linear, often repetitive mission structure toward a more dynamic, replayable progression loop. The total number of tasks now exceeds 250, providing a significant increase in content compared to previous builds.

### Task Categories and Regional Unlocks
Tasks are now organized into three distinct categories, each serving a different purpose in the player's journey. Furthermore, the map is now divided into regions that must be unlocked through reputation and progress.

| Task Type | Color Code | Primary Objective |
|---|---|---|
| **Main Tasks** | Yellow | Focuses on the overarching PMC storyline and world events. |
| **Side Tasks** | (Standard) | Expands on the lore of Lamang and hidden secrets. |
| **Contracts** | Blue | Simpler, repeatable objectives for consistent income and XP. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Death during a task now results in immediate failure. In most cases, the task will become temporarily unavailable. Additionally, failing a task results in a reputation penalty equal to 10% of the potential success reward.

### Replayability and Reputation
The majority of tasks in the 0.4 update are now replayable. While completing a task for the first time offers the maximum reputation boost, subsequent completions still provide value, though at a slightly reduced rate. This ensures that players always have objectives to pursue, even after reaching "end-game" status.

## Economy and Vendor Overhaul

The economy has seen a massive shift to reduce the reliance on a single "meta" weapon (such as the M4) and to provide more utility to different vendors. In **Gray Zone Warfare 0.4**, vendor stock is now specialized and unlimited, removing the frustration of items selling out during peak hours.

*   **Handshake:** Now focuses almost exclusively on Western military-grade gear and weaponry.
*   **Artisan:** Specializes in civilian equipment, AK-platform parts, and essential storage containers for your locker.
*   **Unlimited Stock:** All vendors now have infinite supplies of their listed items, though high-end gear remains gated behind reputation levels.
*   **Ammo Availability:** Top-tier ammunition like 7.62 BP and 545 BS are now purchasable once the appropriate vendor level is reached, balancing the playing field against heavily armored AI and players.

Pricing has also been adjusted. While high-end gear is more expensive, tasks now reward significantly more currency. This shifts the gameplay focus toward completing objectives to fund your kits rather than purely scavenging for sellable loot.

## AI Improvements and World Interactions

The AI in Gray Zone Warfare has often been a point of contention due to their "aim-bot" tendencies. The 0.4 update introduces several behavior modifications to make encounters feel more tactical and less punishing from extreme distances.

1.  **Patrol Groups:** AI NPCs now move in designated patrol routes through jungles and along roads, making the world feel inhabited rather than static.
2.  **Specialized Roles:** You will now encounter specific AI types, including **Bodyguards** and **Sergeants**. These units are distinguished by superior gear and more aggressive tactical movement.
3.  **Boss Mechanics:** Bosses no longer spawn in isolation. They are now accompanied by a dedicated squad of guards, requiring players to plan their approach carefully.
4.  **Detection Balancing:** Adjustments have been made to prevent AI from detecting players through dense foliage with unnatural accuracy or landing "one-tap" kills with iron sights from 200 meters away.

## Technical Optimizations and Player Stats

Performance has been a major focus leading up to the **gray zone warfare 0.4 release date**. Madfinger Games has implemented several memory and rendering optimizations to help the game run smoother on mid-range hardware.

### Memory and Scope Performance
For players on 16GB RAM systems, the 0.4 update is expected to reduce RAM usage by approximately 3GB. This reduction in memory pressure should significantly decrease stutters and crashes during long play sessions. Additionally, the scope system—a notorious performance hog—has been optimized to improve FPS by nearly 50% when aiming down sights.

### The New Stats Page
Players can now track their long-term progress via a comprehensive stats page featuring over 500 trackable metrics. These include:
*   Total kilograms of food consumed.
*   Liters of blood lost during combat.
*   Kilograms of ammunition expended.
*   Exploration progress across all regions.
*   Hidden stashes and loot boxes discovered.

> **💡 Tip:** Most stats will begin tracking from the 0.4 update forward. Previous data from 0.1 to 0.3 may not be reflected in the new detailed breakdown.

## New Features for Newcomers

Recognizing the steep learning curve of tactical shooters, the 0.4 update introduces several features designed to ease new players into the experience. The Forward Operating Base (FOB) has been redesigned to include a **shooting range** and an **obstacle course** for movement practice.

Furthermore, a 110-page in-game **Field Manual** has been added. This manual covers every mechanic in the game, from medical procedures to weapon maintenance and ballistics. New players will also be greeted with a mandatory tutorial sequence that explains the basic "ins and outs" of the Lamang Island conflict.
