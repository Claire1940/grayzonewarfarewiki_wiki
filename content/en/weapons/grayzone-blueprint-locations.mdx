## Midnight Sapphire Blueprint Locations

Midnight Sapphire is one of the most beautiful yet lethal areas on the map. It currently houses two of the most sought-after blueprints in the game. You will need to be prepared for long-range engagements on the approach and close-quarters combat once inside the hotel.

### MCX Blueprint
The MCX is a fan favorite for operators who prefer suppressed builds. To find this blueprint, head to the **Midnight Sapphire Hotel**. You will want to enter the ground floor and navigate toward the back right-hand side of the building. The coordinates are **171 166**. It is usually resting on a surface in one of the utility or back-office areas.

### M700 Blueprint
Since you are already at the hotel, you should attempt to grab the M700 blueprint. This bolt-action sniper rifle is essential for long-range dominance. From the main stairs, head all the way to the rooftop. Navigate to the far right end of the roof. The blueprint is located near the edge, providing a great view of the surrounding golf course while you secure it.

## Fort Narith Blueprint Locations

Fort Narith is a fortress in every sense of the word. The AI here are heavily armored and highly aggressive. To secure the **grayzone blueprint locations** in this sector, high-penetration ammo is recommended.

### DDM4 Blueprint
The DDM4 is a top-tier AR platform. To find it, head to the barracks located at **141 131**. Specifically, you are looking for the middle barrack. Enter the building, proceed up the stairs, and walk to the very end of the hallway. The blueprint is sitting on a table in the final room. 

### AK308 Blueprint
The AK308 is a powerhouse, but obtaining it is a challenge. You must navigate to the jail section of Fort Narith at coordinates **142 130**. Go to the very back of the jail area until you find a locked door. While it is locked, it can be kicked open with enough force. Once inside, look specifically underneath the coffee pot to find the blueprint hidden away.

> **Warning:** Fort Narith is frequently patrolled by squads. Ensure you have a clear extraction path to the nearest Landing Zone (LZ) before entering the jail section, as it can easily become a deathtrap.

## Hunter's Paradise and Rural Blueprints

If you aren't ready for the heavy military presence of the Fort or the Hotel, there are several blueprints located in the more rural or industrial areas of the map.

### M14 (Mini-14)
Located at **Hunter's Paradise (159 168)**, this blueprint is found at the shooting range. Run to the very back of the range and look for the last door on the left side. The blueprint is resting on a workbench inside this small room.

### AKMN (Jackal Company Skin)
For those looking for the AKMN or the specific Jackal Company variant, head to the "Occupied Cottage" at **195 153**. There is a raised red shack in this area. Inside, next to a pillow on the floor, you will find the blueprint. This location is often quieter than the major POIs, making it a good target for solo players.

### MP7 Blueprint
The MP7 is tucked away in a small village in the middle of nowhere at **188 130**. Look for a small house within the village cluster. Inside, you will find a couch next to a table; the blueprint is typically sitting right there on the furniture.

## Special Interest: Tiger Bay and Remote Camps

### SVD Blueprint
The SVD is located in the high-intensity **Tiger Bay Mall (197 129)**. This is arguably the hardest blueprint to get due to the sheer volume of AI. You need to find the bookstore inside the mall. Go behind the checkout register, through the back door, and turn right. The blueprint is on one of the storage shelves.

### Mossberg 590
For the shotgun enthusiasts, head to the remote campsite southeast of YBL at **149 111**. There is an open shack at this site. The blueprint is sitting plainly on the table inside.

## Essential Mechanics for Collecting Blueprints

Knowing the **grayzone blueprint locations** is only half the battle. There are specific game mechanics in 2026 that you must be aware of to ensure your efforts aren't wasted.

1.  **The Double-Tap Interaction:** When you find a blueprint, the first time you press the interact button, your character may simply "inspect" the magazine or the item. You must interact with it a second time to actually pick it up and place it in your inventory.
2.  **Extraction is Mandatory:** You must successfully extract and return the blueprint to the respective trader to complete the unlock. If you die while carrying a blueprint, the quest may fail, and some players have reported bugs where the blueprint does not respawn immediately.
3.  **Safe Slotting:** If you have space in your secure container, place the blueprint there immediately. This ensures that even if you are "tapped" by a sniper on the way to the LZ, you won't lose the progress.

## FAQ

**Q: Do blueprints respawn if I fail to extract with them?**

A: Generally, they should respawn in the next raid session. However, some players in the 2026 version have reported bugs where the item disappears. It is highly recommended to use your secure container to avoid this issue.

**Q: Can I find these grayzone blueprint locations in any order?**

A: Yes, you can collect blueprints in any order you choose. However, some are locked behind specific trader levels or previous mission completions before the "turn-in" option becomes available.

**Q: Are the coordinates exact for every faction?**

A: Yes, the physical **grayzone blueprint locations** remain the same regardless of whether you are playing as Mithras, Crimson, or Lamang Recovery Initiative. Only your starting LZ and travel distance will vary.

**Q: Is the AKMN blueprint at the Occupied Cottage a skin or a full weapon unlock?**

A: It functions as both. Finding the Jackal Company blueprint unlocks the ability to purchase that specific decked-out variant and provides the base weapon platform for your collection.
