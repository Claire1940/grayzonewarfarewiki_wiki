<Section icon={Target} title="Optimizing Your DDM4: Best Builds and Attachments">
Building an effective DDM4 involves balancing recoil control, ergonomics, and accuracy to suit your preferred playstyle. The goal is to create a weapon that feels stable, aims quickly, and maintains precision during sustained fire. Here’s a breakdown of key attachment categories and their impact.

| Attachment Type | Impact on DDM4 | Recommended Choices | Notes |
| :-------------- | :------------- | :------------------ | :---- |
| **Muzzles**     | Recoil control, Sound suppression | Suppressors (e.g., KAC QDC), Compensators | Suppressors reduce muzzle flash and sound, crucial for stealth. Compensators primarily reduce vertical recoil. |
| **Optics**      | Aiming speed, Target acquisition | Red Dot Sights (e.g., MRS), Holographic Sights (e.g., EXPS3), LPVOs | Choose based on engagement range. Red dots for CQB, LPVOs for versatility. |
| **Foregrips**   | Recoil control, Ergonomics | Vertical Foregrips (e.g., RVG), Angled Grips (e.g., AFG-2) | Vertical grips offer better vertical recoil control. Angled grips improve ergonomics and horizontal recoil. |
| **Stocks**      | Recoil control, Ergonomics | CTR Stock, SOPMOD Stock | Affects recoil absorption and overall weapon handling. |
| **Lasers/Lights** | Hip-fire accuracy, Target illumination | Steiner DBAL-A2, SureFire M600V | Useful for close encounters and low-light conditions. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Weight Management">
Update 0.4 introduced significant changes to weapon weight effects. Heavier attachments can negatively impact ergonomics, sway, acceleration, and ADS speed. Always test your builds in the firing range to find the optimal balance between performance and maneuverability.
</Callout>
