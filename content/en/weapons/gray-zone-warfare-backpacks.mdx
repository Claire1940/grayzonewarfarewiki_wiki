<Section icon={Shield} title="Backpack Types and Capacity">
Backpacks in **gray zone warfare** come in various sizes, offering different storage capacities. Understanding these differences is fundamental to effective inventory management. The larger the backpack, the more loot you can carry, but this often comes with trade-offs in terms of mobility and visibility.

Here's a comparison of common backpack types you might encounter:

| Backpack Type | Size (Slots) | Typical Use Case | Pros | Cons |
|:--------------|:-------------|:-----------------|:-----|:-----|
| **Small Day Pack** | 10-15 | Quick tasks, recon | High mobility, low cost | Limited loot capacity |
| **Medium Assault Pack** | 20-30 | Standard raids, balanced | Good capacity, decent mobility | Moderate visibility |
| **Large Rucksack** | 35-50 | Loot runs, extended missions | High loot capacity, more gear | Reduced mobility, high visibility |
| **Specialized Packs** | Varies | Specific tasks (e.g., medical) | Optimized for certain items | Niche use, may lack general space |

<Section icon={Target} title="Strategic Inventory Management Steps">
Effective use of backpacks goes beyond just picking the biggest one. It involves smart inventory management before, during, and after a raid. Follow these steps to optimize your **gray zone warfare** inventory:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pre-Raid Planning">
    Before deploying, assess your mission. Are you collecting specific quest items, or are you on a general loot run? Equip a backpack that matches the expected loot volume and your need for mobility. Always bring essential medical supplies and ammunition, placing them in easily accessible slots.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize Secure Lockboxes">
    Your secure lockbox is invaluable. Any items placed in it are protected upon death. Prioritize placing high-value quest items, rare loot, or essential medical supplies here. This minimizes loss risk, allowing you to use your backpack for more expendable or lower-priority items.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="In-Raid Loot Prioritization">
    When looting, prioritize items based on their value-to-slot ratio. High-value, small items (e.g., certain electronics, jewelry) are generally better than bulky, low-value items (e.g., common weapons with no attachments). Don't hesitate to drop low-value items from your backpack to make space for better loot.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Organize on the Fly">
    Periodically organize your backpack during a raid, especially after engagements or significant looting. Group similar items, consolidate stacks, and move critical consumables to quick-access slots. This saves precious seconds when you need to heal or reload under pressure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Post-Extraction Sales">
    Upon successful extraction, immediately sort your loot. Sell unnecessary items to vendors for cash, which can then be reinvested into better gear, including larger or specialized backpacks. Remember that certain vendors may pay more for specific item types.
  </Step>
</Steps>
