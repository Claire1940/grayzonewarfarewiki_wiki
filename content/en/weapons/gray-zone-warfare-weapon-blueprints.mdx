## Deep Dive: The MCX Nomad Blueprint

The MCX Nomad is arguably the most sought-after item among the **gray zone warfare weapon blueprints**. This build, known as the LVAW (Low Visibility Assault Weapon), features an integrally suppressed short barrel chambered in .300 Blackout. It is incredibly quiet, making it the perfect tool for night operations or stealthy infiltration.

### Location: Midnight Sapphire Hotel
To find the MCX Nomad, you must infiltrate the main hotel at Midnight Sapphire. This area is notorious for high-density AI equipped with AK-12s and heavy armor.

1.  **Approach:** Enter through the golf club fields or the main entrance.
2.  **Floor:** Head to the second floor of the main hotel building.
3.  **The Item:** Look for a red "Guns & Ammo" style magazine sitting on a circular coffee table in one of the lobby/bar lounge areas.
4.  **Extraction:** Once you have the magazine, you must return it to Banshee at your base.

## High-Tier Blueprints: Fort Nar and Tiger Bay

For those looking for maximum fire power, the **gray zone warfare weapon blueprints** found in Fort Nar and Tiger Bay are essential. These areas represent the "end-game" of blueprint hunting due to the extreme lethality of the local AI.

### DDM4 (Fort Nar)
The DDM4 blueprint provides a meta-tier M4A1 build. You can find it in the Fort Nar Barracks. You will need to fight through approximately 10-15 geared enemies to reach the second floor. The blueprint is located on a table at the far end of the hallway. This build is highly prized for its exceptional recoil control and ergonomics.

### AK308 (Fort Nar)
Also located in Fort Nar, the AK308 is hidden inside the jail building. After entering the jail, take a left down the main hallway and look for a door you can kick down. The blueprint sits on a shelf inside. Note that the AK308 must be purchased from Vulture once unlocked.

### SVD (Tiger Bay Mall)
The SVD blueprint is located in the "Pum Galaxy" bookstore inside the Tiger Bay Mall. Tiger Bay is currently the most dangerous zone in the game. You must clear the mall entirely, head downstairs past the apparel stores, and find the office in the back of the bookstore. The blueprint sits on a shelf in the rear office.

## Early-Game Blueprint Hunting

If you are just starting your journey in 2026, you don't have to wait for the end-game to find **gray zone warfare weapon blueprints**. Several are located in lower-threat areas that can be tackled with basic gear.

*   **AKMn:** Found at the Occupied Cottage. Simply head upstairs; the manual is on the floor. It unlocks a beautiful jungle-camo AK variant.
*   **MP782:** Located at the Ransacked Tourist Shelter. Look inside the main hut on the couch. This is a great early-game PDW for close-quarters combat.
*   **M14 Gordy Special:** Located at Hunter's Paradise. It is in the building directly next to the shooting range (not the locked room). It’s sitting on a small chair.

> **💡 Tip:** Blueprints can be purchased from traders even if you are Level 1, provided you have successfully completed the retrieval task. This allows low-level players to run high-end gear early.

## Tactical Advice for Successful Retrieval

Hunting for **gray zone warfare weapon blueprints** is inherently risky. To increase your survival rate, follow these community-vetted strategies:

1.  **Clear the Perimeter:** Never rush into a blueprint building. AI in 0.4 often flank or wait in corners. Use grenades to clear rooms before entering.
2.  **Check for Keys:** While many blueprints are in open rooms, the surrounding areas often contain high-value loot behind locked doors. Check the bodies of AI you kill for area-specific keys.
3.  **Team Up:** Places like Midnight Sapphire and Tiger Bay are significantly easier with a squad. One player can provide overwatch while the other loots the blueprint.
4.  **Inventory Management:** Blueprints take up space. Ensure your stash has enough room before you head out, or you might find yourself unable to pick up the item after a hard-fought battle.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to find the blueprint again if I die?**

A: If you have already picked up the item but die before extracting, you will need to return to the location to pick it up again. However, once you turn the task into the trader, the blueprint is permanently unlocked for purchase.

**Q: Can I share gray zone warfare weapon blueprints with my squadmates?**

A: Only one person can pick up the physical blueprint item per raid instance. If multiple squadmates need the same blueprint, you will either need to wait for the loot to respawn or run the raid multiple times.

**Q: Why can't I see the task for a blueprint I just found?**

A: You must interact with the item first to "read" it, which triggers the task. Then, you must interact with it a second time to physically put it in your inventory. Many players forget the second step!

**Q: Are blueprint weapons better than custom-built ones?**

A: Not necessarily. Blueprints provide a "curated" build that is often very efficient and includes unique camos. However, you can often build a more optimized weapon yourself if you have high-level traders unlocked. The main benefit is the convenience and early access to high-tier attachments.
