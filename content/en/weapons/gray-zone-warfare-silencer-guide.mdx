## Understanding Trader Requirements and Compatibility

Before you can begin quieting your arsenal, you must progress through the game's vendor system. Most early-game suppressors are locked behind trader reputation. Gunny is your primary source for Western attachments, while Artisan handles Eastern-bloc hardware.

To access the most reliable suppressors, you generally need to reach **Level 2** with these traders. This is achieved by successfully completing tasks and spending money within their respective shops. Once unlocked, the "Inspect" feature (double-clicking a weapon) is your best friend. It highlights compatible slots, though players should be aware of a known 2026 UI bug where highlights may stop working after extended sessions. If this occurs, a quick server restart usually resolves the issue.

> **💡 Tip:** Always wear ear protection (earmuffs) when traveling by helicopter. While they don't affect your weapon's sound profile, they significantly dampen the deafening engine noise during transit.

## M4A1 Suppressor Setup

The M4A1 is the workhorse of many PMC factions, but it is notoriously finicky regarding muzzle attachments. You cannot attach a suppressor directly to the standard A2 birdcage flash hider found on most stock rifles. 

To suppress an M4, you must first replace the muzzle device with a compatible adapter. The most common early-game combination involves the **RC1 SOCOM 556 Suppressor**. Note that the CAC QD mount, which many players mistake for a universal adapter, is often incompatible with the RC1. Instead, you must purchase and install the **SF3P Three-Prong Flash Hider**.

| Component | Part Name | Trader Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Muzzle Adapter** | SF3P Three-Prong Flash Hider | Gunny Lvl 2 |
| **Suppressor** | RC1 SOCOM 556 (Black/Tan) | Gunny Lvl 2 |
| **Gas Block** | Low Profile Gas Block | Gunny Lvl 1 |

To achieve a clean sight picture when using optics on a suppressed M4, many players also opt for a **Low Profile Gas Block**. This removes the standard front sight post, which can otherwise obstruct your view through Red Dot or ACOG sights.

## Suppressing the AK Series

The AK platform is generally more straightforward than its Western counterparts but requires items from **Artisan**. The primary suppressor for the 7.62x39mm platform is the **PBS-1**. Unlike the M4, the PBS-1 often threads directly onto the barrel once the generic muzzle brake is removed.

However, if you want to add modern optics alongside your suppressor, you must ensure you are using an **"N" variant** of the AK (such as the AKM-N). These variants feature the side-mounted dovetail rail. Without this rail, you will be unable to attach the BP-02 mount required for Picatinny-style sights.

| AK Part Type | Recommended Item | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Muzzle** | **PBS-1 Suppressor** | Direct-thread sound suppression |
| **Handguard** | AK Polymer with Rails | Allows for foregrips and lights |
| **Optic Mount** | BP-02 Dovetail Mount | Adds top rail for Red Dots/Scopes |

## Handgun Suppression: The Glock 17

Building a "John Wick" style suppressed Glock 17 is a popular choice for stealthy secondary options. The most critical piece of information in this **gray zone warfare silencer guide** regarding pistols is the requirement for a **Threaded Barrel**. 

A standard Glock 17 barrel does not have the threading necessary to accept a suppressor. You must purchase the **G17 Match Grade Threaded Barrel** from Gunny. Once the barrel is swapped, you can attach suppressors like the **Ti-RANT 9** or similar 9mm cans.

1.  **Inspect the Glock 17** and remove the standard slide and barrel.
2.  **Install a Threaded Barrel** (Match Grade).
3.  **Equip a Slide with an Optic Cut** (like the Titan slide) if you wish to use a Red Dot.
4.  **Drag the suppressor** onto the newly installed threaded barrel.

## Long-Range Stealth: The Mosin Sniper

The Mosin-Nagant is a devastating early-game tool, but turning it into a suppressed sniper rifle is a multi-step process. You cannot easily suppress the standard infantry carbine. Instead, you should look for the **Mosen Sniper Action** and the **730mm Barrel**. 

For suppression on the Mosin, Artisan provides the necessary muzzle adapters. It is often more cost-effective to scavenge a Mosin from AI in high-tier areas like Tiger Bay or the Shooting Range and then retrofitting the suppressed components rather than buying every individual part from scratch.

> **Warning:** Using a suppressor on high-caliber rifles like the Mosin will significantly increase the weapon's length, making it difficult to maneuver in tight indoor spaces or dense brush.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I drag the suppressor onto my M4?**

A: You are likely trying to attach it to the stock A2 flash hider or an incompatible CAC QD mount. This **gray zone warfare silencer guide** recommends using the **SF3P Three-Prong Flash Hider** as the base adapter for the RC1 suppressor.

**Q: Do suppressors wear out over time?**

A: As of the 2026 updates, suppressors do not have a durability stat that causes them to "break," but firing rapidly will increase weapon heat, which can lead to accuracy degradation (MOA shift) until the weapon cools down.

**Q: Where can I find the threaded barrel for the Glock?**

A: The G17 Match Grade Threaded Barrel is available from **Gunny at Level 2**. If you do not see it, ensure you have completed his early-game quests to increase your reputation.

**Q: Does a silencer make me completely invisible to AI?**

A: No. While it reduces the distance at which AI can hear your shots, they can still hear the "crack" of a supersonic bullet passing near them or see the impact of rounds on surfaces. Always change positions after firing several shots.
