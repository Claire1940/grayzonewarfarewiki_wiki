## Understanding the LZ System Mechanics

The transport system in Gray Zone Warfare is built around "Landing Zones" (LZs). These are designated areas where Little Bird helicopters can safely touch down to drop off or pick up contractors. When you first start your journey, your map will be largely empty, save for your faction's main base. As you explore the jungle and complete tasks for vendors like Handshake or Gunny, you will naturally discover and unlock new LZs.

To use the system, you must open your Map tab and locate an unlocked LZ. By clicking on the icon and selecting "Call Transport," a timer begins. A helicopter is dispatched from your main base to that specific location. It is important to note that these helicopters are shared resources within your faction; if all birds are currently in the air, you may have to wait for one to become available.

### Helicopter Interaction Controls

| Action | Control/Keybind | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Call Transport** | Map Click -> Call | Dispatches a helicopter to the selected LZ. |
| **Check Ammo/Mag** | **V** | Quickly verify your combat readiness before boarding. |
| **Noise Canceling** | **L** | Reduces engine volume if wearing an in-game headset. |
| **Fast Depart** | **L** (Once Boarded) | Signals the pilot to take off immediately without waiting. |
| **Full Weapon Check** | **Alt + V** | Checks chamber, fire mode, and zeroing distance. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always perform a full weapon check (Alt + V) while waiting for your helicopter. Ensure your fire mode is set to "Single" for long-range LZ defense or "Full Auto" if you suspect a close-quarters ambush.

## How to Unlock and Discover New LZs

Discovery is a manual process. You cannot simply see every **gray zone warfare helicopter landing zones** location from the start. You must physically travel to the vicinity of an LZ to "discover" it. Once discovered, it remains permanently unlocked for your character, allowing you to fly there from the base or call for an extraction from that point in the future.

Strategic exploration is key. Many LZs are positioned near major points of interest (POIs) such as gas stations, water construction sites, or military bunkers. For example, during early-game tasks like "Little Bird Down," you will find that the objective is located in the woods south of a specific gas station, very close to a nearby LZ. Unlocking these points early allows for faster quest progression and provides multiple "plan B" exit routes if a specific area becomes too "hot" with enemy players.

### Discovery and Travel Steps

1.  **Accept a Task:** Vendors will often give you a general grid location for an objective.
2.  **Infiltrate the Area:** Fly to the closest known LZ and travel on foot toward the new objective.
3.  **Physical Proximity:** As you get close to a new landing area, a notification will appear on your screen confirming the LZ is now available.
4.  **Strategic Use:** Once unlocked, you can now use this LZ to bypass long jungle treks on your next deployment.

## Advanced Tactics for Safe Extraction

The most dangerous moment in Gray Zone Warfare is often the extraction phase. Because **gray zone warfare helicopter landing zones** are visible to everyone in your faction and can be camped by enemy factions, you must approach them with extreme caution. In the 2026 meta, "LZ Camping" remains a viable, albeit frustrating, tactic used by rival contractors.

When you call for a transport, the helicopter's arrival is loud. This noise alerts nearby AI and players to your exact location. It is highly recommended to stay hidden in the brush or a nearby building until the helicopter is seconds away from touching down. Do not stand in the center of the landing circle, as this makes you an easy target for snipers.

### Outposts and Respawn Management

With the 0.3 update, outposts have become a critical component of the LZ ecosystem. Outposts act as forward operating bases that allow for closer respawns if you die during a mission. However, these can be "contested."

*   **Contesting an Outpost:** If you shoot at an AI near an outpost, it becomes "Alarmed." A specific icon will appear on the map, marking the area as contested.
*   **Preventing Respawns:** While an outpost is contested, enemy players cannot respawn there. This is a vital tactic when trying to clear an LZ for a safe departure. By keeping the local AI in a state of alarm, you prevent a constant stream of enemy reinforcements from swarming your extraction point.

## Faction-Specific Landing Zones

Your choice of faction—Lamang Recovery Initiative, Mithras Security Systems, or Crimson Shield International—determines your starting corner of the map. Consequently, your initial **gray zone warfare helicopter landing zones** will be different from players in other factions. 

While the central areas of the map contain neutral LZs that any faction can theoretically use (though they are often the site of heavy PVP), the LZs closest to your home base are generally safer. You are not permitted to kill members of your own faction; doing so can lead to being reported and losing reputation. Therefore, utilizing LZs within your faction's "influence zone" is always the safer bet when carrying high-value loot.

> **Warning:** If you die while attempting to reach an LZ, your body remains on the ground with all your gear. If you die a second time before retrieving your first body, the original loot will despawn. Always prioritize safety over speed when returning to a death site near an LZ.

## Surviving the Ride: Quality of Life Tips

The helicopter ride itself can be a moment of vulnerability or a much-needed break. In 2026, several quality-of-life features have been added to make these transitions smoother. If you are wearing tactical headsets, pressing **L** will toggle noise canceling, allowing you to communicate with your squad over the roar of the engine. 

Furthermore, if you are the only one on the helicopter or your entire squad is aboard, pressing **L** again can signal the pilot to depart faster. This reduces the time spent sitting stationary on the ground, which is when you are most likely to be shot by a lurking enemy.

### Weather and Visibility
Lamang Island is known for its tropical storms. Rain can smudge your vision and make spotting enemies near the LZ difficult. To counter this, always equip glasses, goggles, or a hat. These items prevent raindrops from creating a "smudge" effect on your screen, ensuring you have a clear sightline as the helicopter descends.

## Summary of Extraction Best Practices

To ensure you never lose a kit due to a poor extraction, follow this checklist:
1.  **Clear the Area:** Ensure no AI are actively hunting you before calling the bird.
2.  **Check the Map:** Verify if the nearest outpost is contested.
3.  **Stay Low:** Use the `Ctrl + Scroll Wheel` function to adjust your crouch height for optimal cover while waiting.
4.  **Communicate:** If playing with friends, have one person watch the perimeter while the other manages the map and transport calls.
5.  **Secure Loot:** Place your most valuable items (keys, rare attachments) in your Safe Lockbox before heading to the LZ.

## FAQ

**Q: How many helicopters can my faction have in the air at once?**

A: Factions typically have a limited pool of helicopters (usually 4). If all are in use, you will see a "Transport Unavailable" message. You must wait for one to return to base or finish its route before you can call it to your **gray zone warfare helicopter landing zones**.

**Q: Can I get shot while sitting inside the helicopter?**

A: Yes. While the helicopter provides some protection, the doors are open. You can be injured or killed by small arms fire during takeoff and landing. It is highly recommended to wear a high-tier helmet and vest with plates to mitigate this risk.

**Q: Do I have to return to the main base to turn in quests?**

A: As of the 0.3 update, you no longer need to return to the main base for most quest completions. You can turn in tasks "on the fly" through your menu. However, if a quest requires you to pick up a physical item (like a tracker or a key) from a vendor, you must still visit the main base.

**Q: What happens if I miss my helicopter?**

A: If the timer reaches zero and you are not at the LZ, the helicopter will wait for a short duration before departing empty. If you miss it, you will have to wait for the cooldown period to end before calling another transport to that location.
