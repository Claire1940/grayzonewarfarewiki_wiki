## Gray Zone Warfare North Mithras Guide: Strategic POI Navigation

The transition to northern operations often begins with tasks that require you to leave the comfort of the starting zone. For Mithras players, this usually involves moving from the eastern side of the map toward the central and western northern sectors.

### Hunter's Paradise
Located north of the central jungle, Hunter's Paradise is a sprawling resort and shooting range. For Mithras players, the primary landing zone is **Foxtrot 1**. This area is critical for tasks like "True Grit" and "Up to Snuff." The AI here are more coordinated than those in Nam Thaven, often utilizing cover and flanking maneuvers.

### Midnight Sapphire
This luxury resort is a death trap for the unprepared. The AI are numerous and possess high accuracy at range. As part of this **gray zone warfare north mithras guide**, we recommend approaching Midnight Sapphire via **Hotel 1** (Northeast) or **Hotel 3** (Southwest). The "Celebrity Crash" and "Deepest, Darkest Fantasies" missions take place here, requiring you to infiltrate villas that are often overlapping with enemy patrol routes.

### Fort Narith
The ultimate test for any operator. Fort Narith is a massive military base with an airfield, barracks, and a fortified headquarters. It is the primary source for the game's best equipment but features the most aggressive AI in Lamang.

## Essential Mission Walkthroughs for Mithras

Progression in 2026 is gated by specific missions from Handshake and Artisan. Two of the most pivotal tasks for unlocking the northern LZs and high-tier gear are "New Neighbors" and "Fort Narith Stroll."

### New Neighbors (Handshake)
This task requires you to investigate the checkpoints around YBL-1, a bunker complex that serves as a gateway to the southern and western sectors.
1. **Investigate Checkpoint NE of YBL-1:** Approach the bridge at coordinates 140, 125. Interact with the radio or truck door to report findings.
2. **Investigate Checkpoint NW of YBL-1:** Head to the second bridge. Be wary of AI patrols on the dirt road leading toward Fort Narith.
3. **Retrieve Attack Plan:** Head west into the jungle from the checkpoint to find a hidden camp. Clear the 4-5 guards and retrieve the 1x1 Attack Plan from the table inside the tent.

### Fort Narith Stroll (Handshake)
To complete this, you must visit four distinct entrances to the military base. Applying the tactics in this **gray zone warfare north mithras guide** will help you avoid unnecessary firefights.

| Entrance | Coordinates | Tactical Advice |
|---|---|---|
| **Main Entrance** | 130, 144 | Approach from the tree line; do not stand on the main road. |
| **Barracks Entrance** | 131, 142 | Use the hole in the wall near Delta 3 for a safer approach. |
| **Fuel Supply** | 127, 146 | Infiltrate via Delta 1; watch for guards behind the trucks. |
| **Airfield Back** | 127, 141 | Best accessed by skirting the western perimeter fence. |

## Gear and Preparation for Northern Operations

Within the context of a **gray zone warfare north mithras guide**, ammunition choice is the single most important factor. Northern AI often wear Level III or IIIA body armor, rendering standard FMJ or Soft Point rounds ineffective.

*   **Ammunition:** Always use **5.56x45mm M855** or **7.62x39mm PS** (or better). If you are using a sniper rifle like the M700, **7.62x51mm M80** is the baseline for one-shot potential.
*   **Armor:** Do not venture North with anything less than a **Level III Plate Carrier**. The "Lancer" or "M2" carriers are preferred for their balance of protection and weight.
*   **Medical:** Northern engagements are often prolonged. Carry at least two **Tourniquets**, four **Hemostatic Bandages**, and a **Small Surgery Kit**.
*   **Night Ops:** If deploying between 22:00 and 05:00, **AN/PVS-14** or **AN/PVS-31A** NVGs are mandatory. The northern jungles are significantly darker than the starting areas.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you die in a northern POI, your body remains for 60 minutes. However, if you die a second time before recovering your gear, the first body (and all its loot) will despawn instantly.

## Tactical Movement and Extraction

A vital part of any **gray zone warfare north mithras guide** is mastering the "Heli Meta." Extraction points in the North are high-traffic areas for all three factions.

1. **The 4-Second Protector:** When you disembark from a helicopter at an LZ like **Delta 3** or **Golf 1**, you have a 4-second window of invulnerability. Use this time to sprint immediately toward the nearest hard cover.
2. **Listen Before You Leap:** Before calling an extraction, find a concealed position near the LZ and listen for gunfire or other helicopters. If you hear a chopper landing, wait to see if it is a friendly squad or an enemy team.
3. **Mithras Specific LZs:** MSS players should prioritize unlocking **Hotel 1** for Midnight Sapphire and **Delta 2** for Fort Narith. These provide the shortest travel times to key mission objectives.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I see the northern landing zones on my map?**

A: LZs like Delta 3, Foxtrot 1, and Hotel 1 must be "discovered" by physically visiting them or by completing specific discovery tasks from Handshake. Many players "taxi" with other faction members to unlock these early.

**Q: What is the best weapon for a Mithras player in the North?**

A: The **M4A1** or **MK18** (DDM4) are the most versatile options due to the availability of M855 armor-piercing ammunition from Gunny. For those preferring the AK platform, the **AK-74M** with 5.45mm PP or BP rounds is a strong alternative found in this **gray zone warfare north mithras guide**.

**Q: How do I deal with the "aimbot" AI in Fort Narith?**

A: Never repeat-peek the same angle. The AI in Fort Narith "lock on" to your last known position. Use grenades to displace them and always engage from at least 50 meters away when possible.

**Q: Is it better to play Solo or in a Squad for northern missions?**

A: While solo play is possible with extreme patience, northern POIs like Midnight Sapphire are designed for squad coordination. Having a teammate to provide overwatch while you retrieve quest items significantly increases your survival rate.
