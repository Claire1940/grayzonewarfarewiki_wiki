## Essential Blueprint Mechanics

Before heading out into the field, you must understand how the blueprint system works in Gray Zone Warfare. Unlike standard loot, blueprints function as quest items that must be physically carried back to your base and turned in to the respective trader.

> **⚠️ Warning:** There is a known interaction "bug" where clicking a blueprint for the first time only "inspects" the item. You must interact with the blueprint a second time to actually pick it up and place it in your inventory. Always double-check your bag before leaving the area.

| Blueprint Feature | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Item Type** | Usually appears as a "Guns & Ammo" style magazine or a technical folder. |
| **Interaction** | Requires a **double interaction**: once to inspect, once to take. |
| **Death Penalty** | If you die with a blueprint, the quest usually fails and you must retrieve it again. |
| **Trader Turn-in** | Most blueprints are turned in to **Banshee** or **Gunny** to unlock shop items. |

## Midnight Sapphire Blueprint Locations

Midnight Sapphire is one of the most dangerous zones on the map, featuring high-tier AI with advanced armor and optics. However, it is also home to some of the most coveted **grayzone blueprints** in the game, including the MCX and the M700 sniper rifle.

### MCX & MCX Nomad (Camo)
The MCX is a premier choice for players who favor .300 Blackout builds. It excels in close-quarters combat (CQC) and is exceptionally quiet when suppressed.

*   **MCX Standard Location:** Ground floor of the main hotel. Head toward the back right-hand side of the building. Coordinates: **171 166**.
*   **MCX Nomad (Camo) Location:** This variant was introduced in the 0.4 update. It is located on the second or third floor of the hotel. Look for a circular coffee table in the bar/lobby area; the blueprint appears as a red magazine.

### M700 Bolt-Action Sniper
If you are already at the hotel for the MCX, you should make the trek to the roof for the M700. This rifle uses 7.62x51 NATO rounds and is arguably the strongest long-range tool for precision shooters.

*   **Location:** The far end of the hotel rooftop. From the main stairs leading up to the roof, head to the far right end to find the blueprint.

## Fort Narith Blueprint Locations

Fort Narith is a sprawling military complex where the AI is numerous and well-coordinated. You will need to bring plenty of ammunition and medical supplies when hunting for these **grayzone blueprints**.

| Weapon | Coordinates | Specific Location Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **DDM4** | **141 131** | Inside the middle barrack. Go upstairs, head all the way down the hall, and find it on a table. |
| **AK308** | **142 130** | Located in the jail section. Go to the back and kick open the locked door. It is under a coffee pot. |

The DDM4 is a highly versatile 5.56x45 NATO platform, perfect for mid-range engagements. Meanwhile, the AK308 offers the hard-hitting power of the .308 caliber in a familiar AK-style platform, though it is exclusively associated with the trader Vulture.

## Tactical & Long-Range Blueprints

For those who prefer to keep their distance or operate in specialized roles, the SVD and M14 blueprints are essential. These weapons bridge the gap between assault rifles and dedicated sniper platforms.

### SVD (Tiger Bay Mall)
The SVD is a semi-automatic powerhouse firing 7.62x54R. Unlike bolt-action rifles, it allows for rapid follow-up shots, making it deadly in the urban corridors of Tiger Bay.
*   **Location:** Tiger Bay Mall (**197 129**). Locate the bookstore within the mall. Go behind the register, through the back door, and turn right. The blueprint is on a shelf.

### M14 (Hunter's Paradise)
Often referred to as the Mini-14 in-game, this rifle is a staple for semi-auto precision.
*   **Location:** Hunter's Paradise (**159 168**). Navigate to the back of the shooting range. Enter the very last door on the left side; the blueprint is resting on a bench.

## Specialized Weapon Blueprints

Not every engagement happens at 300 meters. For close-range dominance and armor penetration, the MP7 and Mossberg 590 are top-tier choices.

### MP7 PDW
The MP7 uses 4.6x30mm rounds, specifically designed to defeat body armor at high fire rates.
*   **Location:** A small village at coordinates **188 130**. Inside one of the small houses, look for a couch next to a table. The blueprint is located right there.

### Mossberg 590 Shotgun
For those who want to "clear rooms" with authority, the 12-gauge Mossberg is the answer.
*   **Location:** A remote campsite southeast of YBL (**149 111**). Look for a shack that is wide open; the blueprint is sitting on the table inside.

### AK&M Assault Rifle
The AK&M is the classic heavy-hitter of the AK family, chambered in 7.62x39. It offers superior armor penetration compared to its 5.45 counterparts but requires skill to manage the recoil.
*   **Location:** A raised red shack at coordinates **195 153**. Once inside, look to the left of the pillow on the sleeping mat.

## Extraction Strategy for Blueprints

Securing **grayzone blueprints** is only half the battle. The real challenge is surviving the trek back to an extraction point (LZ). Because many of these blueprints are located in high-traffic areas like Tiger Bay or Midnight Sapphire, you should follow these tactical steps:

1.  **Clear the Perimeter:** Never rush into a blueprint room. AI in these areas often respawn or wander into buildings.
2.  **The Buddy System:** If possible, play with a squad. One player should provide overwatch while the other interacts with the item.
3.  **Inventory Management:** Blueprints do not take up much space, but ensure you have a secure container or a safe slot in your backpack.
4.  **Avoid Hot LZs:** If you hear heavy gunfire at your nearest extraction point, consider trekking to a secondary LZ to protect your progress.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I find grayzone blueprints more than once?**

A: Yes, blueprints typically respawn in their designated locations after a server reset or after a certain amount of time has passed. However, once you have turned the blueprint into a trader and unlocked the weapon, you generally do not need to collect it again unless you are helping a teammate.

**Q: Why isn't the blueprint appearing at the coordinates?**

A: Another player may have recently picked it up. In Gray Zone Warfare, world items have a respawn timer. If the room is empty, try clearing the area and returning later, or join a different server instance.

**Q: Do I need a specific level to use blueprints?**

A: While you can collect blueprints at any level, you often need to reach a specific reputation level with a trader (like Banshee Level 2 or 3) to actually purchase the kitted weapon once the blueprint is turned in.

**Q: What happens if I die while carrying a blueprint?**

A: The blueprint will remain on your corpse. If you can get back to your body without the instance closing or the body despawning, you can retrieve it. However, if you lose the body, the quest will remain active, and you will have to go back to the original spawn location to find the item again.**
