## The Secret Vendor: Finding Vulture

One of the most significant additions to the Lamang landscape is the secret vendor known as Vulture. Unlike Gunny or Handshake, Vulture does not stay at your faction's base. He is a scavenger who moves between specific **gray zone warfare hidden locations** known as Major COPs (Command Outposts). 

Vulture is vital because he sells scavenged PMC gear—weapons and armor that players have lost in the field. This allows lower-level players to purchase high-tier equipment, such as M14s or heavy armor, long before they have leveled up their standard vendors. Furthermore, Vulture is the only source for high-capacity safe containers, which are mandatory for organized stash management in 2026.

### Vulture's Weekly Rotation

Vulture operates on a weekly rotation. He can be found at two Major COPs simultaneously. You can identify a Major COP on your map by looking for the icons with three black dots rather than the standard single dot.

| Major COP Name | Location Region | Strategic Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Harrison** | Central Lamang | High PVP activity; excellent for scavenging. |
| **Titan** | Eastern Sector | Easier to defend; often held by major factions. |
| **Westmore** | Northern Border | High-tier AI guards; requires caution. |
| **Crusader** | Western Reach | Remote location; good for quiet trading. |
| **Nomad** | Southern Jungle | Dense foliage; high risk of ambush. |

To interact with Vulture, your faction must currently own the COP. If the computer terminal at the outpost is yellow, it is free to capture. Once captured, Vulture will appear in the vendor list, allowing you to buy and sell items.

## Top Gray Zone Warfare Hidden Locations for High-Tier Loot

The loot system in Gray Zone Warfare is mirrored across the map based on your faction's starting position. As you move further from your HQ, the difficulty of the AI increases, but so does the quality of the rewards. Understanding where these **gray zone warfare hidden locations** are situated within these zones is the key to efficient farming.

### 1. The Vona Town Hall Hideout (Low Tier)
Located near the Town Hall in the starting town (Vona for Crimson Shield members), there is a specific UN loot box. This box frequently spawns the **m700 sniper rifle**. For early-game players, this is one of the best snipers available and can be sold for approximately $800 if you prefer to farm currency.

### 2. The Coastal Shack in Banar (Mid Tier)
Banar is a dangerous step up from the starting towns, but it contains a hidden coastal shack. Inside, you will find two weapon boxes that often contain AK-variant rifles and mid-tier armor. This is a quieter alternative to the main market area where most PVP occurs.

### 3. Fort Narith Air Base Loot Room (High Tier)
Fort Narith is notorious for its aggressive, well-armored AI. However, the bottom floor of the main building houses a secret loot room filled with attachments and rare gear. Unlike many other rooms in the base, this specific location does not always require a key, making it a high-priority target for "naked runs" or low-gear infiltration.

## Farming Strategies for 2026

To maximize your efficiency when visiting **gray zone warfare hidden locations**, you must understand the "Search Loading" mechanic. In 2026, loot in containers does not appear instantly. You must wait for the icons to populate one by one, simulating a real-world search. Leaving a container too early might cause you to miss a rare key or a high-value attachment.

### Boss Hunting
Bosses are the ultimate source of unique weapons. You can identify a boss by their unique attire—most notably, the **Beret**. Bosses can spawn even in starting towns, but the bosses in high-tier zones like Midnight Sapphire carry the most "spicy" kits, including fully kitted snipers and rare MK18 variants.

### Midnight Sapphire: The Loot Honey Pot
Midnight Sapphire remains the premier location for high-level PMCs. Because it is a hotel complex, the AI is funneled through corridors and doorways, making them easier to manage than in the open jungle. 

> **Warning:** While the AI is easier to funnel, they carry the best ammo in the game. Even high-tier armor will fail quickly against the armor-piercing rounds used by Midnight Sapphire guards.

## Leveling Vulture and Unlocking Safes

To get the most out of the **gray zone warfare hidden locations** associated with Vulture, you must increase your reputation with him. Vulture does not accept standard weapons or armor for rep; he primarily buys high-value military equipment and specialized loot found in high-tier zones.

### Storage Upgrades
The main incentive for leveling Vulture is the safe containers. These allow you to store massive amounts of currency and high-value items without cluttering your main stash with wallets.

*   **Small Safe:** Unlocks at Vulture Level 3.
*   **Large Safe:** Unlocks at Vulture Level 4. Provides 108 slots of specialized storage.

To level him quickly, farm the military equipment found on guards at Midnight Sapphire or the boss loot at Fort Narith. Selling a single high-value military item (worth roughly $60k) can grant up to 300-700 reputation points depending on the item's rarity.

## FAQ

**Q: How often do the gray zone warfare hidden locations for Vulture change?**

A: Vulture rotates his location every week. He moves between the Major COPs (the icons with three black dots) on the map. He is always at two locations simultaneously for all players on the server.

**Q: Can I find the m700 sniper rifle in the starting town?**

A: Yes, one of the most reliable **gray zone warfare hidden locations** for the m700 is the UN loot box located in the hideout near the Town Hall in your faction's starting town (e.g., Vona for Crimson Shield).

**Q: Why won't Vulture buy my guns?**

A: Vulture is a specialized scavenger. Unlike Gunny, he generally only buys high-value military equipment, specific high-tier loot, and certain scavenged items. To level him up, focus on selling military electronics and boss-tier items.

**Q: Is it safe to farm at Midnight Sapphire in PvE mode?**

A: While you won't encounter enemy players in PvE mode, the AI at Midnight Sapphire is extremely dangerous. They move in dense packs, wear high-level armor, and use armor-piercing ammunition. Always bring your best gear and plenty of medical supplies.
