Each region is anchored by a Major POI, such as a city, an airfield, or a military installation. While you can traverse the entire island by foot, the sheer scale of the map makes the helicopter transport system essential.

## Landing Zones and Extraction Tactics

Landing Zones (LZs) are the lifeblood of your operations. Without them, you are forced to spend hours trekking through hostile terrain. To unlock an LZ, you must physically enter a roughly 100-meter radius of the designated zone. Once discovered, you can call in a "Little Bird" helicopter from your Main Operating Base (MOB) or any other discovered LZ.

There are currently 15 faction-locked LZs (5 per faction) located immediately around the starting bases. These are safe zones where rival factions cannot land or extract. However, as you move deeper into the contested sectors, LZs become "hot," meaning players from any faction can be present at the same time.

> **💡 Tactical Tip:** When calling for transport, the helicopter will always pick you up from the LZ closest to your current physical position. Always clear the immediate area of AI before the bird arrives to avoid being shot while boarding.

## Combat Outposts (COPs) and the Black Market

Combat Outposts are a newer addition to the **gray zone warfare sector map guide** that have fundamentally changed how factions hold territory. Unlike standard POIs, COPs can be captured and controlled. 

### Major vs. Minor COPs
*   **Major COPs:** These are large, heavily fortified positions like Stalwart (154:160) and Westmore (168:161). Capturing a Major COP allows your faction to use it as a respawn point and provides access to your global locker.
*   **Minor COPs:** There are 27 smaller outposts scattered across the map. While they don't offer respawn points, they serve as strategic restock locations and defensive buffers.

### The Secret Vendor: Vulture
Vulture is a unique "black market" vendor who only appears at two Major COPs each week. His location rotates every Monday. Vulture does not require traditional quest completion to unlock; you simply need to find the COP he is currently occupying. He sells "scavenged" gear from fallen PMCs, allowing low-level players to bypass vendor level requirements for high-tier attachments and armor.

## Starter Task Locations by Sector

For those just beginning their journey, the first few tasks from Handshake, Gunny, and Lab Rat are crucial for building reputation. These tasks are generally mirrored across the three faction starter towns.

### First Recon (Handshake)
This task requires you to scout three locations in your local town. 
1.  **Gas Station:** Usually located on the outskirts.
2.  **Convenience Store:** A large building in the town center.
3.  **Demolished Building:** Look for heavy machinery like yellow diggers.

### Cache Retrieval (Gunny)
You must place trackers on two specific crates. 
*   **Lumberyard:** Look for a small shed on the eastern side of the yard.
*   **Warehouse:** Usually located near the water tower; look for a side door leading to a small storage room.

### Meds Wanted (Lab Rat)
Head to the town's Water Tower. Directly beneath it, you will find a medical site with white tents and blue UNLRA logos. The medical supplies are inside the northwest building on the shelves.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you die while carrying a unique quest item (like the medical supplies or the clerk's necklace), the item will despawn from your inventory. Always put quest items in your "lockbox" (secure container) immediately upon picking them up.

## Environmental Hazards and Navigation Tips

Lamang Island is beautiful but deadly. Beyond the AI and enemy players, the map itself contains hazards that require specific gear to overcome.

### Radiation Zones
Certain sectors, particularly those near the "Ground Zero" areas, contain high levels of radiation. Lingering in these zones without protection causes continuous health decay.
*   **Solution:** Carry **Potassium Iodide** pills to curb the effects.
*   **Strategy:** Check your map for yellow hazard icons before choosing an LZ for extraction.

### Minefields
The perimeters of Main Operating Bases and certain Major COPs are protected by anti-personnel mines. These are designed to funnel attackers toward the main entrances.
*   **Solution:** Stay on paved roads or well-worn dirt paths when approaching a base. 
*   **Strategy:** If you hear a "click," do not move. In many cases, however, these mines are proximity-fused and will detonate instantly.

### Locked Doors and Keys
Many high-tier loot rooms and quest objectives are behind locked doors. Keys are randomly dropped by AI enemies in the corresponding sector. For example, the **KV Doctor's Office Key** is required for the "Medical Detective" quest in Kiu Vongsa and can be found by farming AI in that town.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find the secret vendor Vulture using the gray zone warfare sector map guide?**

A: Vulture is not marked permanently on the map. He occupies two Major Combat Outposts (COPs) every week, rotating on Mondays. You must capture or visit a Major COP held by your faction to see if he is present.

**Q: Are Landing Zones shared between different factions?**

A: Starter LZs (the 5 closest to each base) are faction-locked. However, all other LZs in the central and contested regions are shared. If you call a helicopter to a shared LZ, be prepared for an ambush, as enemy players may be landing or extracting at the same time.

**Q: What happens if I lose a quest item like a key or a folder?**

A: If the item is a unique quest objective (like a folder or medical box), it will respawn at its original location in the next raid. If it is a key you found on an AI, you will need to farm AI in that specific sector again to find another one.

**Q: Can I travel between LZs without going back to my main base?**

A: Yes, you can request transport from one discovered LZ directly to another. However, each flight consumes a significant amount of time and resources, so plan your routes to complete multiple tasks in a single sector before moving on.
