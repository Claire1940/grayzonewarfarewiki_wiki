Gray Zone Warfare is a tactical extraction shooter primarily designed for PC, emphasizing a realistic and immersive experience. As of its early access launch on April 30, 2024, the game does not feature native, official controller support for PC players. This means that while you might be able to plug in a controller, the game won't natively recognize it or provide optimized button layouts and sensitivity settings. <Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Utilize Steam Input">
    Steam's built-in Big Picture Mode offers robust controller configuration options. You can create custom profiles for Gray Zone Warfare, mapping mouse movements to analog sticks and keyboard keys to buttons. This is often the most straightforward method for Steam users.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Third-Party Mapping Software">
    Software like reWASD or JoyToKey allows for more granular control over button remapping. These tools can emulate keyboard and mouse inputs, providing a more customizable experience, including deadzone adjustments and sensitivity curves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Community Profiles">
    Check online forums like Reddit (r/GrayZoneWarfare) or community Discord servers for pre-made controller profiles shared by other players. These can serve as a good starting point, saving you time on initial setup.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Optimizing Your Controller Experience">
When using third-party software, focus on mapping essential actions like movement, aiming, shooting, and basic inventory navigation. Consider leaving less critical or precise actions to a nearby mouse or keyboard for quick access.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Challenges of Controller Play on PC">
Adapting a controller to a PC-centric tactical shooter like Gray Zone Warfare presents several challenges. The game's design prioritizes the precision and speed offered by a mouse and keyboard, which controllers inherently struggle to match in certain aspects.

| Feature         | Mouse & Keyboard Advantage                  | Controller Limitation                                |
| :-------------- | :------------------------------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------- |
| **Aiming**      | Pinpoint accuracy, rapid flick shots        | Slower, less precise aiming, reliance on aim assist (if any) |
| **Inventory**   | Fast drag-and-drop, quick hotkey access     | Cumbersome cursor movement, slower item management   |
| **Movement**    | Instant directional changes, fluid strafing | Analog stick movement can feel less responsive       |
| **Keybinds**    | Vast array of customizable keys             | Limited buttons, often requiring complex radial menus |
| **UI Navigation** | Direct cursor interaction                 | Slower menu traversal, potential for frustrating inputs |
