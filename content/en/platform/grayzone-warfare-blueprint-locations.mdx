## Tactical Breakdown: Midnight Sapphire Blueprints

Midnight Sapphire is one of the most lucrative yet dangerous areas on the map. It serves as a high-tier loot zone where the AI is heavily armored and highly aggressive.

### The MCX and M700
Both the MCX and the M700 are located within the main Hotel complex. The MCX is an exceptional choice for close-quarters combat due to its .300 Blackout chambering, which performs remarkably well when suppressed. You can find it on the ground floor toward the rear right-hand side of the building.

The M700, a bolt-action powerhouse, requires a climb to the rooftop. Once you reach the roof via the main stairs, head to the far right end. The blueprint is typically resting on a ledge or table. This weapon is a staple for long-range overwatch, capable of dropping most AI with a single well-placed shot to the upper torso or head.

## Fort Narith: The DDM4 and AK308

Fort Narith is a massive military installation that demands a squad-based approach for safe navigation. The **grayzone warfare blueprint locations** here are tucked away in high-security zones.

### DDM4 Location
The DDM4 is the gold standard for 5.56 NATO platforms in the current 2026 meta. To find it, enter the middle barrack at coordinates 141 131. Navigate to the second floor and proceed to the very end of the hallway. The blueprint is located on a desk. This rifle offers a perfect balance of recoil control and modularity, making it a favorite for mid-range skirmishes.

### AK308 Location
For those who prefer the stopping power of the 7.62x51 NATO round in an AK platform, the AK308 is a must-have. Head to the jail section (142 130). You will encounter a locked door that can be breached by kicking it. Inside, look near the coffee pot to secure the blueprint. 

> **💡 Tip:** Fort Narith is a "High Loot" zone. The AI here often carries armor-piercing rounds. Ensure you are wearing at least Tier 3 plates before attempting to solo this area.

## How to Successfully Extract with Blueprints

Securing a blueprint is only half the battle. If you die before returning the item to a trader, the quest will fail, and you may lose the item entirely. Some players have reported bugs where lost blueprints do not respawn immediately, making successful extraction paramount.

1. **Secure Container:** Always place the blueprint in your Secure Container (Gamma/Alpha box) immediately upon pickup. This ensures that even if you die, the item remains with your character.
2. **Double Check Pickup:** As mentioned previously, ensure the item is actually in your inventory and not just "inspected."
3. **Avoid Hot LZs:** When extracting from high-risk areas like Tiger Bay, avoid the most popular Landing Zones (LZs). If possible, run to a secondary LZ further away to avoid "LZ campers" or heavy AI patrols.
4. **Faction Cooperation:** If playing in PvPvE, try to coordinate with other squads from your faction. Having a "tail" on your helicopter can prevent other factions from wiping your progress.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a key for all grayzone warfare blueprint locations?**

A: No, many blueprints like the MCX and M700 are in open or breachable areas. However, the AK308 requires you to kick down a door, and future updates may lock more blueprints behind specific keycard requirements.

**Q: What happens if I die with a blueprint in my backpack?**

A: If the blueprint is not in your Secure Container, you will lose it upon death. You must return to the location to retrieve it, though some players have reported issues with items not respawning correctly if the quest is active.

**Q: Can I buy blueprints from traders?**

A: You cannot buy the physical blueprints, but finding them and returning them to the traders (like Gunny or Vulture) unlocks the ability to purchase the associated weapons and parts directly from their shop.

**Q: Are blueprint locations the same for all three factions?**

A: Yes, the physical locations of the blueprints on the map are static and do not change regardless of whether you are Crimson Shield, Mithras, or Lamang Recovery Initiative. However, your travel distance to each location will vary based on your faction's HQ.
