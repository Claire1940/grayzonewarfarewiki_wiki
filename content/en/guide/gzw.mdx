## Ballistics Overhaul: Impact Angles and Ricochets

The most significant change in the 0.4.1 update involves the complex ballistic system that defines the lethality of every encounter. Previously, players reported "tanky" AI and inconsistent hit registration where well-placed shots failed to neutralize targets. The developers have addressed this by adjusting the effect of impact angles on overall bullet penetration performance.

In earlier versions, steep impact angles often resulted in disproportionate damage reduction or unintended ricochets. The new system ensures that the "feel" of the game matches the visual feedback. If you land a shot on a vital area, the internal calculations now better prioritize penetration over deflection, especially when dealing with armor and biological structures.

| Feature | Previous Behavior (Pre-0.4.1) | Updated Behavior (Patch 0.4.1) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ricochet Probability** | Extremely high, even on soft surfaces and bones. | Reduced frequency; more predictable deflection. |
| **Impact Angles** | Steep angles caused massive penetration loss. | Adjusted math to favor lethality at off-angles. |
| **Bone Interactions** | Bones could cause bullets to bounce internally. | Less likely to bounce off bones that should be penetrable. |
| **Kill Consistency** | Inconsistent due to "adamantium" AI skulls. | Higher reliability for head and torso vitals. |

> **💡 Tip:** Even with the ricochet reduction, always aim for the "T-box" on the head or the center mass of the chest to ensure the highest probability of bypassing armor and reaching vital organs.

## Weapon Zeroing and Optic Fixes

For long-range marksmen in **gzw**, the zeroing bug was perhaps the most frustrating technical hurdle. Before the 0.4.1 patch, many players noticed that their zeroing would reset to default values immediately after leaving the Aim Down Sights (ADS) view. This led to shots consistently hitting low, even when the target was within the intended range.

The update has fully resolved the convergence point issues between the scope and the bullet's ballistic trajectory. This means that if you set your M700 or M4 to a 200-meter zero, that setting will persist through movement and re-aiming. 

### Key Optic Improvements:
*   **Zeroing Persistence:** Your manual adjustments no longer reset when you stop aiming.
*   **Scope Compatibility:** Previously un-zeroable scopes have been fixed to allow for full range adjustment.
*   **ADS Clarity:** Fixes to NPC model resolution when viewed through high-magnification optics at a distance.

## Technical Optimizations and UI Enhancements

Beyond the combat mechanics, the 0.4.1 update focuses heavily on the stability and quality of life for the **gzw** community. Memory optimizations on the server side aim to reduce the performance degradation often seen during long sessions or in high-density areas like the Tiger Bay Mall.

### Loot and Economy Adjustments
The developers have updated the loot spawn chances for various valuable items across Lamang. While specific percentage changes aren't always disclosed, the goal is to make high-tier locations more rewarding for the risk involved. Additionally, the "wallet bug"—which caused critical errors when stacking uneven amounts of currency—has been resolved.

### User Interface Fixes:
1.  **Blood Level Indicator:** The UI now accurately reflects your actual blood level, preventing "surprise" comas.
2.  **Backpack Stacking:** Fixed an exploit/bug where backpacks containing weapons could be nested indefinitely, which previously caused inventory corruption.
3.  **Task Statistics:** Decision-based tasks are no longer counted twice in your completion statistics.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be cautious when moving items within container detail windows. While many bugs were fixed, the complex inventory system can still occasionally desync if items are moved too rapidly during server lag.

## FAQ

**Q: Did the GZW 0.4.1 update improve FPS?**

A: Yes, the patch included several server-side memory optimizations and fixed a rare bug that limited players to 15 FPS after respawning. While individual results vary based on hardware, general stability has seen an upward trend in 2026.

**Q: Can I still ricochet bullets off the ground into enemies?**

A: While ricochets are still part of the complex ballistic system, the frequency has been reduced to prevent "ping-pong" bullets. It is no longer a reliable strategy to rely on ground deflections for kills.

**Q: How do I fix the "Critical Error" when moving money in my wallet?**

A: This issue was specifically addressed in Patch 0.4.1. Ensure your game is updated to the latest version via Steam to eliminate the error when handling partial stacks of currency.

**Q: Why are my shots still hitting low after the update?**

A: Check your zeroing distance. If you are zeroed for 100m but shooting at a target at 50m, your bullet will hit slightly low or high depending on the bore height of your optic. The patch fixed the bug where the zeroing *reset*, but manual range estimation is still required.
