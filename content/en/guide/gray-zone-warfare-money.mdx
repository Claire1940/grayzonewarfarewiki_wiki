## The Villa Vongfett Gold Mine

One of the most consistent ways to build wealth is the Villa Vongfett run, located near the Oscar 1 landing zone. This area is densely packed with rare valuables that take up minimal inventory space, making it an ideal destination for solo players or small squads. The primary goal here is to locate high-value jewelry and the elusive Villa Vongfett room key, which remains one of the most sought-after items in the 2026 meta.

When approaching the villa, exercise caution near the front gates, as AI combatants typically patrol the entrance. Once cleared, your priority should be the interior rooms which contain jewelry boxes and briefcases. These containers frequently house diamond necklaces and rings that sell for a premium. Additionally, check the shelves for "Snake Wine" and other rare consumables that fetch a high price at the vendors.

| Loot Source | Potential Items | Value Tier |

| **Jewelry Box** | Diamond Necklaces, Gold Rings | High |

| **Tables/Shelves** | Snake Wine, Digital Cameras, Tequila | Medium-High |
| **Purses** | US Dollars (up to $1,000), Small Valuables | High (per slot) |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Always loot the AI guards in this area. They have a chance to drop the Villa Vongfett room key. In the current player-driven economy, this key can be traded or sold for upwards of 50,000 credits due to the high-tier loot it hides.

## Maximizing Gray Zone Warfare Money via the Winchester Route

If Villa Vongfett is contested, the Winchester circuit offers a quieter but equally profitable alternative. This route focuses on a specific roadside location and the nearby Syang Radio Station. Start by heading toward the road near coordinates 2145. Look for a stationary truck; in the back, you will often find a purse containing significant amounts of cash and high-end jewelry like diamond necklaces.

After hitting the truck, transition to the Syang Radio Station. This multi-story building is a treasure trove for tech loot and firearms components. The second floor is particularly valuable, featuring a jewelry box and a firearms box that can spawn high-end attachments or even complete weapon systems.

### Syang Radio Station Loot Locations

1.  **Second Floor Office:** Check the desks and the specific jewelry box located in the rear corner.
2.  **Key Spawn:** Next to the telephone on the second floor, keep an eye out for rare key spawns that grant access to locked rooms within the station or nearby farm sheds.
3.  **Firearms Box:** Usually located in the maintenance or security room, this box is a primary source for "Vulture" vendor items.

## Strategic Looting: AI and Inventory Management

To truly optimize your intake of **gray zone warfare money**, you must change how you interact with fallen AI. While many players only check for primary weapons, the real profit often lies in the smaller components. Magazines, high-tier ammunition, and weapon attachments can be stripped and stacked in your backpack. 

The vendor known as "Vulture" is your primary target for selling these items. Leveling up Vulture is critical for long-term success, as it unlocks better trade-in values and superior equipment. When your inventory is full of "junk" weapons, take a moment to remove the magazines and optics. These individual parts often have a better value-to-weight ratio than the heavy rifles themselves.

### Efficient Looting Checklist
*   **Strip Attachments:** Suppressors, high-magnification optics, and tactical lights should always be prioritized over base weapons.
*   **Check Every Body:** Even a basic grunt can carry a smartphone or a rare key in their pockets.
*   **Stack Currency:** US Dollars found in purses and wallets do not take up much space; always prioritize raw cash over bulky low-tier gear.
*   **Empty Magazines:** If you find high-penetration ammo (like AP rounds) in AI mags, keep the ammo even if you discard the magazine.

## Vendor Optimization and Vulture Leveling

Making **gray zone warfare money** is only half the battle; knowing where to spend and sell is the other half. Each vendor in Gray Zone Warfare has a "Reputation" and "Spend" requirement to reach higher tiers. Vulture is the most important vendor for players looking to transition into the late game.

| Vendor | Best Items to Sell | Why Level Them? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vulture** | Rare Valuables, Tech, Jewelry | Unlocks High-End Gear and Better Sell Rates |
| **Gunsmith** | Weapon Parts, Scopes, Mags | Access to Meta Attachments and Ammo |
| **Medic** | Rare Chemicals, Medical Kits | Access to Surgical Kits and Strong Stims |

To level Vulture quickly, focus your farm runs on jewelry and electronics (like digital cameras and smartphones). These items provide a high "spend" credit when sold back to him, helping you bypass the reputation gates faster.

> **Warning:** Avoid selling rare keys to vendors unless you have duplicates. The utility of a key for repeated loot runs far outweighs the one-time cash injection from a vendor sale.

## Security and Night Operations

Many of the most profitable locations, such as the Winchester truck and the Radio Station, are heavily contested during peak daylight hours. Operating at night using Night Vision Goggles (NVGs) can significantly increase your survival rate. AI detection ranges are slightly reduced, and player visibility is limited, allowing you to move between loot spawns with less risk of being sniped.

When performing a night run, ensure you have a reliable infrared (IR) laser for aiming, as traditional optics can be difficult to use with NVGs. Moving slowly and clearing buildings room-by-room is essential, especially in Villa Vongfett where AI can hide in dark corners.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the single most valuable item to find for gray zone warfare money?**

A: While high-end jewelry is great, the Villa Vongfett room key is currently the most valuable due to its high demand in the player trade market. It can easily sell for 50,000+ credits to other players.

**Q: Should I sell my loot to any vendor or wait for a specific one?**

A: You should prioritize selling valuables (jewelry, electronics) to Vulture. Leveling him up is essential for accessing the best gear in the game. Only sell weapon parts to the Gunsmith if you specifically need to level his reputation.

**Q: Is it better to farm solo or in a squad for money?**

A: Solo farming is often more profitable per person because you do not have to split the high-value loot spawns like jewelry boxes. However, a squad provides security, which is vital if you are carrying a rare key or expensive items to the extraction point.

**Q: How often do the loot boxes reset?**

A: Loot boxes and world items typically reset when a new instance is created or after a specific period of time has passed without players in the immediate vicinity. If an area is picked clean, it is best to rotate to a different LZ or wait for a server refresh.
