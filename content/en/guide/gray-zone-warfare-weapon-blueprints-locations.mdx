## Detailed Blueprint Walkthroughs

### 1. The MCX Blueprint (Midnight Sapphire)
The MCX is a premier choice for players who prefer .300 Blackout suppressed builds. It excels in close-quarters combat due to its high fire rate and stopping power. To find this blueprint, head to the Midnight Sapphire Hotel at coordinates **171 166**. Enter the ground floor of the main hotel building and move toward the back right-hand side. The blueprint is typically found sitting on a desk or shelf in the office area.

### 2. The M700 Sniper Blueprint
While you are at the Midnight Sapphire, you should also secure the M700 bolt-action sniper blueprint. This weapon is a long-range powerhouse using 7.62x51 NATO rounds. 
*   Locate the main stairs leading to the hotel rooftop.
*   Navigate to the far right end of the rooftop.
*   The blueprint is located on a small table near the edge of the building.

### 3. The DDM4 Blueprint (Fort Narith)
The DDM4 is a highly versatile AR platform using 5.56x45 NATO. It is widely considered one of the best all-around rifles for mid-to-long-range engagements. 
*   Travel to Fort Narith at coordinates **141 131**.
*   Locate the middle barrack building.
*   Go up the stairs and proceed to the end of the hallway.
*   The blueprint is sitting on a table in the final room.

### 4. The SVD Blueprint (Tiger Bay)
The SVD is a semi-automatic sniper rifle that allows for rapid follow-up shots, making it more forgiving than the M700. However, it is located in Tiger Bay, one of the most dangerous areas on the map.
*   Head to the Tiger Bay Mall at coordinates **197 129**.
*   Find the bookstore within the mall complex.
*   Go behind the service register and through the back door.
*   The blueprint is located on a shelf in the storage room.

## Advanced Farming and Boss Loot

Beyond static blueprints, some of the best weapons in Gray Zone Warfare are obtained by defeating bosses or opening locked rooms. Bosses are usually identifiable by their unique clothing, such as wearing a **Beret**. 

### Locked Room Strategy
Many high-tier weapon attachments and rare guns are locked behind doors requiring specific keys. The best way to farm these keys is to repeatedly clear small, dense areas like the Sawmill or the guard houses outside the YBL-1 bunker.
1.  Clear the area of all AI combatants.
2.  Loot every body; keys have a random chance to spawn on any guard in that POI.
3.  Once a key is obtained, look for the corresponding door, which often contains weapon boxes with pre-modded rifles.

## How to Properly Collect Blueprints

A common mistake players make when reaching **gray zone warfare weapon blueprints locations** is failing to actually pick up the item. The interaction system in the current Early Access build can be finicky.
*   **The Double Tap:** When you first interact with a blueprint, your character may simply "inspect" the item or the magazine. You must interact with it a second time to place it in your inventory.
*   **Inventory Space:** Ensure you have at least a 1x1 or 1x2 slot available in your backpack or secure container before reaching the location.
*   **Safe Keeping:** If you have space in your secure container (the pouch that persists after death), place the blueprint there immediately. This guarantees you keep the item even if you are killed on the way to the Landing Zone (LZ).

## FAQ

**Q: Do weapon blueprints respawn if someone else picks them up?**

A: Yes, blueprints are instanced or respawn on a timer. If you arrive at a location and the blueprint is missing, wait a few minutes or try switching servers. However, in most cases, if you can see the item, it is available for you to grab.

**Q: What happens if I lose a blueprint after dying?**

A: If the blueprint was a quest item for a vendor like Gunny or Artisan, you may need to restart the mission or wait for a server reset. Check your quest log to see if the objective has been reset to "Find the Blueprint."

**Q: Are all weapons in the game available via blueprints?**

A: Not currently. Some weapons, like the MK18, are unlocked primarily by leveling up your reputation with vendors (specifically Gunny Level 3). The **gray zone warfare weapon blueprints locations** listed here cover the specific platform unlocks found in the world.

**Q: Can I find blueprints in the starting towns?**

A: While you can find high-quality weapons like the M700 in UN loot boxes near the starting Town Halls, actual "Blueprints" for the crafting/vendor system are generally located in the mid-to-high tier zones further away from the faction HQs.

**Q: Which blueprint should I prioritize first?**

A: The DDM4 and the MCX are the most impactful for mid-game progression. The DDM4 provides a reliable 5.56 platform that is easy to mod, while the MCX offers superior stealth capabilities for solo players.
