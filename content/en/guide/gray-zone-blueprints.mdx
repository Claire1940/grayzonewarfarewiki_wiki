## Advanced Weapon Building and Vendor Mechanics

Once you have secured your **gray zone blueprints**, the real work begins at the vendors. Gunny, Artisan, and Banshee all offer different parts that can drastically change how a weapon performs. A hidden trick when using vendors is to click on your weapon once to highlight it in yellow. As you scroll through the vendor's inventory, any compatible parts will also be highlighted in yellow, making it much easier to see what fits your build.

### Ergonomics vs. Recoil Control

There is a significant debate within the community regarding whether to prioritize recoil control or ergonomics. In 2026, the meta has shifted slightly toward ergonomics for medium-to-close range engagements.

*   **Ergonomics:** Affects how quickly you can aim down sights (ADS) and how long you can hold your breath before arm stamina drains. High ergonomics makes the gun feel "snappier."
*   **Recoil Control:** Reduces the vertical and horizontal kick when firing in full-auto.

| Stat Priority | Best For | Recommended Attachments |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Ergonomics** | Close Quarters / PvP | Short barrels, lightweight grips, tactical stocks. |
| **High Recoil Control** | Long Range / PvE | Long barrels, heavy compensators, vertical foregrips. |
| **Balanced Build** | General Purpose | 16-inch barrels, suppressors, mid-tier optics. |

### The Muzzle Velocity Factor

Muzzle velocity is often an overlooked stat. Higher velocity not only makes it easier to hit moving targets at range but can actually improve armor penetration. For example, M855 ammo performs significantly better when fired from an 18-inch barrel compared to a 10-inch barrel. If you are struggling to take down armored AI in Tiger Bay, consider swapping to a longer barrel to maximize your ammo's effectiveness.

## Tactical Looting and Inventory Management

Managing your loot is just as important as winning gunfights. Many players struggle with inventory space, especially when trying to carry back multiple **gray zone blueprints** or high-value military tech.

### Wallets vs. Admin Pouches
While Artisan sells an Admin Pouch for 75k, many veteran players prefer using standard Wallets. A Wallet only costs about 5k and provides four slots in a 2x1 footprint. Two wallets provide eight slots for the same space an Admin Pouch uses to provide six. Furthermore, wallets can store more than just money; they can hold business cards, credit cards, and even small military items like jammers or perfume.

### PMC Crate Locations
If you are looking for high-tier gear without spending money, focus on PMC boxes. These are often found in high-traffic areas and contain pre-modded weapons or high-tier armor.

*   **Stalwart:** Contains a PMC weapon crate (often holds M700s or modded M4s).
*   **Westmore:** Contains a PMC armor crate (Level 3+ plates).
*   **Crimson Shield Area:** Hidden crates located in the trenches between Mystic and Crusader.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Large POIs like Midnight Sapphire are heavily guarded. If you are overweight from looting, you can drop your backpack, jump over a wall, and then pull the bag over from the other side to bypass high-threat gates.

## Survival and Combat Efficiency

To successfully extract with your blueprints, you need to master the game's medical and animation systems. Gray Zone Warfare utilizes a realistic medical system where heavy bleeds are the number one cause of death.

### Animation Canceling
You can cancel almost any animation—including reloading, inspecting, or healing—by right-clicking. This is vital if you are caught mid-heal by an aggressive AI. For bolt-action weapons like the Mosin or pump shotguns, you can stop the reload process by left-clicking, which will finish the current shell/bullet insertion and allow you to fire immediately.

### Managing Heavy Bleeds
Always prioritize your "Hot Bar" for heavy bleed treatment. By holding down the `H` key, you can select which medical item is your default. Set this to a Tourniquet or a high-end syringe. In a fight, a simple tap of `H` will then automatically apply the correct treatment for a heavy bleed, saving you precious seconds of blood loss.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I find gray zone blueprints more than once?**

A: No, once you have successfully turned a blueprint into a trader, it is unlocked for that wipe. However, if you die before extracting with the blueprint, the quest may fail or bug out, requiring you to find it again in a subsequent raid.

**Q: Why isn't the blueprint appearing at the coordinates?**

A: Ensure you are at the exact coordinates. Some blueprints, like the AK308, are hidden under objects (like a coffee pot). Also, check if another player in your session has already picked it up; if so, you may need to wait for the loot to respawn or join a different server.

**Q: Do blueprints unlock the weapon for purchase or just the parts?**

A: Usually, finding the platform blueprint allows the trader to sell the base version of the weapon, while specific modification blueprints unlock the ability to buy advanced attachments like specialized handguards or stocks.

**Q: What is the best way to level up Banshee for better binoculars?**

A: To level up Banshee quickly, sell him high-value military equipment found in Midnight Sapphire. Jewelry and watches should be sold to Turncoat for maximum profit, while Artisan pays more for electronics and cards. Selling the right items to the right vendors accelerates your reputation gain.
