### Understanding the Neck Hitbox Issue
The health system in Gray Zone Warfare is organ-based. To kill an enemy instantly, you must hit the brain, heart, or lungs. Currently, the neck and collarbone areas do not contain vital organs in the game's model. Because many high-powered scopes (like the Elcan or Voodoo) are zeroed for 100m, shooting at an enemy at 50m causes the bullet to hit lower than the reticle—often landing in the "empty" neck area.

*   **The Solution:** Aim slightly higher than the head (at the top of the helmet) when engaging enemies within 50-75 meters.
*   **Weapon Choice:** Longer barrels increase muzzle velocity, resulting in a flatter trajectory and more consistent hits.

With the inclusion of binoculars in the 2026 updates, the "red dot meta" has shifted. Players can now scout points of interest (POIs) from safety without draining arm stamina. This is crucial for identifying AI patrols, which now roam between major locations.

*   **Binoculars (Key 9):** Use these to scan for "Lethal" AI factions before committing to an entry. The Tier 4 Banshee binoculars even include a built-in rangefinder.
*   **Helicopter Management:** You can now call helicopters closer to your location (within 100m) for faster extractions. Once seated, hold **L** to expedite takeoff and avoid being a sitting duck at the LZ.
*   **Stealth Audio:** If you are wearing an active headset, use the **O** key to silence the deafening helicopter noise, allowing you to maintain situational awareness as you land.

## FAQ

**Q: When will the full Gray Zone Warfare 1.0 release happen?**

A: According to the current **grayzone warfare roadmap 2026**, the developers are focused on completing the remaining 80% of features. While a specific 1.0 date hasn't been set, the pace of major patches suggests we are still in the mid-to-late stages of early access development.

**Q: Where can I find the secret vendor, Vulture?**

A: Vulture rotates between two of the seven "Major COPs" (triple-star triangles) every week. You can use community tools like `whereisvulture.com` to track his current location and access his high-tier inventory.

**Q: How do I gain reputation without doing quests?**

A: You can gain reputation by selling high-value items to traders. The conversion rate is approximately 1 rep for every $100 in sales. Selling a 60k item will net you 600 reputation, which is a great way to level up Artisan or Turncoat.

**Q: Why do my headshots feel inconsistent in Patch 0.4?**

A: This is usually due to scope zeroing issues and the lack of a neck hitbox. Aim for the top of the helmet at close ranges to ensure the bullet drops into the brain or face hitbox, especially when using 100m-zeroed optics.
