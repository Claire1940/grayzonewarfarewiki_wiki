## Handshake and Gunny: The Combat Essentials

Handshake is often the first vendor players interact with, as he handles the primary questline. His inventory is centered on protection and logistical support. By the time you reach Level 4 with Handshake, you gain access to 3A+ helmets and the "Big Dog" plate carriers, which are essential for surviving late-game zones.

Gunny, on the other hand, is your source for Western-style weaponry. While Level 2 provides the reliable M4 and M855 rounds, Level 4 is where the real power lies. In 2026, Gunny offers the British L403A1 assault rifle, a beastly weapon that dominates mid-to-long-range engagements.

### Gunny's Progression Highlights
*   **Level 2:** M4 pre-builds, M855 ammunition, and magazine cases for stash organization.
*   **Level 3:** Daniel Defense parts, M700 sniper rifles, and high-magnification scopes (up to 18x).
*   **Level 4:** L403A1 Assault Rifle, specialized suppressors, and the massive 96-slot rifle case.

## Lab Rat and Artisan: Utility and Survival

Lab Rat remains the most critical vendor for sustained operations. Without her Level 2 unlocks, players lack access to blood bags, making recovery from heavy bleeding nearly impossible. In the 2026 meta, her high-tier injectors (stems) are game-changers. For instance, the Hemostatic Syringe available at higher levels can stop all bleeding in just two seconds, allowing you to stay in the fight.

Artisan focuses on Eastern-style equipment and storage solutions. If you find yourself constantly running out of space in your stash, Artisan is your best friend. Her level progression provides various cases, from small key holders to massive 210-slot intel items cases.

## Turncoat and Banshee: Advanced Warfare

Turncoat is the specialist for AK enthusiasts. While his early levels offer basic 7.62x39mm options, his Level 4 inventory is legendary. This is where you can reliably purchase the AK-15 and high-penetration "BS" or "BP" ammunition. These rounds are necessary for punching through the heavy armor worn by high-level AI and rival PMCs.

Banshee is the gateway to night operations. Reaching Level 2 with Banshee is mandatory for anyone planning to raid POIs after dark, as he provides the first accessible Night Vision Goggles (NVGs). At Level 4, he sells the Razor 1-10x scope, widely considered the best LPVO in the game for its clarity and versatile zoom range.

> **⚠️ Warning:** High-tier ammunition from Turncoat is expensive. Ensure you are actually hitting your shots, as spraying BS rounds can drain your bank account faster than a failed extraction.

## Vulture: The Secret Vendor

Vulture is a unique addition to the **gray zone warfare vendor levels** system. Unlike standard traders who reside at your base, Vulture is a scavenger found at two rotating major COPs (Command Posts). He doesn't move throughout the week (usually resetting on Mondays), and his inventory is largely based on gear scavenged from dead PMCs on that specific server.

### How to Level Vulture
Leveling Vulture is notoriously difficult. He only accepts "High Value Loot" (jewels, rare electronics, etc.). Furthermore, he pays significantly less for these items than Turncoat does. You are essentially "paying" for reputation with lost potential income.

*   **Level 2 Unlock:** MP7 A2 (allows for grip customization, unlike the A1 variant).
*   **Level 4 Unlock:** AK-308 (a high-caliber beast that is otherwise loot-only).

Because Vulture's stock depends on PMC deaths, his inventory can be "spicy" on active PvP servers. You might find 20-inch M4 barrels or Delta backpacks that you haven't officially unlocked at other traders yet.

## Strategic Tips for Leveling in 2026

1.  **Double-Dip Missions:** Many tasks for Handshake and Gunny take place in the same POIs. Always check your task list before deploying to ensure you are progressing multiple **gray zone warfare vendor levels** in a single run.
2.  **The "Spend" Requirement:** If you have the reputation but haven't leveled up, you likely haven't met the money spent/received requirement. Buying and immediately selling back expensive suppressors or optics is a quick (though slightly lossy) way to bridge this gap.
3.  **Loot for Vulture:** Keep a dedicated container in your stash for high-value items like watches and necklaces. Save these specifically for Vulture runs to maximize your Rep gains with the secret vendor.
4.  **Check the Official Site:** Stay updated on patch notes via the [Gray Zone Warfare official site](https://www.grayzonewarfare.com/) to see if vendor requirements or stock have been adjusted in recent 2026 updates.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I find Vulture at the designated COP?**

A: Vulture is currently known to be slightly buggy in the 2026 build. If he isn't at the weekly designated locations, the server instance might be bugged. Try switching servers or checking community trackers for his current coordinates.

**Q: Do I lose vendor reputation if I fail a mission?**

A: No, you do not lose reputation for failing a mission or dying. However, you do lose any progress made on "collection" tasks if you haven't turned the items in yet.

**Q: Which vendor sells the best ammunition for the M4?**

A: Gunny is the primary source for 5.56mm. While he starts with basic rounds, reaching higher **gray zone warfare vendor levels** with him unlocks M855 and eventually AP rounds.

**Q: Is it worth leveling Vulture to Level 4?**

A: Many veteran players believe the grind to Level 4 Vulture is only worth it if you specifically love the AK-308. Since you can find most of his gear by looting military crates, the high cost of leveling him via rare loot is often seen as a late-game "prestige" goal rather than a necessity.
