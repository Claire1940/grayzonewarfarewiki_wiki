## Choosing Your Faction and Starting Out

Your first major decision is selecting one of the three available factions. This choice is permanent for the duration of the wipe and determines your starting location on the map. Most importantly, you can only squad up with players from the same faction. If you plan on playing with friends, ensure everyone coordinates their choice before creating their characters.

Inside the base, you will find your stash, vendors, and the helipad. The game uses a unique transport system where you must call in a helicopter (LZ) to move between the base and various Landing Zones across the island. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Killing members of your own faction is strictly prohibited. Team killers can be reported and may face severe penalties or faction-wide hostility.

## The Medical System and Survival

Unlike traditional shooters, health in this game is not a simple bar. You must manage blood levels, hydration, energy, and specific physical traumas. If you are hit, you need to check your health tab to identify the injury.

| Injury Type | Symptom | Required Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bleeding** | Constant blood loss (HP drop) | Bandages or Tourniquets |
| **Dizzy Effect** | Blurred vision/unsteadiness | **Blood Bag** |
| **Fracture** | Restricted movement/aim | Splint or Surgical Kit |
| **Pain/Headache** | Audible groaning/shaking | Painkillers |
| **Coughing** | Giving away position | Specific Medication |

To recover from the "Dizzy" state, simply bandaging isn't enough; you must replenish your blood volume. Additionally, keeping your hydration and energy levels high is vital for stamina regeneration during long treks through the jungle.

## Gear Management and Death Mechanics

Gray Zone Warfare is an extraction shooter, meaning if you die, you lose everything currently equipped on your person—unless it is stored in your "Safe Lock Box." This protected container is where you should keep your most valuable items, such as keys, rare meds, or high-value currency.

If you die, your body remains in the world for a limited time. You can attempt a "naked run" to recover your gear. However, if you die a second time before reaching your first body, the original corpse—and all the loot on it—will despawn permanently.

**Pro Tip for Squads:** If a teammate dies, you can right-click their body and "Drop All" items onto the ground. This prevents the loot from despawning even if your friend dies again on their way back to the site.

## Mastering Weaponry and Combat

The gunplay is highly realistic. You can check your magazine capacity by pressing **V**, or perform a full weapon inspection (checking chamber, fire mode, and zeroing) by holding **Left Alt + V**.

### Essential Combat Controls
*   **B:** Change Fire Mode (Single vs. Auto). Single shot is recommended for 2026 gameplay to conserve ammo and maintain accuracy.
*   **C + Scroll Wheel:** Adjust crouch height for looking over specific cover.
*   **L:** Toggle noise cancelling while on the helicopter to hear your squad better.
*   **X:** Go prone for maximum stability.

For beginners, scavenging AKs and SKSs from AI enemies in the starting towns is highly effective. These weapons are abundant, and their ammunition is easy to find, allowing you to save your high-end NATO gear for more dangerous missions.

## Vendor Secrets and Economy

Building reputation with vendors is the key to unlocking better gear. Each vendor has specific preferences for the loot they buy. Selling the right items to the right person will maximize your profit margins.

| Vendor | Preferred Loot Items | Reward for Progression |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Handshake** | Intel, Documents, Folders | Combat Gear & Uniforms |
| **Gunny** | Alcohol (Whiskey, Wine), Weapons | Ammo & Attachments |
| **Labrat** | Phones, Medical Supplies | High-tier Meds |
| **Artisan** | Credit Cards, Technical Tools | Specialized Weapon Parts |
| **Turncoat** | Watches, Necklaces, Valuables | Rare Black Market Items |

> **💡 Tip:** To quickly find compatible parts for a weapon, open the vendor screen and click on your gun in the stash. Every compatible attachment and ammo type will be highlighted in the vendor's inventory.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change my faction after starting the game?**

A: No, faction choice is locked until the next seasonal wipe. Choose carefully, especially if you plan to play with a specific group of friends.

**Q: How do I get a larger secure container?**

A: While higher editions of the game start with larger containers, you can earn a "bigger ass" (secure container) by progressing through specific high-level quest lines from the vendors.

**Q: Why is my character constantly coughing or dizzy?**

A: Coughing is usually a symptom of a chest injury or environmental factor, while dizziness indicates low blood volume. Refer to this **gray zone warfare starter guide 2026** medical table to identify the correct treatment, such as a blood bag for dizziness.

**Q: Do items in my backpack disappear if I die?**

A: Yes, all items in your backpack, pockets, and equipment slots are lost upon death. Only items inside your Safe Lock Box are protected. You must return to your corpse to retrieve lost gear.
