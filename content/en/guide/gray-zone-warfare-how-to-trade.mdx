## Understanding Vendor Trading and Reputation

The primary way to acquire gear in Gray Zone Warfare is through the various vendors located at your Base of Operations (Base). Each vendor specializes in different types of equipment, from medical supplies to high-end weapon modifications. Unlike traditional RPGs, trading here is a two-way street that directly impacts what you can buy in the future.

When considering **gray zone warfare how to trade** with NPCs, you must remember that buying items increases your loyalty level with that specific trader, whereas selling items generally does not. This creates a gameplay loop where you must spend money to unlock the right to spend even more money on better gear.

| Vendor Name | Specialization | Key Items Sold | Preferred Loot to Buy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gunny** | Firearms & Ammo | M4A1, Optics, Magazines | Weapon parts and standard ammo |
| **Artisan** | Tools & Repair | AK-series, Toolboxes, Mods | Industrial loot and mechanical parts |
| **Lab Rat** | Medical Supplies | Bandages, Splints, Surgery Kits | Medical injectors and first aid |
| **Handback** | Tactical Gear | Armor, Helmets, Rigs | Level 3A+ Armor and tactical vests |

> **💡 Tip:** To level up a vendor quickly, focus on buying items you use frequently, like ammunition and basic medical supplies. Selling your scavenged loot to the correct vendor is important for clearing space, but it won't help you reach that next loyalty tier.

## Player-to-Player Trading: Gifting and Sharing

As of 2026, Gray Zone Warfare does not feature a formal "Trade Window" between players. However, "trading" between squadmates is a cornerstone of the tactical experience. If you find a duplicate key or a high-value optic that your friend needs, the process is manual but effective.

To facilitate player trading, you simply need to drop the item from your inventory onto the ground. Your teammate can then pick it up. This is particularly vital for keys, which are often the biggest bottleneck in mission progression.

### Best Practices for Item Sharing
*   **Secure Containers:** Always keep high-value trade items like keys or rare medical injectors in your secure container (pouch). If you die during a "trade run," items in your secure container remain with you.
*   **Base Trading:** It is safest to trade items within the Base of Operations. Dropping gear in the field is risky due to AI respawns and potential enemy PMC interference.
*   **Key Rings:** Use a key ring inside your secure stash to maximize the number of keys you can carry for potential trades without risking them.

## Mission Spotlight: Tools of the Trade

Many players searching for **gray zone warfare how to trade** are actually looking for the specific Artisan mission titled "Tools of the Trade." This mission is a "fetch" quest that requires you to locate a specific toolbox and return it to the vendor to unlock her services.

### Walkthrough Steps

| Step | Objective | Location Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Accept Mission | Speak to **Artisan** at the Base of Operations. |
| **2** | Locate Construction Site | Head to the main town area (varies by faction). |
| **3** | Find the House | Locate the small house directly underneath the large construction site. |
| **4** | Retrieve Toolbox | Find the **Artisan's Toolbox** in the back-right corner of the house. |
| **5** | Secure & Extract | Place the toolbox in your **Secure Container** and call an LZ extraction. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** The construction site is a high-traffic area for AI. Ensure you clear the surrounding streets before attempting to enter the house, as AI can spawn behind you or right on top of your position.

## Maximizing Profit: The Economics of Looting

To trade effectively, you need capital. In Gray Zone Warfare, not all loot is created equal. Understanding the "Value per Slot" (VPS) is the difference between a wealthy PMC and a broke one. Carrying an entire scavenged rifle might seem lucrative, but it often takes up 8 to 10 inventory cells.

| Item Type | Cell Size | Avg. Value | Value Per Slot |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High-End Optics** | 1 | $100 - $150 | **$100+** |
| **Suppressors** | 1 | $80 - $120 | **$80+** |
| **Full Assault Rifle** | 10 | $300 | **$30** |
| **Standard Ammo Stack** | 1 | $10 | **$10** |

As shown in the table above, stripping attachments like the Voodoo optic or suppressors from enemy weapons is far more efficient than carrying the weapons themselves. You can fit multiple optics in the space of a single rifle, effectively tripling your profit per extraction.

## Advanced Inventory Management for Traders

Effective trading also requires a well-organized stash. Since your stash size is limited based on your game edition, you must use every trick in the book to maximize space for items you intend to trade or sell later.

1.  **Bag Stacking:** You can place smaller backpacks inside larger ones. This is the only way to "stack" containers and can significantly increase your storage capacity for future trade goods.
2.  **Medical Containers:** Use items like the MAAC (Medical Attachment and Container) inside your secure pouch. These take up one cell but provide two cells of space specifically for medical items.
3.  **The "Drop" Rule:** If you are about to crash or if a teammate dies, drop their gear on the ground rather than leaving it on the body. Items on the ground persist longer and are less likely to despawn if the player dies a second time while trying to recover their loot.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I trade items with players from a different faction?**

A: No, you can only team up and safely interact with players within your own faction. Attempting to trade with other factions in a PVP zone usually results in a firefight, as there is no formal neutral trade zone.

**Q: Does selling items to Artisan help me unlock better AK mods?**

A: While selling items to Artisan provides you with cash, it does not increase your Reputation level. To unlock better mods, you must complete her specific tasks, such as the "Tools of the Trade" mission, and buy items from her to increase the total money spent.

**Q: What is the best way to handle gray zone warfare how to trade for keys?**

A: The best way is to join a faction-specific Discord or use proximity chat at the Base. Many veteran players are willing to drop duplicate keys for newer players to help them progress through early-game bottlenecks.

**Q: Is there a penalty for dropping items for friends?**

A: There is no mechanical penalty, but you should always be aware of your surroundings. In PVP servers, other players can see you dropping items and may move in to steal the "trade" before your friend can pick it up. Always perform player trades inside the wire at your home base.
