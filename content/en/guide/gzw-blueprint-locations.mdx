## Midnight Sapphire: MCX and M700 Blueprints

Midnight Sapphire remains one of the most dangerous zones in the game due to the high density of elite AI guards. To secure the **gzw blueprint locations** here, players are advised to land at Landing Zone (LZ) Foxtrot 1. From there, move south toward the hotel facilities, skirting the golf course to avoid unnecessary patrols.

### The MCX Ghost Nomad
The MCX is a fan-favorite for close-quarters combat (CQC) due to its .300 Blackout chambering. To find it, enter the main Midnight Sapphire Hotel. Navigate to the ground floor and head toward the back right-hand side. The blueprint is located on a table in a lounge area.

### The M700 Bolt-Action
While you are at the hotel, you should grab the M700 blueprint. From the main lobby, take the stairs all the way to the rooftop. Head to the far right end of the roof. The blueprint is sitting out in the open, offering a great view of the surrounding valley—though you shouldn't stay exposed for long.

> **⚠️ Warning:** When interacting with blueprints, you must **double-tap** the interact key. The first press often only "inspects" the magazine or item; the second press actually adds it to your inventory.

## Fort Narith: DDM4 and AK308

Fort Narith is a sprawling military complex that requires a methodical approach. The **gzw blueprint locations** here are split between the living quarters and the high-security detention area.

### DDM4 (5.56 NATO)
The DDM4 is a versatile Western platform. Navigate to the coordinates **141 131**, which lead to the middle barrack building. Enter the structure, head up the stairs, and walk to the end of the hallway. The blueprint is placed clearly on a table. This area is often patrolled by armored AI, so bring armor-piercing (AP) rounds.

### AK308 (7.62x51)
The AK308 blueprint is unique because it is tied to the Vulture trader. Head to the jail section of Fort Narith at **142 130**. You will find a locked door at the back of the facility that can be kicked open. Once inside, look specifically under the coffee pot in the break area to find the document.

## Hunter's Paradise and Remote Locations

Not all **gzw blueprint locations** are found in major military hubs. Some are hidden in smaller settlements or recreational areas that require significant travel.

### M14 (Mini-14)
Located at Hunter's Paradise (**159 168**), this blueprint is found at the very back of the shooting range. Enter the last door on the left side of the range facility. The blueprint is resting on a workbench.

### MP7 (4.6x30mm)
The MP7 is located in a small, unnamed village at **188 130**. This is a "middle of nowhere" location, making it a quiet but long trek. Inside the main house of the village, check the area next to the couch and coffee table to secure this high-fire-rate PDW.

### Mossberg 590
For fans of the 12-gauge pump action, the Mossberg is located far southeast of the YBL-1 bunker at **149 111**. Look for a remote campsite with a small open-front shack. The blueprint is sitting on the table inside.

## Tiger Bay: The SVD Blueprint

Tiger Bay is arguably the most difficult zone in Gray Zone Warfare due to the sheer number of AI enemies and the complex urban layout. The SVD blueprint, a powerful semi-automatic sniper, is located inside the Tiger Bay Mall at **197 129**.

1.  Enter the Mall and locate the Bookstore.
2.  Move behind the main register counter.
3.  Go through the back office door.
4.  The blueprint is located on a storage shelf to your right.

> **💡 Tip:** Tiger Bay is best tackled in a squad. If playing solo, use suppressed weapons and move slowly through the storefronts to avoid being swarmed by the "mall guards."

## Essential Survival Mechanics for Blueprints

Finding the **gzw blueprint locations** is only half the battle. Extracting them safely is the real challenge. In the current 2026 build of the game, blueprints function as quest items that must be physically carried back to your base and handed over to the appropriate trader (usually Banshee, Gunny, or Vulture).

*   **Death Consequences:** If you die while carrying a blueprint in your backpack or pockets, the quest will fail. Unlike some keys, blueprints currently do not always stay in your "safe container" depending on the specific quest parameters.
*   **Bug Warning:** Some players have reported that if they die with a blueprint, the item does not immediately respawn in the world. It is rumored to have a 3-day (real-time or in-game) reset period, so treat every blueprint run as a "one-life" mission.
*   **Trader Hand-in:** Once you return to base, do not simply leave the blueprint in your locker. Open the Trader menu, navigate to the "Tasks" tab, and manually hand over the item to unlock the weapon skin or purchase option.

For more technical details on weapon modding once you've unlocked these frames, check out the [Official Gray Zone Warfare Steam Community](https://steamcommunity.com/app/2479810) for the latest patch notes and player-made ballistics charts.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a specific key to access any of the gzw blueprint locations?**

A: Most blueprints are in open areas or rooms that can be "kicked open," such as the AK308 room in Fort Narith. However, the SVD in Tiger Bay and the MCX in Midnight Sapphire require navigating through high-security areas where keys might make your approach safer, though they aren't strictly required for the blueprint rooms themselves.

**Q: Can I find these blueprints in PVE mode?**

A: Yes, all **gzw blueprint locations** are identical in both PVP and PVE modes. Many players prefer doing blueprint runs in PVE to avoid being camped at LZs by rival factions, especially in high-traffic areas like Midnight Sapphire.

**Q: Why can't I see the blueprint on the table even though I'm at the right coordinates?**

A: Blueprints are "instanced" per raid but can be picked up by other players. If another player in your session has already taken the blueprint, you may need to extract and start a new raid to find it again. Also, ensure you have the corresponding quest active from the trader, as some blueprints may not appear unless the task is tracked.

**Q: What is the best way to extract from Tiger Bay after getting the SVD?**

A: The safest extraction is usually heading back toward the coastal LZs. Avoid the main roads and move through the back alleys of the residential district. If you have a helicopter arriving, clear the LZ thoroughly, as AI in Tiger Bay are known to follow players all the way to the extraction point.
