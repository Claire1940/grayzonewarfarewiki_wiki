## Locating the Secret Vendor: Vulture

One of the most significant additions to the Lamang landscape is the secret vendor known as Vulture. Unlike standard base vendors such as Gunny or Handshake, Vulture does not start as an accessible option in your menu. He is a scavenger who moves between specific high-value locations on a weekly rotation. Finding him is the only way to unlock his services and gain access to his unique inventory of recovered PMC gear.

Vulture is always located at one of the "Major C.O.P.s" (Command Outposts) on the map. These are easily identifiable on your tactical map by the three black dots icon, rather than the single dot used for minor outposts.

| Major C.O.P. Name | Region | Tactical Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Harrison** | Central | High-density urban combat zone |
| **Titan** | Northern | Strategic vantage point |
| **Westmore** | Western | Coastal supply route |
| **Stalwart** | Southern | Jungle perimeter defense |
| **Crusader** | Eastern | Industrial hub access |
| **Nomad** | South-West | Remote extraction corridor |

> **💡 Tip:** Vulture's location resets every week. If you cannot find him at your usual spot, check the other Major C.O.P.s or consult with your faction members to see where he has moved.

To interact with Vulture, your faction must currently control the Major C.O.P. where he is residing. This requires clearing out any hostile AI or rival PMCs and interacting with the main computer terminal. If the terminal light is yellow, the zone is ready for capture. Once captured, Vulture will appear in your vendor list, allowing you to browse his wares.

## Vulture’s Inventory and Benefits

Vulture serves a unique role in the Lamang economy. He scavenges equipment left behind by fallen PMCs—weapons, armor, and backpacks that were not successfully extracted. This makes him an invaluable resource for low-level players who may not have high-level reputation with standard vendors yet.

For example, a level 1 player might find an M14 or high-tier body armor in Vulture's shop that would normally require Level 4 loyalty with Banshee. The gear sold by Vulture is typically repaired to 95% or 100% durability, ensuring it is combat-ready.

| Item Category | Potential Loot | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapons** | M14, Modified AKs, Snipers | Access high-tier firepower early |
| **Protection** | Tier 3+ Plates, High-end Helmets | Increased survivability for new players |
| **Storage** | Large Backpacks, Rigs | Enhanced looting capacity per run |
| **Special** | Small Safe, Large Safe | Massive stash organization and security |

As you increase your reputation with Vulture, his inventory shifts from basic gear to high-end storage solutions. At maximum level, he sells the Large Safe, a 108-slot container designed to hold currency and high-value items, significantly cleaning up your stash.

## How to Level Up Vulture Reputation

Unlike other vendors who offer specific contracts or missions, Vulture is leveled primarily through trade. However, he is very picky about what he buys. He generally ignores standard weaponry and basic supplies, preferring high-value "loot" items and military-grade equipment found in high-tier zones like Midnight Sapphire.

1. **Farm High-Value Areas:** Target areas like the villas in Midnight Sapphire or the administrative buildings in Lang.
2. **Collect Military Equipment:** Look for specialized military items found on elite guards or bosses.
3. **Sell in Bulk:** Selling approximately 70,000 worth of items to Vulture will grant roughly 700 reputation points.
4. **Focus on Quality:** A single high-tier military item (worth 60k to other vendors) might sell for 30k to Vulture but provide a massive boost to his specific reputation track.

> **Warning:** Vulture often pays less (sometimes 50% less) for items than other vendors. You are essentially "paying" for the reputation gain rather than maximizing profit.

## Curing Nausea and Dizziness

Survival in the grey zone isn't just about winning gunfights; it's about managing your operator's physical condition. Two common and debilitating status effects are nausea and dizziness. These often occur after taking significant trauma to the head or torso, or as a result of severe blood loss.

To cure these conditions, you must use specialized medical kits. Standard bandages or painkillers will not suffice.

| Medical Item | Sources | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sur Kit** | Lab Rat, Enemy Loot | Repairs internal organ damage |
| **Searcher Kit** | Lab Rat, World Spawns | Advanced surgical repairs |

When your screen begins to blur or sway (dizziness) or your character begins to make audible retching sounds (nausea), open your health menu immediately. Identify the damaged blacked-out limbs or organs. Using a Sur Kit or Searcher Kit on these specific areas will remove the status effect. In some cases, if the injuries are extensive, you may need to use two kits to fully stabilize your operator.

## Tactical Tips for Mastering the Grey Zone

Success in Lamang requires a blend of patience and aggression. As part of our **grey zone wiki** philosophy, we recommend the following tactical approaches for 2026:

*   **Check the Terminal:** Before attempting to find Vulture, always check the map to see which faction holds the Major C.O.P. If it's contested, prepare for a fight.
*   **Inventory Management:** Always keep at least one Sur Kit in your secure container. Losing your medical supplies in the field can turn a minor dizziness spell into a fatal extraction failure.
*   **The Safe Strategy:** Once you unlock the Large Safe from Vulture, use it to store all your physical currency. This frees up dozens of slots in your main stash that would otherwise be taken up by wallets.
*   **Scavenge Smart:** When visiting Vulture, look for backpacks first. A larger backpack allows you to carry more high-value loot back to base, which you can then sell back to him to level up his reputation faster.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is Vulture located in the grey zone wiki?**

A: Vulture is found at one of the Major Command Outposts (C.O.P.s) marked with three black dots on the map. He rotates weekly between locations like Harrison, Titan, and Westmore.

**Q: Why can't I see Vulture in my vendor list?**

A: Vulture only appears in your vendor list once you have physically visited him at his current location and your faction has captured that specific Command Outpost.

**Q: How do I fix the blurred screen effect (Dizziness)?**

A: Dizziness is usually caused by internal injuries. You need to use a Sur Kit or a Searcher Kit on the damaged body part (often the head or chest) to clear the effect.

**Q: Is it worth leveling Vulture to max rank?**

A: Yes. While he pays less for items, his max-rank rewards—the Small and Large Safes—are the best storage solutions in the game for managing high-value loot and money.
