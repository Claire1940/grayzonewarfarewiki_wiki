## Advanced Ballistics and Combat Tuning

One of the most significant hurdles for players in the current 0.4 environment is the perceived inconsistency of headshots against AI. While many players attribute missed shots to "height over bore"—the physical distance between your scope's optical axis and the rifle's barrel—there are deeper mechanical issues at play. Currently, zeroing settings may reset when toggling your Aim Down Sights (ADS), and hitboxes for the neck are often categorized as part of the thorax rather than the head. 

To compensate for these factors, experienced operators recommend aiming slightly higher than the intended point of impact, often targeting the very top of an enemy's helmet. Additionally, using longer barrels increases muzzle velocity, resulting in a flatter trajectory that minimizes the "arc" of the bullet, making your shots more predictable at medium ranges.

| Combat Mechanic | Impact on Gameplay | Recommended Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| **Zeroing Reset** | Shots land lower than expected after re-aiming. | Aim for the top of the helmet at 50-100m. |
| **Neck Hitbox** | Non-fatal shots to the throat area. | Focus on the upper cranium to ensure a kill. |
| **Barrel Length** | Shorter barrels have more "looping" arcs. | Use long barrels for flatter trajectories. |
| **Height Over Bore** | Point of impact is lower than reticle at close range. | Aim high when targets are under 25 meters. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not rely solely on the Elcan or other 100m-zeroed scopes for precision neck shots; the current modeling of the spine means missing the head often results in a non-fatal thorax wound.

## Transportation and Extraction Tactics

The helicopter system remains the lifeblood of Lamang. In 2026, the logistics of calling a "hilo" have been streamlined, but many players still waste valuable time waiting at Landing Zones (LZs). To optimize your extraction, call your helicopter when you are approximately 100 meters away from the LZ. This ensures the timer is synchronized with your arrival, reducing the time you spend exposed in the open.

Furthermore, the 0.4 update introduced several quality-of-life features for air travel. If you are the only passenger, you can hold the **L key** to expedite the takeoff process, which is critical when under fire.

| Helicopter Feature | Command/Hotkey | Tactical Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Expedite Takeoff** | Hold **L** | Reduces time spent sitting still at the LZ. |
| **Silence Headset** | Press **O** | Mutes the deafening engine noise (Requires Active Headset). |
| **Invulnerability** | Automatic | 5-second protection upon landing; total protection while in flight. |
| **Call Distance** | ~100 Meters | Minimizes wait time and exposure to LZ campers. |

## Trader Reputation and the Secret Vendor

Progressing through the various factions requires more than just completing tasks. While missions are the primary source of reputation, you can also gain standing by selling high-value items to specific traders. The reputation gain is calculated as the **Sale Amount divided by 100**. For example, selling a 60,000-credit item will net you 600 reputation points. This is particularly useful for leveling up Artisan, Turncoat, and Banshee, who often have fewer early-game missions than Gunny or Handshake.

The most mysterious figure in the Lamang economy is **Vulture**, the secret vendor. Vulture does not stay in one place; he rotates between two of the seven major Command Outposts (COPs) every week. Finding him is essential for high-level players, as his Level 4 inventory includes a massive 108-slot safe for your inventory.

### How to Find Vulture
Because Vulture's location changes weekly, the community relies on external tracking tools. You can check his current coordinates at various community-run sites or by scouting the major COPs (marked with triple-star triangles on your map).

| Trader | Primary Benefit | Reputation Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Banshee** | Binoculars and Scopes | Sell high-value optics and electronics. |
| **Vulture** | 108-Slot Inventory Safe | Sell watches and rare jewelry. |
| **Gunny** | M4 Parts and 5.56 Ammo | Complete early-game starter town tasks. |
| **Artisan** | AK Modifications | Sell captured 7.62 weapons and parts. |

## Essential Equipment: Binoculars and Lockboxes

The introduction of binoculars has changed the scouting meta within the **gray zone warfare wiki**. Accessed via the **9 key**, binoculars allow for long-range reconnaissance without the arm stamina drain associated with aiming a high-magnification rifle scope. The basic set is available from Banshee early on, while his Level 4 set includes an integrated rangefinder—a must-have for snipers looking to calibrate their holdovers.

Equally important for survival is the Lockbox. This persistent storage slot ensures that any items placed inside—such as quest items, wallets, or rare keys—remain with your PMC even after death.

## Enemy Factions and Ammo Matching

Lamang is populated by different AI factions, each favoring specific calibers. A key strategy for long deployments is to match your weapon choice to the local enemy types so you can scavenge ammunition in the field. This is especially effective in PvE, where you can sustain your ammo count indefinitely by looting downed hostiles.

1.  **Sawmill/Rural Areas:** Enemies typically carry AK-platform rifles using 5.45x39mm (PS) or 7.62x39mm.
2.  **Hunter's Paradise:** Hostiles often utilize shotguns or MP5s with Extreme Penetrator rounds, which are surprisingly effective against mid-tier armor.
3.  **Lang Armed Forces (LAF) Bases:** These professional soldiers use 5.56x45mm (M855). Bringing an M4 or Daniel Defense rifle here allows you to stay topped off on high-quality NATO rounds.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the secret vendor Vulture?**

A: Vulture is unlocked automatically, but his physical presence is restricted to two rotating major Command Outposts (COPs) each week. You must physically travel to these triple-star locations to interact with him.

**Q: What is the best way to earn reputation quickly?**

A: While tasks are great, selling high-value items is often faster. Every 100 credits in sales equals 1 reputation point. Selling a single 60k item can grant as much reputation as several early-game missions.

**Q: Why are my headshots not killing AI in one hit?**

A: This is often due to a combination of zeroing bugs and the neck hitbox being tied to the thorax. To ensure a kill according to the current **gray zone warfare wiki** standards, aim for the very top of the enemy's head or helmet to account for the bullet landing slightly lower than the reticle.

**Q: How do I use the new binoculars?**

A: You must first purchase them from Banshee. Once equipped in your PMC slot, press the **9 key** to pull them out. They do not consume arm stamina, making them superior to scopes for long-term scouting.
