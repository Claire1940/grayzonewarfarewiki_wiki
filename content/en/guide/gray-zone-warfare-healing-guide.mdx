Surviving the unforgiving jungles of Lamang requires more than just a steady aim and tactical positioning. ## Understanding the Health UI and Vitals

Before you can treat a wound, you must understand what your body is telling you. By pressing the **Tab** key and navigating to the health tab, you are presented with a detailed anatomical diagram of your operator. This screen is your primary source of information during and after a firefight.

### The Three Pillars of Survival

1.  **Blood Volume:** This is essentially your "HP" bar, measured in liters. A healthy adult has around 5.0L to 6.0L. If this drops below approximately 2.0L, your character will slip into a coma. Blood loss causes visual effects like redness and extreme blurriness.
2.  **Hydration:** Water and energy drinks are vital for maintaining stamina. Dehydration leads to dizziness and fatigue, making it harder to aim or sprint to cover.
3.  **Energy/Food:** Similar to hydration, keeping your energy levels high ensures your character doesn't suffer from exhaustion during long treks across the map.

| Vital Metric | Normal Level | Coma/Danger Threshold | Recovery Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blood Volume** | 5.0L - 6.0L | **< 2.0L** | Natural regeneration or Blood Bags |
| **Hydration** | 100% | **< 10%** | Water, Sodas, Energy Drinks |
| **Energy** | 100% | **< 10%** | MREs, Canned Food, Snacks |

> **💡 Tip:** Blood does regenerate naturally over time in Gray Zone Warfare, but it is a very slow process. If you are significantly low, using a blood bag is the only way to quickly return to combat effectiveness.

## Essential Medical Items and Their Uses

Your tactical rig and pockets should always be stocked with specific medical supplies. Items placed in slots marked with a **lightning bolt icon** can be used via hotkeys (holding 'H' by default), which is critical when you are under fire.

| Item Name | Primary Use | Animation Speed | Recommended Quantity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tourniquet (CT)** | Stops **Heavy Bleeding** immediately | Very Fast | 2-3 |
| **Bandage / Dressing** | Seals light bleeds and minor wounds | Moderate | 4-6 |
| **Splint** | Repairs **Fractured Bones** (Yellow icons) | Moderate | 1-2 |

| **Blood Bag (S/L)** | Restores 0.5L to 1.0L of blood volume | Moderate | 1 (Secure Container) |
| **Painkillers** | Removes pain effects and blurriness | Fast | 1-2 |

## Treating Different Types of Injuries

Injuries in Gray Zone Warfare are categorized by their severity and the type of medical intervention required. Following a strict order of operations can save your life when multiple injuries occur simultaneously.

### 1. Bleeding (The Immediate Threat)
Bleeding is indicated by blood drop icons on your UI. 
*   **Light Bleeds (One Drop):** These are slow and won't kill you instantly. You can usually finish a short engagement before needing a bandage.
*   **Heavy Bleeds (Two+ Drops):** These are lethal. Your blood volume will plummet, leading to a coma within seconds. You must apply a **Tourniquet** immediately to the affected limb.

### 2. Fractures
Represented by yellow bone icons on the health screen. A fractured leg will severely limit your movement speed and prevent sprinting, while a fractured arm causes massive weapon sway, making it nearly impossible to land long-range shots. Use a **Splint** to fully recover functionality.

### 3. Organ Damage
If you see yellow or orange icons in the center of your character's torso, you have sustained organ damage. This often results in persistent coughing (which alerts enemies to your position) or dizziness. Only a **Surgery Kit** can repair these. 

> **Warning:** Surgery is a long, stationary process. Never attempt surgery in the middle of a street or an unsecured building. Have a teammate pull security while you operate.

## Advanced Healing: Surgery and Blood Transfusions

As you progress and gain access to better traders, you will find more advanced medical gear. The **Surgery Kit** comes in two main variants: the "Suck Kit" (3 uses) and the "Sur Kit" (5 uses). These are essential for long raids where you might take multiple non-lethal hits to the chest or stomach.

Blood bags are equally important. While blood regenerates, a "Miserable" state (low blood) makes your screen black and white and incredibly blurry. Carrying a small blood bag in your **Secure Container** is a common pro strategy in 2026. This ensures that even if you lose your main rig, you have the tools to restore your vision and stamina after a rough fight.

## Reviving Teammates and Managing Comas

One of the most unique aspects of the **gray zone warfare healing guide** is the coma mechanic. If a teammate's blood volume drops too low or they take a non-lethal but incapacitating hit, they enter a coma. They aren't dead, but they are completely helpless.

### How to Save a Fallen Squadmate
1.  **Drag to Cover:** Approach the body and use the interaction prompt to drag them. Healing in the open is a death sentence for both of you.
2.  **Check POV Symbols:** The downed player will see icons representing their needs (Droplet for bleeding, Stomach for surgery). They should communicate this via VOIP.
3.  **Apply Medicals:** You must have the medical items in your **Rig or Pockets** (lightning bolt slots) to use them on an ally. You cannot use items directly from your backpack on someone else.
4.  **Stabilize:** Stop bleeds first, then use a surgery kit or blood bag to bring them back to consciousness.

| Coma Icon | Meaning | Required Item |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blood Droplet** | Active Bleeding | Tourniquet or Bandage |
| **Stomach Icon** | Critical Organ Damage | Surgery Kit |
| **Brain Icon** | Extreme Low Blood | Blood Bag |
| **Bone Icon** | Severe Fracture | Splint |

## Pro Tips for Combat Survival

*   **Cancel Animations:** If an enemy pushes you while you are bandaging, **Right-Click** to immediately cancel the animation and raise your weapon. It is better to bleed a little more than to die with a bandage in your hand.
*   **Pre-Medicating:** If you know a breach is coming, taking painkillers can prevent the "Pain" effect (blurriness) from hitting you mid-fight if you take a non-lethal shot.
*   **The AI Loot Loop:** Most AI enemies carry basic bandages and food. If you are running low on supplies, check the pockets of downed scavengers to restock your basic medical needs.
*   **Secure Container Management:** Always keep your most expensive and "multi-use" items (like the 5-use Surgery Kit and a Large Blood Bag) in your secure container. Bandages and tourniquets are cheap and should stay in your rig for quick hotkey access.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I heal myself while moving in Gray Zone Warfare?**

A: No. Most medical actions require you to be stationary. Attempting to move will often cancel the animation. Always find hard cover before starting the healing process.

**Q: Why can't I use a surgery kit on my teammate?**

A: Ensure the surgery kit is in your chest rig, tactical belt, or pockets (the slots with the lightning bolt). You cannot perform medical actions on others using items stored inside your backpack.

**Q: How long does it take for blood to regenerate naturally?**

A: Natural regeneration is very slow and depends on your hydration and energy levels. It can take upwards of 10-15 minutes of real-time to recover a significant amount of blood without a transfusion.

**Q: Is there a way to fix a "Blacked Out" limb permanently?**

A: In this **gray zone warfare healing guide**, "blacked out" usually refers to severe organ damage or multiple fractures. Use a surgery kit to restore the organ's functionality and a splint for the bones. Unlike some other shooters, limbs do not "disappear," but they become useless until surgically repaired.
