## Understanding the Blueprint System

Blueprints in Gray Zone Warfare serve as essential quest items and unlockables. Once found, they must be safely extracted and turned in to specific traders. However, there are several nuances to the system that 2026 players must keep in mind to avoid frustration.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Blueprints are currently known to be "one-time" items. If you pick one up and die before extracting, the quest may fail or the item may not respawn immediately due to persistence bugs. Always prioritize immediate extraction after finding a blueprint.

When you locate a blueprint, you may notice that the initial interaction doesn't immediately put it in your inventory. This is a common point of confusion. You often have to "double-tap" the interact button; the first interaction usually "inspects" the item (like checking a magazine), while the second actually picks it up.

### Blueprint Extraction Checklist
*   **Secure Container:** If you have space, place the blueprint in your secure container immediately.
*   **Squad Support:** Do not hunt for blueprints solo in high-threat areas like Midnight Sapphire or Tiger Bay.
*   **Trader Turn-in:** Return to base and interact with the specific vendor (Gunny, Artisan, or Vulture) to complete the unlock.

## Detailed Guide to Assault Rifle Blueprints

Assault rifles are the backbone of any PMC's loadout. Tracking down these **grayzone blueprint locations all** requires venturing into some of the most heavily guarded military installations on the map.

### MCX (.300 Blackout)
The MCX is a fan favorite for suppressed, high-intensity builds. To find it, head to the Midnight Sapphire Hotel (171 166). Navigate to the ground floor and head toward the back right-hand side of the main building. It is tucked away in a service area. This weapon excels in close-quarters combat (CQC) due to its high fire rate and hard-hitting .300 Blackout rounds.

### DDM4 (5.56x45 NATO)
The DDM4 is a premium AR-15 platform. You will find this blueprint at Fort Narith (141 131). Locate the middle barracks building and head to the second floor. Walk all the way down the hallway to the final room; the blueprint is sitting clearly on a wooden table. Expect heavy resistance from well-armored AI in this sector.

### AK&M (7.62x39)
For those who prefer the reliability of the AK platform, the AK&M is located at 195 153. Look for a raised red shack in the clearing. Inside, the blueprint is hidden on the floor next to a pillow. This weapon is excellent for mid-range engagements where armor penetration is a priority.

## Long-Range Precision Blueprints

Sniping is a core component of Gray Zone Warfare's tactical gameplay. Finding these **grayzone blueprint locations all** will allow you to engage enemies from hundreds of meters away, often before they even register your presence.

### M700 Bolt-Action
While you can find basic M700s in starting town loot boxes, the blueprint for the high-end version is located on the rooftop of the Midnight Sapphire Hotel. You must take the main stairs all the way to the top. Once on the roof, run to the far right end (opposite the stairs). The blueprint is resting on a ledge overlooking the grounds.

### SVD (7.62x54R)
The SVD is arguably the best semi-auto sniper in the game. Obtaining it is a rite of passage, as it requires entering the Tiger Bay Mall (197 129). This area is notoriously difficult due to the sheer density of AI. Look for the bookstore within the mall. Go behind the checkout register, through the back door, and turn right. The blueprint is on a metal shelf.

### M14 / Mini-14
Located at Hunter's Paradise (159 168), this blueprint is found at the very end of the shooting range. Enter the last door on the left-hand side of the range area. The schematic is sitting on a workbench. This is a great mid-tier DMR for players transitioning out of the early game.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to find the blueprint every time I want to buy a gun?**

A: No. Once you have successfully extracted a blueprint and turned it in to the appropriate trader, that weapon (or specific variant) becomes permanently available for purchase, provided you have the required trader reputation level.

**Q: I went to the coordinates for the MP7, but the blueprint wasn't there. Why?**

A: If another player in your session has recently picked up the blueprint, it may take some time to respawn. Additionally, ensure you are checking the exact spot (on the couch) as some items have very small interaction hitboxes.

**Q: Can I lose the blueprint after I turn it in?**

A: No. Once the quest is completed with the trader, the unlock is permanent for your character's current progression. You will not lose the ability to buy the weapon even if you die in subsequent raids.

**Q: Are there grayzone blueprint locations all over the map for every single gun?**

A: Not every gun has a dedicated blueprint. Many weapons, like the M4A1 or basic AK-74, are unlocked simply by leveling up your traders (Gunny and Artisan) through standard missions and reputation gains. The blueprints are reserved for "specialty" or high-tier variants.
