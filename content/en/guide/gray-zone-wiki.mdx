## Understanding the Lockbox System

One of the most critical components of your loadout in Gray Zone Warfare is the Lockbox. This specialized container is designed to protect your most valuable assets from being looted upon death. Unlike your backpack, rig, or pockets, any item placed inside the Lockbox remains in your inventory even if you are KIA (Killed in Action). This makes it the primary tool for securing high-value currency, rare keys, and essential quest items.

When you first begin your career, you are typically issued a basic "Safe Lock" box. However, as you progress through the ranks and complete specific milestones, you can acquire larger versions that offer more grid space.

| Lockbox Tier | Grid Size | Acquisition Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Safe Lock** | 2x2 (4 Slots) | Standard Starting Equipment |
| **Elite Folder** | 2x3 (6 Slots) | Mid-tier Quest Reward / Supporter Edition |
| **Tactical Case** | 3x3 (9 Slots) | End-game Faction Milestone |

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize placing your Wallet, Medkit, and unique quest items like intel folders or encrypted USB drives inside your Lockbox before leaving the Forward Operating Base (FOB).

## Quest Walkthrough: Paranoia Agent

The "Paranoia Agent" task is a pivotal mission for players operating within the Crimson Shield International faction. This quest emphasizes the investigative nature of the game, requiring you to track down leads and confirm the status of missing personnel. Following the correct sequence is vital, as the objective updates dynamically based on your interaction with the environment.

### Step 1: Locating the Intel
Your first objective is to locate a specific building within the designated mission area. Once inside, you must search for a workstation featuring a computer. On this computer, you will find a note that provides the necessary context for the disappearance you are investigating. Reading this note is mandatory to trigger the next phase of the quest.

### Step 2: The Investigation
After exiting the item menu from the computer, your map will update with a secondary location. It is highly recommended to mark a waypoint on your compass to maintain orientation in the dense environment. You will need to move to a nearby two-story building.

### Step 3: Body Inspection
Enter the two-story structure and head to the upper floor. Move to the right-hand corner of the room to find the deceased subject. Inspecting the body completes the investigation phase, allowing you to return to the FOB and claim your rewards.

| Objective Stage | Location Type | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1** | Office Building | **Read Note** on Computer |
| **Phase 2** | Two-Story House | **Inspect Body** upstairs |
| **Phase 3** | Base Camp | **Turn in Task** to Vendor |

## Tactical Gear and Loadout Management

A successful deployment starts in the stash. In Gray Zone Warfare, gear is categorized by its weight, protection level, and ergonomic impact. While it may be tempting to always run the heaviest armor available, the stamina drain and noise generation can be detrimental during long-range reconnaissance. This **gray zone wiki** recommends balancing your protection based on the expected engagement distance.

1. **Light Recon:** Focuses on high mobility. Use Level II or III armor with a suppressed SKS or M4A1. Ideal for solo players.
2. **Heavy Assault:** Utilizes Level IV plates and high-capacity magazines. Best suited for squad-based operations in urban centers.
3. **Designated Marksman:** Prioritizes optics and long-range ballistics. Requires a heavy investment in high-tier ammunition.

## Medical Systems and Field Recovery

The medical system in 2026 is highly granular. Players do not just have a "health bar"; instead, they must manage blood loss, organ damage, and bone fractures. Using the wrong medical item can lead to wasted resources or, worse, a slow death in the field.

| Injury Type | Symptom | Recommended Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light Bleeding** | Slow Health Drain | **Bandage** (Sterile) |
| **Heavy Bleeding** | Rapid Blood Loss | **Tourniquet** / QuickClot |
| **Fracture** | Restricted Movement | **Splint** / Surgical Kit |
| **Organ Damage** | Coughing / Pain | **Painkillers** & Extraction |

> **Warning:** Using a tourniquet will stop heavy bleeding immediately, but it may cause long-term limb damage if not replaced with a permanent dressing once you reach a safe zone.

*   **Mithras Security Group:** A traditional PMC focused on dominance and resource extraction. Their quests are often combat-heavy.

## Survival Strategies for Lamang Island

To truly master the environment, you must look beyond the basic tutorials. The AI in Gray Zone Warfare is designed to flank and suppress players who stay in one position for too long. 

*   **Relocate After Firing:** If you take a shot and don't secure the kill, assume your position is compromised. Move at least 20-30 meters to a new angle.
*   **Manage Your Hydration:** The tropical climate of Lamang Island causes rapid dehydration. Always carry at least two bottles of water for any raid lasting longer than 20 minutes.
*   **Listen to the Environment:** Audio cues are your best friend. The sound of rustling bushes or distant gunfire can tell you exactly where an enemy squad is moving.

By consistently utilizing this **gray zone wiki**, you can stay ahead of the meta and ensure that your PMC remains a dominant force on the island. Tactical patience and knowledge are the most powerful weapons in your arsenal.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I upgrade my Lockbox in Gray Zone Warfare?**

A: You can upgrade your Lockbox by completing high-level trader quests or by purchasing higher-tier editions of the game. Some seasonal events in 2026 may also offer temporary or permanent storage expansions.

**Q: What happens if I forget to put a quest item in my Lockbox?**

A: If you die with a quest item in your pockets or backpack, it will be dropped with your body. You will need to return to your corpse to retrieve it, or in some cases, restart the quest objective entirely. Consulting a **gray zone wiki** before starting a mission can help you identify which items are most critical to protect.

**Q: Can other players steal the contents of my Lockbox?**

A: No. The Lockbox is a secure container that cannot be looted by other players. Only the items in your external inventory (vest, backpack, pockets) are at risk when you are defeated in the field.

**Q: Where can I find the body for the Paranoia Agent quest?**

A: The body is located on the second floor of a building near the initial intel site. Look for a two-story structure and head to the upper-right corner room to find the objective.
