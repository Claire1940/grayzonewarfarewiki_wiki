## Initial Setup and The First Steps

When you first spawn into the base camp in 2026, you are presented with the "First Steps" tutorial. While veterans might be tempted to skip it, new players should spend the 5 to 8 minutes completing it to familiarize themselves with the updated 0.4 control scheme. 

If you have purchased special editions, such as the Supporter Edition, your first stop should be the vendor **Handshake**. You can claim your DLC items immediately. However, for the purposes of this guide, we will focus on a standard equipment path that any player can follow regardless of their purchase tier.

### Inventory Management
Before heading out, use the "Sort Items" function in your locker. Organization is key in Gray Zone Warfare. Categorize your meds, ammunition, and gear so you can re-kit quickly after a lost deployment. 

> **💡 Tip:** Every time your character wipes or you manually reset, your DLC and Supporter items are returned to your mailbox. Don't be afraid to use them, but prioritize learning the basic gear first.

One of the biggest mistakes new players make is bringing high-end M4s with SP (Soft Point) rounds into the starter zones. SP rounds struggle against even basic armor, and the M4 is expensive to replace. In the current 2026 meta, the **M870 Shotgun** is the king of the early game.

| Item | Vendor | Cost (Credits) | Why it works |
|---|---|---|---|
| **M870 Shotgun** | Gunny | 1,200 | Reliable one-tap potential on AI |
| **Buckshot Shells** | Gunny | 200 | Extremely cheap and effective |

The M870 is underrated because it requires precision, but it consistently one-shots AI in the starting town when aiming for center mass. It also saves significant inventory space since you don't need to carry multiple 30-round magazines.

## Essential Systems in Gray Zone Warfare New Player Guide 2026

Survival in Lamang isn't just about winning gunfights; it is about managing your physical condition. The medical system in Gray Zone Warfare is modular, meaning different items fix specific types of trauma.

### The Medical Kit Breakdown
You should always carry a "Hard Case" from **Lab Rat**. This item occupies two slots in your secure container but provides four internal slots, allowing you to protect expensive medical supplies even if you die.

| Medical Item | Use Case | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Surgery Kit** | Heals damaged organs (Yellow/Orange status) | High |
| **Blood Bag** | Restores overall blood volume to prevent coma | Medium |
| **CT (Tourniquet)** | Stops Heavy Bleeding immediately | Critical |
| **Bandage** | Fixes Medium Wounds | High |
| **Painkillers** | Removes vision blur and stops injury moaning | Medium |
| **Splint** | Repairs fractured limbs | Low |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Light bleeds and bruises do not require medical intervention. They will naturally heal within 2-3 minutes. Save your bandages for Medium and Heavy wounds to preserve resources.

## Tactical Navigation and Extraction

Movement in Gray Zone Warfare is governed by the Landing Zone (LZ) system. To start your first mission, you must call in a transport helicopter via the map (M).

1. **Call the Heli:** Open the map, select your desired LZ (e.g., Charlie 1), and request transport.
2. **Check the Color:** On your mini-map, your helicopter will be **Green**. Blue helicopters belong to other players in your faction.
3. **The Mute Toggle:** Helicopters are incredibly loud. In the 0.4 update, you can press the **'O' key** to dampen the audio during the flight, allowing you to hear your teammates or simply protect your ears.

### Waypoints and Compass
Once you land, don't run blindly. Use the map to right-click your objective and set a waypoint. This will appear on your compass at the top of the screen, ensuring you stay on track while moving through dense foliage. Always move between cover (trees, walls, or ditches) to avoid being picked off by patrolling AI.

## Combat Efficiency and Looting

When engaging enemies, especially with the shotgun, patience is your best friend. Wait for the AI to turn their back or enter a reload animation before peeking.

### The "Double-Click" Looting Method
In a tactical shooter, spending too much time standing over a body is a death sentence. To loot efficiently:
- Press **F** to open the inventory.
- **Double-click** items to quickly peek inside containers or pockets.
- Use **CTRL + Left Click** to instantly move items or stack money.
- Press **Tab** or **Escape** to exit the loot screen immediately.

### Boss Hunting and Keys
In the starting towns, look for "Boss" AI. They often wear unique clothing or carry engraved weapons, such as the **Sisle Pistol**. These bosses frequently drop high-value keys required for later missions. If you find a key, immediately move it to your secure container (Safe Pocket).

## Capturing Command Posts (COPs)

Command Posts are vital strategic locations that act as forward operating bases. Capturing them unlocks new vendors and provides safe zones for your faction.

| COP Status | Meaning | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Gray** | Neutral / Unclaimed | Defended by local AI; clear them to capture. |
| **Red** | Enemy Faction Owned | High-risk area; must be cleared of enemy players/AI. |
| **Blue** | Faction Owned | Safe zone with guards; use for resupply or task turn-in. |

Capturing a COP like **Artis** is a major milestone for new players. Once captured, you unlock the Artisan vendor, who provides specialized gear and advanced weaponry. When capturing a COP, be prepared for a counter-attack from local AI. Use your painkillers beforehand to ensure your vision remains clear even if you take a stray hit.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens if I die while a mission is active?**

A: If you accept a mission and die before completing it, there is a possibility the mission will be temporarily locked or reset. It is best to only accept missions you intend to complete in that specific deployment to avoid losing progress.

**Q: How do I stop the "dizzy" vision effect?**

A: Dizziness is usually caused by low blood volume. Use a Blood Bag to restore your levels to at least 5,000 units. If you are also in pain, stack two painkillers to achieve 100% pain relief for 300 seconds.

**Q: Is the M870 shotgun viable in late-game areas?**

A: While this **gray zone warfare new player guide 2026** recommends the shotgun for the early and medium tiers, it can be used in endgame POIs if you use high-tier slugs and focus on headshots. However, most players transition to suppressed assault rifles for high-tier stealth operations.

**Q: Can I share loot with players from other factions?**

A: In Warfare mode, players from other factions are hostile. In Joint Operations, you can drop items for any player, but there is no formal trading system. Communication via VOIP is the best way to coordinate loot sharing.
