## Unlocking the Secret Vendor: Vulture

In the 0.4 update, the vendor roster expanded to include a "secret" scavenger named Vulture. Unlike Handshake or Gunny, Vulture does not stay at your base. He is a nomadic trader who moves between Major Command Posts (COPs) on a weekly rotation.

### How to Find Vulture
Vulture can be found at any Major COP marked with three black dots on your map. These locations include:
*   **Harrison**
*   **Titan**
*   **Westmore**
*   **Stalwart**
*   **Crusader**
*   **Bronco**
*   **Winchester**
*   **Nomad**

To access his shop, your faction must currently control the COP where he is stationed. You can verify control by checking the main computer at the COP; if the screen is yellow, it is captured and Vulture will be accessible in your vendor menu.

### Vulture’s Inventory and Benefits
Vulture is unique because he resells gear scavenged from dead PMCs. This means his inventory is server-dependent. If a high-level player died with a kitted M14 or a large backpack and no one looted it, Vulture might have it for sale at a fully repaired state (95-100% durability). This is an excellent way for new players to bypass level requirements for advanced gear.

| Item Type | Requirement | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PMC Scavenged Gear** | Level 1 | Access to high-tier guns/armor early in the wipe. |
| **Small Safe** | Level 3 | Secure storage for money and high-value loot. |
| **Large Safe** | Level 4 | 108-slot container for massive wealth storage. |

## How to Level Up Vulture Reputation
Standard missions do not typically grant reputation for Vulture. Instead, you must engage in a specific trade economy. While most vendors buy specific categories (Gunny buys guns, Lab Rat buys meds), Vulture is interested in military-grade equipment and high-value "junk" loot.

1.  **High-Value Loot:** Items like watches, jewelry, and rare electronics found in villas or safes.
2.  **Military Equipment:** Specialized gear found on elite guards in areas like Midnight Sapphire.
3.  **The "Big Ticket" Item:** There is a rare military item worth approximately 60,000 currency found near the boss at the top of Midnight Sapphire. Selling this to Vulture (even at his 50% buy rate) provides a massive boost to his XP.

> **💡 Tip:** Vulture generally pays 50% less than other vendors for common items. Only sell to him if you are specifically trying to grind his reputation to unlock the Large Safe.

## Tactical Advice for Blueprint Hunting

Acquiring a **gray zone warfare blueprint** often requires entering the most dangerous "hot zones" on the map. Midnight Sapphire and Tiger Bay are notorious for high AI density and player ambushes.

### The Double-Tap Interaction
One of the most common mistakes players make is failing to actually pick up the blueprint. When you find a document:
*   **First Interaction:** Your character will "Inspect" the item.
*   **Second Interaction:** You must press the interact key again to actually put the blueprint into your inventory.
*   **Verification:** Check your quest inventory or backpack to ensure the item is physically there before leaving the area.

### Strategic Extraction
Since blueprints are lost on death, your extraction path is critical. When looting the SVD blueprint in the Tiger Bay Mall, do not try to fight your way out through the main entrance. Use the back service corridors and wait for a clear window to run for the LZ. If you are playing on a PvP server, consider doing blueprint runs during off-peak hours or with a full squad to provide security during the interaction animation.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I find the same gray zone warfare blueprint multiple times?**

A: Once you have successfully extracted with a blueprint and turned it in to the respective vendor, it will no longer appear for you in that world instance. However, if you fail to extract, it will respawn in its original location in a new session.

**Q: Does Vulture's location change every day?**

A: No, Vulture operates on a weekly rotation. Once he is spotted at a Major COP like Harrison, he will remain there for the duration of the week across all servers in that region.

**Q: Why can't I see Vulture in my vendor list?**

A: You must first visit the Major COP where he is currently located while your faction controls it. Once you have "discovered" him for the week, he should appear in your vendor tab, though you can only trade with him when you are physically at a captured COP.

**Q: What is the best way to farm reputation for Vulture?**

A: Focus on looting "Military Equipment" items from elite guards at Midnight Sapphire. While Vulture pays less than other traders, the reputation gain is tied to the value of the items sold. High-value electronics are also excellent for leveling him up quickly.
