## Understanding Helicopter Fast Travel in Gray Zone Warfare

Unlike many games with instant teleportation, Gray Zone Warfare's fast travel system is integrated into its tactical realism. Instead of simply clicking a point on the map and appearing there, players utilize a helicopter transport service. This method still requires careful planning, as the helicopter is not an invulnerable magic carpet, but it significantly reduces the time spent traversing dangerous terrain on foot. This system allows you to get closer to your objectives or safely return to base, making it an indispensable tool for both solo operatives and squad play.

## How to Utilize Helicopter Transport

The helicopter system serves as your primary means of deploying to the field and extracting back to your main base. It's a two-way service that needs to be initiated from specific points on the map. Here's a breakdown of the process:

### Deploying to the Field

When you're ready to embark on a mission from your main base, the helicopter transport is your first step into the hot zone.

| Step | Action | Details |
| :-- | :----- | :------ |
| **1. Open Map** | Press the designated key (usually **M** or **Tab** depending on your keybinds) to bring up the full map of Lamang Island. | The map provides an overview of all accessible areas, including your base and unlocked Landing Zones. |
| **2. Select LZ** | Click on the **Landing Zone (LZ)** closest to your desired mission objective. | LZs are marked points on the map where helicopters can safely drop off and pick up players. |
| **3. Call Transport** | After selecting an LZ, an option to **"Call Transport"** will appear. Click this to initiate the deployment. | This action begins a timer, indicating when your helicopter will arrive at your main base. |
| **4. Board Helicopter** | Once the helicopter arrives at your base, approach and **board** it with your squad. | The helicopter will then automatically fly you to the selected LZ and drop you off. |

### Returning to Base (Extraction)

After a successful (or even unsuccessful) run in the field, you'll need to extract your loot and return to safety.

| Step | Action | Details |
| :-- | :----- | :------ |
| **1. Open Map** | Press **M** or **Tab** to open your map while in the field. | You need to be within a designated Landing Zone to call for extraction. |
| **2. Select Base** | Click on your **main base** icon on the map. | This indicates your intention to return to your faction's headquarters. |
| **3. Call Transport** | Select the **"Call Transport"** option. | The system will identify the nearest unlocked LZ to your current position as the pickup point. A timer will start. |
| **4. Head to Pickup Point** | Navigate to the **designated LZ** before the timer runs out. | This is crucial; if you don't reach the LZ in time, the helicopter will leave without you. |
| **5. Board Helicopter** | Board the helicopter once it lands at the LZ. | It will then transport you and any squad members back to your main base. |

## Navigating Landing Zones (LZs)

Landing Zones are the backbone of the fast travel system in Gray Zone Warfare. These strategic locations are crucial for both deployment and extraction. Initially, you'll have access to only a few LZs near your main base, but as you explore the vast map, you will progressively unlock more.

| Aspect | Description | Importance |
| :----- | :---------- | :--------- |
| **What are LZs?** | Designated drop-off and pickup points for helicopters across Lamang Island. | They are the **only** locations outside your main base from which you can initiate or complete fast travel. |
| **Unlocking LZs** | New LZs are unlocked simply by **discovering them** during your explorations. | Actively seeking out and reaching new LZs expands your fast travel network, allowing for more strategic deployments. |
| **Visibility** | Unlocked LZs are clearly marked on your map, often with specific names (e.g., "Charlie 2 LZ," "Charlie 3 LZ"). | Easy identification helps in planning your routes and mission objectives effectively. |
| **Faction Specificity** | While LZs are universal, your faction choice determines your starting LZs and initial deployment areas. | Coordinate with friends to ensure you're in the same faction to utilize shared LZs for squad play. |

## FAQ

**Q: Can I fast travel from anywhere on the map in Gray Zone Warfare?**

A: No, you can only fast travel from your main base or from an unlocked Landing Zone (LZ). You must be physically present at one of these locations to call for helicopter transport.

**Q: How do I unlock new Landing Zones (LZs) for fast travel?**

A: New LZs are unlocked simply by exploring the map and discovering them. Once you've reached a new LZ, it will be marked on your map and become available for future deployments and extractions.

**Q: Is fast travel safe in Gray Zone Warfare?**

A: While fast travel via helicopter is generally safer than walking long distances, it's not entirely risk-free. There's a waiting period for the helicopter to arrive, during which you can be attacked by AI enemies or other players. Always clear the LZ before calling transport.

**Q: Can my squadmates use my summoned helicopter for fast travel?**

A: Yes, any players from your faction who are present at the LZ when your helicopter arrives can board it with you. Ensure good communication to make sure everyone is ready to go.
