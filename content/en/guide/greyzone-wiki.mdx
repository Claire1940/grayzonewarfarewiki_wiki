## Advanced Ballistics and Headshot Mechanics

One of the most frequent frustrations for contractors in 2026 is the perceived inconsistency of headshots against AI enemies. While many players initially blame "height over bore"—the physical distance between your scope's center and the rifle's barrel—the reality in the current Spearhead update is more complex. Tactical shooters often require you to account for realistic ballistics, but a combination of zeroing bugs and hitbox constraints can lead to missed kills even at close range.

Currently, many scopes like the Elcan are zeroed to 100 meters by default. Theoretically, engaging an enemy at 50 meters should result in a slightly high impact. However, players are reporting shots landing low. Furthermore, the head hitbox is extremely tight; if a round strikes the neck, it is currently registered as a thorax hit. Because the game does not yet model the spine as a fatal organ, these "neck shots" often fail to drop the target instantly.

| Issue | Cause | Recommended Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| **Low Impact** | Zeroing system bugs in 0.4 | Aim at the **top of the helmet** or slightly above. |
| **Non-Fatal Headshots** | Tight hitbox/Neck registered as Thorax | Use longer barrels to increase **muzzle velocity**. |
| **Zeroing Reset** | ADS/Un-ADS bug | Double-check zeroing settings after every reload. |

> **💡 Tip:** To maximize your lethality against armored AI, prioritize longer barrels. The increased velocity flattens the bullet's arc, making your point of aim much more consistent with your point of impact.

## Logistics: Helicopter Extractions and Audio Management

The helicopter system remains the lifeblood of Lamang Island. In 2026, the developers have streamlined the process, allowing birds to enter from various sides of the map, significantly reducing wait times. However, managing your extraction is still a tactical endeavor.

When calling a transport, try to be within 100 meters of the Landing Zone (LZ). If you are further away, the pilot will wait, but this increases the window for enemy players to intercept your flight. Once onboard, you can press the **L key** to expedite the takeoff, which is a lifesaver if the LZ is "hot" and taking fire.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The helicopter engine noise is intentionally deafening to simulate realism. To protect your hearing and maintain focus, press the **O key** to silence your active headset while in flight.

### Extraction Safety Protocols
1. **Call Distance:** Request pickup when 50-100m from the LZ.
2. **Invulnerability:** You are protected for 5 seconds after disembarking to prevent LZ camping.
3. **Expedite:** Hold **L** to leave immediately once seated.
4. **Noise Control:** Use the **O** hotkey to toggle headset dampening.

## The Secret Vendor: Vulture and Reputation

While most players focus on Gunny or Handshake, the **greyzone wiki** community has spent considerable time uncovering the mechanics of the secret vendor, **Vulture**. Unlike standard traders, Vulture does not appear on your map by default. He rotates his location weekly between two of the seven major Command Posts (COPs) on the island.

To find him, you must look for the triple-star triangle icons on your map. Vulture is the primary source for high-capacity safes, including the massive 108-slot container available at Loyalty Level 4.

## Faction Intel and Ammo Matching

Survival in the "Grey Zone" often depends on matching your equipment to the specific faction you are fighting. Different regions of the map are occupied by various AI groups, each utilizing specific calibers. By matching your weapon to the local supply, you can scavenge ammunition from fallen enemies, extending your operational time without needing a resupply from base.

*   **Sawmill Region:** Enemies typically carry AK-platform rifles. Use 5.45x39mm or 7.62x39mm weapons to utilize their dropped cartridges (like 5.45 PS).
*   **Hunter's Paradise:** Local hostiles often use MP5s and shotguns. The 9mm "Extreme Penetrator" rounds found here are surprisingly effective against mid-tier armor.
*   **LAF Bases:** The Lang Armed Forces utilize NATO standards. Bringing an M4 or Daniel Defense rifle allows you to scoop up plentiful 5.56 M855 ammunition.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the most up-to-date greyzone wiki information in-game?**

A: The developers have integrated a comprehensive "Handbook" into the UI. You can find it in the top-right corner of your screen. It contains over 100 pages of mechanics, including medical data and weapon systems.

**Q: How do I unlock the secret vendor Vulture?**

A: Vulture is not unlocked via a specific quest but rather by visiting his current location. He rotates weekly between two of the seven major Command Posts (marked with three stars). Check community maps or "Where is Vulture" trackers to find his current position.

**Q: Why are my headshots not killing AI enemies?**

A: This is usually due to a combination of zeroing bugs and the tight head hitbox. If your shot hits the neck, it counts as a thorax hit. Aim slightly higher than the center of the head, or use a longer barrel to ensure a flatter bullet trajectory.

**Q: Can I increase my reputation with vendors without doing missions?**

A: Yes. In the 2026 update, you earn 1 point of reputation for every $100 worth of items you sell to a specific vendor. This is the most efficient way to level up Vulture and Artisan if you prefer looting over tasking.
