Navigating the dense jungles and urban ruins of Lamang Island requires more than just a steady aim; it requires a deep understanding of **gray zone warfare extraction points**. In this tactical extraction shooter, getting into the fight is only half the battle. Your survival and the safety of your hard-earned loot depend entirely on your ability to reach a Landing Zone (LZ) and successfully call for transport back to your base camp. Unlike traditional shooters where an exit might be a simple door or a static point, **gray zone warfare extraction points** are dynamic hubs of activity where helicopters serve as your only lifeline. Whether you are a recruit for the Lamang Recovery Initiative or a mercenary for the Crimson Shield International, mastering the timing, location, and mechanics of these extraction zones is the difference between a successful mission and losing everything on your character.

## How Extraction Works in Gray Zone Warfare

The extraction system in Gray Zone Warfare is built around the use of helicopters (Little Birds) that ferry players between their faction's Base Camp and various Landing Zones scattered across the map. These LZs are not just for leaving the map; they are also your primary method of insertion.

To initiate an extraction, you must interact with your tactical map. By opening your map (default key: Tab), you can view all discovered **gray zone warfare extraction points**. It is important to note that you do not simply click the LZ you are standing at to leave. Instead, you must select your destination—usually your faction's Base Camp—and then request transport.

| Step | Action | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Open Map** | Press Tab to view your current location and nearby LZs. |
| **2** | **Select Destination** | Click on your Base Camp or a different LZ you wish to travel to. |
| **3** | **Call Transport** | Click the "Call Transport" button in the UI. |
| **4** | **Wait for Arrival** | Monitor the timer and the helicopter's icon on the map. |
| **5** | **Boarding** | Approach the helicopter once it lands and hold the interact key to board. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your surroundings before calling a helicopter. The sound of an incoming Little Bird acts as a dinner bell for both enemy AI and rival players who may be lurking near the LZ.

## Managing Landing Zones and Wait Times

One of the most complex aspects of using **gray zone warfare extraction points** is the logistics of helicopter availability. The game world has a strict limit on the number of active helicopters allowed in the air at any given time. Currently, there is a maximum of four choppers per faction. If you attempt to call for transport and receive an "Unavailable" message, it means all transport assets are currently occupied.

### The Four-Chopper Limit
When the server is busy, you may find yourself spamming the call button. A useful strategy for impatient players is to zoom into your destination on the map and position the "Call Transport" button directly over it. This allows you to click rapidly until a slot opens up as soon as another helicopter leaves the airspace.

### Proximity and Timing
The game calculates the arrival time of your transport based on your distance from the LZ. If you call for a pickup while you are still 500 meters away, the game will delay the helicopter's arrival to give you time to reach the point. 

| Scenario | Wait Time Impact | Tactical Advice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Calling from afar** | **Increased** | Use this if you are under fire and need a "hot" extraction timed for your arrival. |
| **Calling at the LZ** | **Minimized** | Provides the fastest possible pickup but leaves you exposed at the LZ longer. |
| **Busy Server** | **Variable** | You may have to wait several minutes for a helicopter to become free. |

## Unlocking New Extraction Points

When you first start your journey in 2026, your map will be largely empty. You begin with only a few LZs near your faction's starting town. To expand your reach across the island, you must physically travel to new areas. 

As you explore the map for tasks or loot, new **gray zone warfare extraction points** will automatically unlock when you enter their vicinity. These points are often located near points of interest (POIs) such as gas stations, airfields, or military camps. Once unlocked, these LZs remain available for the duration of your career with that faction, allowing you to deploy directly to those regions in future raids.

### Faction-Specific Deployments
Your choice of faction—Lamang Recovery Initiative, Mithras Security Group, or Crimson Shield International—determines your starting corner of the map. While the central regions of the island are contested, the LZs near your base are generally safer. However, always remember that friendly fire is a reportable offense; keep track of your faction members on the map (represented by blue or green squares) to avoid tragic accidents at the LZ.

## Tactical Considerations at the LZ

The moments spent waiting at **gray zone warfare extraction points** are some of the most dangerous in the game. Because the helicopter's path and landing are visible and audible to everyone in the area, you are a sitting duck during the extraction window.

1.  **Find Cover:** Never stand directly in the center of the landing circle while waiting. Hide in nearby bushes or behind structures.
2.  **Use the "Crouch-Scroll" Technique:** Use `Ctrl + Mouse Wheel` to adjust your height precisely behind cover, ensuring you have a line of sight without exposing your head.
3.  **Weapon Readiness:** Ensure your weapon is set to the appropriate fire mode. Use full-auto for close-quarters defense at the LZ, but switch to semi-auto if you need to pick off snipers watching the extraction point from a distance.
4.  **Listen for Audio Cues:** If your character is coughing or injured, use medical supplies before the chopper arrives. Noise can give away your position to an enemy player waiting to "exit camp" the LZ.

> **Warning:** If you die while waiting at an LZ, your body will remain there with all your loot. While you can attempt to run back and retrieve it, dying a second time will cause your original body (and all its gear) to despawn forever.

## Loot and Death Mechanics

Extraction is the only way to secure the items you've found. Anything stored in your "Safe Lockbox" is protected even if you fail to reach an extraction point. However, your primary weapons, armor, and backpack contents are at risk.

If you are playing in a squad and find yourself pinned down at one of the **gray zone warfare extraction points**, your teammates can help secure your gear. If you die, a friend can right-click your body and "Drop All" to put your items on the ground. This prevents the gear from despawning if you die again on your way back to reclaim your items.

| Item Type | Status on Death | Status on Successful Extraction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Safe Box Items** | **Retained** | Stays in box |
| **Equipped Weapons** | **Dropped on Body** | Secured in Base |
| **Quest Items** | **Lost** | Completed/Handed In |
| **Backpack Loot** | **Dropped on Body** | Can be sold or stashed |

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I call a helicopter to my location?**

A: There are two main reasons. First, you might be in a "busy" state where all four faction helicopters are already in use. Second, you must be within a certain proximity of a discovered Landing Zone to use it as a pickup point. You cannot call a helicopter to a random spot in the middle of the jungle.

**Q: Can enemy players see my extraction helicopter?**

A: Yes. The helicopter is a physical entity in the game world. It makes significant noise and can be seen from a great distance. Enemy players can even shoot at the helicopter, though it is quite durable. The real danger is players waiting near **gray zone warfare extraction points** to ambush you as you board.

**Q: How do I unlock more gray zone warfare extraction points?**

A: You unlock them by exploring the map. As you move toward mission objectives or new towns, you will receive a notification that a new LZ has been discovered. You can then use this LZ for both insertion and extraction in future sessions.

**Q: What happens if I miss the helicopter?**

A: If the helicopter lands and you do not board within its departure window (usually about 15-20 seconds), it will leave without you. You will have to wait for it to clear the map before you can call for another transport. Always be ready to move as soon as the wheels touch the ground.
