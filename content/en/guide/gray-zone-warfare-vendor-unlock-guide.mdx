## How to Unlock and Level Up Vendors

Progressing through the vendor ranks requires a combination of **Reputation (Rep)** and **Financial Turnover**. Reputation is primarily earned through the completion of Tasks (missions). However, as you reach the mid-game, you will encounter "split tasks" where multiple vendors want the same objective.

### The Reputation System
Each level has a specific Rep threshold. If you fail to reach this, you cannot access the next tier of gear, even if you have spent millions of dollars with that trader.

*   **Level 1 to Level 2:** Typically requires ~500 Rep.
*   **Level 2 to Level 3:** Typically requires ~1500 Rep.
*   **Level 3 to Level 4:** Requires specialized end-game tasks.

### Strategic Siding Decisions
In 2026, the meta for a **gray zone warfare vendor unlock guide** emphasizes siding with "difficult" vendors. Handshake and Gunny have a massive surplus of missions (over 50 and 28 respectively). This means you can afford to "betray" them during split missions in favor of Artisan, Banshee, or Turncoat, who have significantly fewer tasks.

1.  **Prioritize Turncoat/Banshee:** Their missions are rarer. Use split rewards to boost their Rep first.
2.  **Ignore Lab Rat after Level 2:** Most players find her Level 3 offerings (large med bags) to be a lateral move or even a downgrade compared to Level 2 efficiency.
3.  **The Artisan Trade-off:** Artisan sells critical AK-308 magazines at Level 3. If you prefer Eastern Bloc weaponry, ensure you side with her whenever Gunny is the alternative.

## The Secret Vendor: Unlocking Vulture

Introduced in the 0.4 "Spearhead" update, Vulture is a unique "Secret Vendor" who operates differently from the base traders. He does not stay in one place and does not have a standard quest-based unlock system.

### Finding Vulture's Location
Vulture spawns at two **Major COPs** (Control Points) every week. On the map, these are identified by icons with three black dots instead of one. His location rotates every Monday. To access him, your faction (or you personally) must capture the COP by clearing the AI and interacting with the central computer. Once the computer turns yellow, Vulture will become available in the vendor menu while you are in that zone.

**Common Major COP Locations:**
*   Harrison
*   Titan
*   Westmore
*   Stalwart
*   Crusader
*   Nomad

### Vulture’s Scavenged Inventory
Vulture sells gear that he has "scavenged" from fallen PMCs on your specific server. This means his stock is dynamic. If a high-level player dies and doesn't recover their gear, Vulture may sell their fully decked-out M4s, 20-inch barrels, or loot-only helmets. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Vulture is currently known to be buggy. If you capture a Major COP and he doesn't appear, try switching servers. His stock is server-dependent, so "server hopping" can be a viable strategy to find rare parts.

## Leveling the Vulture Reputation

Unlike standard traders, Vulture does not offer missions. The only way to level him up is by selling him **High Value Loot** (valuables like watches, necklaces, and rare military documents).

| Vulture Level | Unlock Requirements | Reward / Featured Item |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Default Access | Basic Scavenged Gear, Random Ammo |
| **Level 2** | Sell ~100k in Valuables | **MP7 A2** (Customizable variant) |
| **Level 3** | Sell ~300k in Valuables | **Small Safe** (Secure container for money) |
| **Level 4** | Sell ~750k in Valuables | **Large Safe** (108 slots), **AK-308** |

### The Value Trade-off
Vulture pays significantly less for valuables than Turncoat or Lab Rat (often 50% less). When following this **gray zone warfare vendor unlock guide**, you must decide if the convenience of buying a Large Safe is worth the massive loss in potential currency. Most veteran players recommend leveling him only once you have a stable bankroll of over $1,000,000.

## Fast-Tracking Vendor Progression

If you find yourself stuck behind a reputation wall, there are a few ways to "brute force" progress, though they are expensive.

1.  **Financial Pumping:** You gain a tiny amount of Rep for every dollar spent or earned with a vendor. If you are 50 points away from Level 3, buying and immediately selling back expensive suppressors can bridge the gap.
2.  **High-Tier Loot Runs:** Focus on areas like **Midnight Sapphire** or **Tiger Bay**. The high-tier military equipment found here can be sold to specific vendors (Gunny for attachments, Handshake for armor) to boost turnover quickly.
3.  **The 60k Military Item:** There is a rare military item found near the boss at the top of Midnight Sapphire worth $60,000. Selling this to Vulture is the fastest known way to reach Level 4, granting $30,000 in credit and a massive XP boost.

> **💡 Tip:** To level Artisan quickly, buy her expensive weapon kits and sell the parts back individually. It is a loss-heavy process but much faster than waiting for rare missions.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the secret vendor Vulture in Gray Zone Warfare?**

A: You do not need a specific quest to unlock Vulture. Instead, you must find him at one of the two "Major COPs" he occupies each week. Once your faction captures the COP, he will appear in your vendor list as long as you are at that location.

**Q: Which vendor should I side with during split missions?**

A: Generally, you should side with Artisan, Banshee, or Turncoat. Handshake and Gunny have so many missions that you will naturally reach Level 3 with them regardless of a few "betrayals." The other vendors have limited missions, making their reputation much harder to earn.

**Q: What is the fastest way to level up Vulture?**

A: The fastest way is to sell high-value military loot found in end-game areas like Midnight Sapphire. While Vulture pays less than other vendors, selling him valuables is the only way to increase his reputation and unlock the Large Safe.

**Q: Can I buy the AK-308 from a vendor?**

A: Yes, you can purchase the AK-308 from Vulture once you reach Level 4. Alternatively, you can occasionally find it as a reward for high-level Turncoat missions or as rare loot in military crates.
