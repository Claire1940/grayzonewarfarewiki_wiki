## Master Ballistics and Headshot Mechanics

One of the most frequent frustrations for players in the 0.4 update involves inconsistent headshots against AI combatants. While many players attribute missed shots to "height over bore"—the physical distance between your scope's center and the barrel's bore—the reality in 2026 is more complex.

Current testing reveals that zeroing is often unreliable. Even when a scope like the Elcan is zeroed to 100 meters, shots may land lower than intended. Furthermore, the game's hitbox model is incredibly precise. The neck is currently modeled as part of the thorax rather than the head. Because the spine is not a fatal organ in the current damage model, a shot to the neck often results in a non-fatal wound, allowing the AI to return fire.

| Shot Placement | Result | Tactical Advice |
|---|---|---|
| **Top of Helmet** | High Probability Kill | Aim for the very top of the head to compensate for low-striking rounds. |
| **Center Face** | Variable | Risk of hitting the neck/thorax hitbox if zeroing bugs occur. |
| **Neck Area** | Non-Fatal | Avoid; this counts as thorax damage and lacks vital organ modeling. |
| **Long Barrel** | Increased Velocity | Use longer barrels to flatten the bullet arc for more consistency. |

> **Pro Tip:** If you are using magnified optics at medium range, always aim slightly higher than you think is necessary. Aiming at the very top edge of an enemy's helmet ensures that even if the bullet drops due to zeroing issues, it will still impact the cranial cavity.

## Helicopter Logistics and Safety

Helicopters are your lifeline in Lamang, but they are also points of extreme vulnerability. Understanding the timing and safety mechanics of extractions can prevent unnecessary deaths.

In 2026, the helicopter system has been optimized to allow more frequent flights. You no longer need to "pre-book" slots as aggressively as in earlier builds. However, calling a helicopter while you are standing directly on the Landing Zone (LZ) is the most efficient method. If you call a transport from 300 meters away, the arrival timer increases significantly to allow you time to reach the LZ, often resulting in a longer wait than if you had simply walked to the zone first.

### Key Helicopter Controls & Mechanics:
*   **Expedite Takeoff:** Once seated, hold the **L** key to signal the pilot to leave immediately. This is vital when under fire.
*   **Active Noise Cancelling:** Press **O** to toggle your headset's noise reduction.
*   **Invulnerability Frames:** Players are invulnerable while seated in a moving helicopter and receive a **5-second grace period** upon landing to prevent LZ camping.

## Trader Reputation and the Secret Vendor

Leveling up traders is the primary way to access high-tier armor and armor-piercing ammunition. While tasks are the standard way to gain reputation, many players forget that selling high-value loot also contributes to your standing.

The formula for reputation gain through commerce is straightforward: **Total Sale Value / 100 = Reputation Points.**

| Item Value | Rep Gained | Best Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| $1,000 | 10 Rep | Sell common weapon parts and consumables. |
| $10,000 | 100 Rep | Focus on high-tier optics and suppressed weapons. |
| **$60,000** | **600 Rep** | Rare 3x3 items are the most efficient for leveling Artisan and Vulture. |

### Finding Vulture
Vulture is a unique "secret" vendor who does not stay in one place. He rotates weekly between two of the seven **Major COPs** (indicated by triple-star icons on your map). Vulture is the only source for the massive 108-slot safe, making him a priority for players with large inventories. To find his current location, players often consult community tools or the official [Gray Zone Warfare Steam Community](https://steamcommunity.com/app/2477940) pages.

## Enemy Factions and Ammo Matching

A common mistake in Gray Zone Warfare is bringing the wrong ammunition to a specific region. In Update 0.4, AI factions use specific gear sets. If you match your weapon's caliber to the local garrison, you can scavenge ammo from fallen enemies to sustain long deployments.

1.  **Sawmill:** Enemies here primarily use AK-platform rifles chambered in **5.45x39mm (PS)** or **7.62x39mm**. Bringing an AK-15 or AK-74 allows you to stay topped off on rounds indefinitely.
2.  **Hunter's Paradise:** This area is dominated by shotguns and MP5s. The MP5s often carry **Extreme Penetrator** rounds, which are surprisingly effective against mid-tier body armor.
3.  **LAF Bases (Trenches):** The Lang Armed Forces utilize NATO weaponry. If you are heading to the trenches (Coordinates 130 150), an M4 or Daniel Defense rifle is recommended, as you will find plenty of **M855** ammunition on the bodies of your foes.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the Banshee trader?**

A: Banshee is unlocked by progressing through the main story quests in your faction's starter town. You will typically unlock him just before the missions transition to the more difficult central regions of the map.

**Q: Why can't I rebind the "Drop Item" key?**

A: Currently, the "Delete" key is hard-coded for dropping items in the inventory. While you cannot change this in the in-game settings as of early 2026, many players use third-party mouse software to map the Delete function to a side button for faster looting.

**Q: Is there a way to see the distance to a target without a rangefinder?**

A: While binoculars with built-in rangefinders are the most accurate, you can use the map's grid system to estimate distance. Each small grid square represents 100 meters. By checking your position relative to an enemy landmark, you can calculate the approximate holdover required for your shot.

**Q: Does the "grayzone wiki" handbook update automatically?**

A: Yes, the in-game handbook is updated by the developers with every major patch (like the 0.4 Spearhead update) to reflect changes in mechanics, hitboxes, and item stats. Always check the "Recent Changes" section after a patch.
