## Combat Mechanics and Ballistics in 0.4

One of the most frequent frustrations in the 0.4 Spearhead update involves inconsistent headshots against AI. While many players attribute this to "height over bore"—the physical distance between your scope and the barrel—the reality is more complex. Current data suggests that zeroing is currently experiencing technical issues. Even when a scope like the Elcan is zeroed to 100m, shots often land lower than the point of aim.

Furthermore, the head hitbox is extremely tight. If a round lands slightly low, it strikes the neck, which is currently modeled as part of the thorax rather than the head. Because the spine is not yet fully modeled, these shots often fail to hit vital organs, resulting in non-fatal wounds.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** To compensate for zeroing bugs, aim for the very top of an enemy's helmet. This ensures that even if the round drops slightly, it still connects with the cranial hitbox.

| Feature | Mechanic Detail | Practical Advice |
|---|---|---|
| **Zeroing** | Often resets when toggling ADS | Re-verify aim points after un-aiming |
| **Barrel Length** | Increases muzzle velocity | Use longer barrels for flatter trajectories |
| **Head Hitbox** | Constrained tightly to the skull | Aim at the "mohawk" of the helmet |
| **Neck Damage** | Counts as thorax/chest hits | Avoid aiming for the chin or throat |

## Logistics: The Helicopter and Extraction System

The helicopter system remains the lifeblood of movement in Gray Zone Warfare. However, many players spend unnecessary time waiting at Landing Zones (LZs). In the 0.4 update, calling a helicopter while standing directly on the LZ results in a shorter wait time (approximately 1 minute) compared to calling it from 300 meters away, which can trigger a 2.5-minute timer.

Safety is also a major concern during extraction. You are granted 5 seconds of invulnerability upon landing to prevent LZ camping, and you are entirely invulnerable once the helicopter has successfully taken off.

### Essential Hotkeys for Logistics
1.  **Silence Headsets (O Key):** If you are wearing an active headset, press **O** to silence the deafening roar of the helicopter. This does not work if you are not wearing gear.
2.  **Expedite Takeoff (L Key):** Once seated, hold **L** to signal the pilot to leave immediately. This is vital when under fire at a contested LZ.
3.  **Binoculars (9 Key):** Use binoculars to scout areas without draining arm stamina. The Level 4 Banshee binoculars even include a built-in rangefinder.

## Trader Progression and Secret Vendors

Advancing your standing with traders is the only way to access high-tier gear like the 60k value 3x3 items or specialized ammunition. While tasks are the fastest way to gain XP, you can now gain reputation by selling high-value items to vendors. The conversion rate is roughly **100 reputation points for every $10,000 worth of goods sold**.

This is particularly important for traders like Artisan, Turncoat, and Banshee, who have fewer missions than Handshake or Gunny. 

### The Secret Vendor: Vulture
Vulture is a unique vendor who does not stay in your main base. He rotates weekly between two of the seven "Major COPs" (marked with triple-star triangles on your map). 
*   **Location Tracking:** Use community tools like `whereisvulture.com` to find his current position.
*   **The Reward:** Reaching Level 4 with Vulture unlocks a massive **108-slot safe**, allowing you to store high-value loot without cluttering your limited stash space.

## Tasking and Mission Structure

Tasks in Gray Zone Warfare are categorized into Main Tasks, Side Tasks, and Contracts. Completing these is the primary method for leveling your PMC and unlocking new equipment. Note that "Task Items" only spawn when the mission is active in your log. If you die with a task item, it may or may not stay in your secure lockbox depending on the specific mission parameters.

> **Warning:** After the 0.4 Spearhead update, many previous task locations have shifted. Always check your in-game Handbook (found in the top right corner of the UI) for the most current intel.

### Repeatable Tasks
Outside of the starter region, tasks can be repeated indefinitely. This allows players to farm reputation and money even after completing the main storyline. If you fail a repeatable task, it enters a 20-minute cooldown before it returns to the available pool. Note that rewards for subsequent completions are lower than the initial payout.

## Strategic Looting and Enemy Intel

Knowing which ammo to bring to specific zones can save your life. In [Gray Zone Warfare](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2479810/Gray_Zone_Warfare/), different AI factions utilize specific calibers, allowing you to "scavenge" as you fight if you match your weapon to the local threat.

### Region Breakdown
*   **Sawmill:** AI here primarily use AK-variant weapons (5.45x39mm and 7.62x39mm). Bringing an AK allows you to replenish ammo directly from fallen enemies.
*   **Hunter's Paradise:** Expect shotguns and MP5s. The MP5s often carry "Extreme Penetrator" rounds, which are surprisingly effective against mid-tier armor.
*   **Lamang Armed Forces (LAF) Bases:** These professional soldiers use 5.56x45mm NATO rounds. This is the best place to farm M855 ammunition for your M4 or Daniel Defense rifles.

## Quality of Life Improvements

The 0.4 update introduced several "hidden" features that streamline the gameplay experience. The most notable is the **Delete Key** functionality. You can now hover over an item in your inventory or a loot container and press the Delete key to instantly drop it. While this cannot currently be rebound in the settings, it significantly speeds up the looting process during high-intensity raids.

Additionally, the **In-Game Handbook** is a massive 110-page resource built directly into the UI. It contains detailed data on medical supplies, weapon stats, and survival mechanics. Always consult this before heading into a new biome.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find the wiki gray zone warfare secret vendor?**

A: The secret vendor, Vulture, rotates between two Major COPs every week. Major COPs are identified by triple-star icons on the map. You can also check community-run tracking sites to see his current location.

**Q: Why are my headshots not killing AI in the 0.4 update?**

A: This is usually due to a combination of zeroing bugs and a very small head hitbox. If your shot lands slightly low, it hits the "neck," which is currently part of the thorax hitbox. Aim for the top of the enemy's head to compensate.

**Q: Can I increase trader reputation without doing tasks?**

A: Yes. You receive 1 reputation point for every $100 worth of items you sell to a trader. Selling a high-value $60,000 item will net you 600 reputation points.

**Q: How do I use the new binoculars?**

A: You must first purchase them from Banshee. Place them in your dedicated PMC slot and press the **9** key to equip them. They are excellent for scouting without consuming arm stamina.
