## Advanced Ballistics and Headshot Consistency

One of the most significant hurdles players face in the 2026 update is the perceived inconsistency of headshots against AI. While many tactical shooters utilize "height over bore" mechanics—where the bullet exits the barrel lower than the optic—Gray Zone Warfare's current issues stem from a combination of zeroing bugs and hitbox constraints.

Currently, many scopes like the Elcan are zeroed to 100 meters by default. In theory, engaging a target at 50 meters should result in a high impact point. However, due to a known bug where zeroing settings often reset upon aiming down sights (ADS), shots frequently land lower than intended. Furthermore, the head hitbox is tightly modeled; missing the skull by an inch often results in hitting the "thorax" because the neck and spine are not currently modeled as fatal zones.

| Optic Type | Default Zero | Common Issue | Tactical Adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Red Dot / Holo** | 50m | Minimal deviation | Aim for the forehead. |
| **Elcan / Combat Scopes** | 100m | Hits low at mid-range | **Aim at the top of the helmet.** |
| **High-Power Scopes** | 100m+ | Zeroing resets on un-ADS | Re-check zeroing after every move. |

> **💡 Tip:** Using a longer barrel increases bullet velocity, which flattens the projectile's arc. This makes your shots more consistent at varying distances and reduces the need for aggressive holdovers.

## Logistics and Helicopter Tactics

Helicopter travel remains the primary method of insertion and extraction, but the 2026 mechanics have been streamlined. To avoid long wait times, call your transport when you are within **100 meters** of the Landing Zone (LZ). Calling it from further away triggers a longer arrival timer to "give you time to get there," which often results in players waiting at the LZ for several minutes.

### Key Helicopter Hotkeys
*   **Silence Headset (O Key):** If you are wearing an active headset, press O to dampen the deafening engine noise during transit.
*   **Expedite Takeoff (Hold L):** Once seated, hold L to signal the pilot to lift off immediately, bypassing the wait timer for other players.

> **⚠️ Warning:** You are invulnerable while seated on the helicopter after takeoff and for 5 seconds after landing. Use this brief window to scan for LZ campers before moving to cover.

## The Secret Vendor: Vulture

One of the most elusive elements in any **grey zone warfare wiki** is the location of **Vulture**. Unlike standard traders, Vulture only appears at two of the seven "Major COPs" (denoted by triple-star triangles on your map) at any given time. These locations rotate weekly.

Vulture is notoriously difficult to level up because he primarily gains reputation through sales rather than tasks. However, his Level 4 inventory includes the **108-slot massive safe**, which is the most efficient way to store high-value items like watches and jewelry without cluttering your main stash.

### Reputation Farming Formula
In 2026, you can gain reputation with any trader by selling them items. The formula is simple: **Sale Amount / 100 = Reputation Gained.**
*   Selling a **$10,000** item earns you **100 Rep**.
*   The most valuable 3x3 items sell for **$60,000**, granting a massive **600 Rep** in a single transaction.

## Tactical Base Sniping Spots

For those engaged in Faction vs. Faction (FvF) combat, mastering the terrain around the main bases is vital. All three faction bases share a similar front-end layout, meaning these spots are applicable regardless of your chosen side.

### Attacking Positions
1.  **The Dirt Mound:** Located to the left of the main entrance. This spot offers a line of sight through the fence, allowing you to pick off AI on the sandbags. AI rarely return fire effectively to this position.
2.  **The Middle House Jump:** A "secret" area that can be accessed by sprinting and jumping at the corner of the fence or using the garbage can as a boost. The audio in this house is currently bugged, making your shots sound like they are coming from multiple directions, which confuses defenders.
3.  **The Poolside Bush:** Provides excellent concealment and cover. From a prone position, you can see both the left and right roofs of the base.

### Defending Positions
*   **The Left Tent:** Accessible by vaulting onto the white container. It provides an unsuspecting angle on the houses attackers use for cover.
*   **The Truck:** A personal favorite for many veterans because it provides actual hard cover and allows for "sneaky" angles on the main road.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find the secret vendor Vulture in Gray Zone Warfare?**

A: Vulture rotates between two of the seven Major COPs every week. You can check community resources like `whereisvulture.com` to find his current 2026 coordinates. He is essential for obtaining the 108-slot safe.

**Q: Is there a way to drop items faster while looting?**

A: Yes, you can use the **Delete** key on your keyboard to instantly drop highlighted items. While this cannot currently be rebound in the settings, it significantly speeds up the process of clearing space for high-value loot.

**Q: Why aren't my headshots killing enemies instantly?**

A: In the 0.4 update, the head hitbox is very precise, and the neck is considered part of the non-fatal thorax. Combined with a zeroing bug that often causes shots to land low, it is recommended to aim for the very top of an enemy's helmet to ensure a kill.

**Q: How do I unlock the new binoculars?**

A: Binoculars are unlocked through the main story questline in the starter town. Once you progress far enough, Banshee will offer them for sale. Higher-tier versions with rangefinders become available at Banshee Level 4.
