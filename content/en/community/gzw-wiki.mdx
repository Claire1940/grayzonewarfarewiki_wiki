## The GZW Wiki Guide to Action Sounds

Understanding what your enemy hears is just as important as hearing them. In Gray Zone Warfare, many player actions are broadcast to anyone nearby, while others remain completely silent. This distinction is vital for stealthy approaches in high-traffic areas like Fort Narith or the YB-1 Bunker.

### Audible Actions
*   **Stomach Rumbling:** If your hunger or hydration is low, your character's stomach will growl. This is audible to nearby enemies and can give away your position in close-quarters combat (CQB).
*   **Aiming Down Sights (ADS):** Raising your weapon makes a distinct metallic click. While the drop-off is steep, it is easily heard through thin walls.
*   **Zeroing:** Adjusting your scope's elevation is surprisingly loud. Avoid zeroing your weapon if you suspect an enemy is in the immediate vicinity.
*   **Material Interaction:** Running on a paved road is significantly quieter than running on grass or through bushes. Use roads for stealthy approaches when possible.

### Silent Actions
*   **Inventory Management:** Opening your bag and moving items around is completely silent to other players.
*   **Repacking Magazines:** You can refill your mags without making a sound, allowing you to stay combat-ready during a stealthy hold.
*   **Dropping Bags:** If you need to shed weight for a sprint or a flank, dropping your backpack makes no noise. However, **picking it back up** does trigger an audio cue.

## Advanced Audio: Occlusion and Indoor Combat

The 0.4 update introduced a simplified occlusion system that can be deceptive. In areas like the YB-1 Bunker, the game uses "occlusion zones." If you and an enemy are separated by two or more boundaries (such as multiple heavy blast doors or specific corridor turns), sound may be completely cut off, even if you are technically only a few meters apart.

When clearing buildings, never rely 100% on your ears. A player standing just around a specific corner might be completely silent due to an occlusion layer bug. Always "slice the pie" and visually clear rooms, even if your **gzw wiki** knowledge suggests the area is quiet.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best headset to use in the GZW wiki 2026 meta?**

A: For raw distance, the **Contact 2** from Banshee Level 4 is the clear winner, offering a 65m detection range for running targets. However, for budget-conscious players, the **GSSH** from Turncoat Level 1 provides excellent value, outperforming more expensive headsets in specific walking detection tests.

**Q: Is the 2026 weapon modding system the same as Escape from Tarkov?**

A: While similar in its "part-by-part" logic, GZW currently lacks a dedicated "Workbench" interface. Players must manually inspect parts for compatibility. However, the cost savings of building from the "lower" receiver make it a mandatory skill for efficient play.

**Q: Do suppressors affect weapon damage or velocity in the 0.4 update?**

A: Suppressors primarily affect the "Muzzle Device Efficiency" stat, which impacts recoil and flash. While they don't significantly reduce damage, they are essential for avoiding AI "swarming" behaviors, as unsuppressed shots can draw enemies from a much larger radius.

**Q: How do I fix the bug where my headset stops working?**

A: This is a common issue noted in the **gzw wiki** community. To fix it, open your inventory, drag your headset to your backpack or the ground, and then put it back on. This should reactivate the audio compression and amplification filters.
