## Essential Blueprint Mechanics and Tips

Before rushing into high-threat areas like Tiger Bay or Fort Narith, you must understand how the blueprint interaction works. Many players make the mistake of thinking they have collected an item when they have only "inspected" it.

> **⚠️ Critical Warning:** When you find a blueprint, you must **double-tap the interaction button**. The first interaction usually triggers an "inspect" animation (checking the magazine or receiver). You must interact a second time to actually place the blueprint into your inventory.

Furthermore, these blueprints are quest-critical items. If you die before extracting and handing them over to the appropriate trader, you will lose the item. Some players have reported bugs in 2026 where lost blueprints do not respawn immediately, potentially stalling your progression. Always prioritize your survival once the blueprint is in your secure container or backpack.

## Midnight Sapphire: Sniper and Assault Rifle Locations

Midnight Sapphire is one of the most beautiful yet deadly locations in the game. It houses two of the most sought-after blueprints: the MCX and the M700. Because this area is heavily patrolled by elite AI, it is recommended to approach with a suppressed weapon and plenty of ammunition.

### MCX Blueprint (Assault Rifle)
The MCX is a versatile .300 Blackout rifle, perfect for suppressed builds and close-quarters combat. 
*   **Coordinates:** 171 166
*   **Specific Location:** Head to the Midnight Sapphire Hotel. Enter the ground floor and move toward the back right-hand side of the building. The blueprint is located on a table in this area.

### M700 Blueprint (Sniper Rifle)
If you prefer long-range engagements, the M700 bolt-action sniper is a must-have. It utilizes 7.62x51 NATO rounds and offers incredible stopping power.
*   **Location:** Midnight Sapphire Hotel Rooftop.
*   **Route:** Find the main stairs and ascend to the very top. Once on the roof, navigate to the far right end (opposite the stairs). The blueprint is sitting on a ledge/table overlooking the grounds.

## Fort Narith: Military-Grade Firepower

Fort Narith is a fortress teeming with armored AI. You will need to bring armor-piercing rounds (like M855 or 7.62x39 BP) to survive the barracks and jail sections. This area contains the DDM4 and the high-powered AK308.

### DDM4 Blueprint (AR Platform)
The DDM4 is a modern 5.56x45 NATO rifle known for its reliability and modularity.
*   **Coordinates:** 141 131
*   **Specific Location:** Locate the middle barrack within the Fort Narith complex. Go up the stairs to the second floor and walk to the very end of the hallway. The blueprint is sitting clearly on a table in the final room.

### AK308 Blueprint (Battle Rifle)
The AK308 is a heavy-hitting rifle that can be purchased from Vulture once the quest is complete.
*   **Coordinates:** 142 130
*   **Specific Location:** This is located in the jail section of Fort Narith. Look for a locked door at the back of the jail block. You do not necessarily need a key; this door can be **kicked open**. Once inside, look specifically under the coffee pot to find the hidden blueprint.

## Hunter's Paradise and Rural Outposts

Not all **gray zone blueprint locations** are tucked away in massive fortresses. Some are found in training camps or small, occupied villages.

### M14 / Mini-14 Blueprint (DMR)
The M14 functions as a designated marksman rifle, providing semi-auto precision for mid-to-long range fights.
*   **Coordinates:** 159 168
*   **Specific Location:** Head to Hunter's Paradise. Run past the main buildings to the shooting range at the back of the complex. Go to the very last door on the left side of the range. The blueprint is resting on a wooden bench inside.

### MP7 Blueprint (PDW)
The MP7 is the king of close-quarters combat, firing 4.6x30mm rounds that melt through body armor.
*   **Coordinates:** 188 130
*   **Specific Location:** This is located in a small, unnamed village in the middle of the jungle. Find the central house in the cluster. Inside, next to a table and a couch, you will find the blueprint.

### AKMN Jackal Company (Assault Rifle)
This specific variant features a desert camo skin and pre-installed attachments like the PKA-S scope.
*   **Coordinates:** 195 153
*   **Specific Location:** Known as the "Occupied Cottage." Look for a raised red shack. Enter the shack and look to the left of the pillow on the sleeping mat to find this unique weapon unlock.

## Remote Campsites and Urban Malls

The final blueprints require traveling to the far reaches of the map or braving the densest urban combat zones.

### Mossberg 590 Blueprint (Shotgun)
While shotguns are niche, the Mossberg 590 is devastating in forest ambushes.
*   **Coordinates:** 149 111
*   **Specific Location:** This is at a remote campsite southeast of the YBL bunker. Look for an open-front shack. The blueprint is sitting on a table among some camping gear.

### SVD Blueprint (Sniper Rifle)
The SVD is the ultimate semi-auto sniper, capable of rapid follow-up shots with the powerful 7.62x54R cartridge.
*   **Coordinates:** 197 129
*   **Specific Location:** This is located inside the Tiger Bay Mall, one of the most dangerous zones in the game. Locate the bookstore within the mall. Go behind the checkout register and through the back door. Turn right immediately, and the blueprint will be on a shelf.

> **💡 Tactical Tip:** Tiger Bay Mall is often swarming with both AI and enemy players. It is highly recommended to attempt this run with a full squad. Use smoke grenades to cross the open areas of the mall to reach the bookstore safely.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I see the blueprint even though I'm at the correct coordinates?**

A: There are two possibilities. First, another player in your session may have already picked it up (though they usually respawn after a short duration). Second, some blueprints are hidden under objects, like the AK308 which is hidden directly under a coffee pot. Look for the interaction prompt carefully.

**Q: Do I need to be on a specific quest to find these gray zone blueprint locations?**

A: While you can physically find the blueprints at any time, they are usually tied to specific missions from Gunny, Artisan, or Vulture. It is best to wait until you have the quest so you can immediately hand them in for the XP and vendor unlock.

**Q: Can I put blueprints in my secure container?**

A: Yes, it is highly recommended to place any blueprint into your secure container (Gamma, Safire, etc.) as soon as you pick it up. This ensures that even if you are killed before reaching the extraction point, you will still have the item in your inventory when you return to base.

**Q: What is the "double tap" bug people talk about?**

A: It is not necessarily a bug, but a mechanic. The game treats the first interaction as an "Inspect" action. To actually "Take" the item, you must trigger the interaction a second time. If you only press it once and run away, you will leave the blueprint behind. Always check your inventory to confirm the item is there before leaving the room.
