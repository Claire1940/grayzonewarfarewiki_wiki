## Mastering Ballistics and the Headshot "Bug"

In the 2026 landscape of Lamang, many players have reported frustration with headshots failing to register against AI combatants. While "height over bore" is a factor in any realistic military simulation, the current 0.4 update features specific quirks regarding zeroing and hitboxes. Currently, the head hitbox is extremely tight, and the neck is categorized as part of the thorax. Because the spine is not uniquely modeled, a neck shot often fails to be fatal, leading players to believe their shots disappeared.

To compensate for these issues, experts recommend aiming for the very top of an enemy's helmet. This ensures that even with slight mechanical inconsistencies or zeroing bugs—where settings often reset after toggling ADS—the round is more likely to impact the vital brain box rather than the non-lethal upper chest area.

| Ballistic Factor | Impact on Gameplay | Recommended Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| **Zeroing Bug** | Scopes may reset to default 100m or hit low regardless of setting. | Aim at the **top of the helmet** for mid-range targets. |
| **Neck Hitbox** | Categorized as Thorax; non-fatal without spine modeling. | Avoid "center mass" headshots; aim higher than the nose. |
| **Barrel Length** | Shorter barrels decrease velocity and increase bullet arc. | Use **long barrels** for flatter trajectories and consistency. |
| **Height Over Bore** | Bullet exits barrel, not the optic. | At sub-25m, aim significantly higher than the reticle. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always double-check your zeroing after un-aiming. In the current 0.4 build, some optics fail to retain their manual distance settings between engagements.

## Locating Vulture: The Secret Vendor

One of the most significant additions in 2026 is Vulture, a secret vendor who does not appear on your base menu by default. Vulture specializes in scavenged PMC gear and high-capacity storage solutions. Unlike standard traders, you must physically locate him at specific Major Command Operations (COPs) around the map.

Vulture's location rotates weekly between two of the seven Major COPs. These locations are marked on your map with triple-star triangles. Capturing the COP is often necessary to access his terminal. While he buys items for less than other traders, he is the only source for the massive 108-slot Large Safe, making him essential for end-game inventory management.

## Tactical Equipment: Binoculars and Headsets

The 0.4 update has finally introduced dedicated binoculars, accessible via the '9' key. These are vital for scouting enemy positions without draining arm stamina, which occurs when using weapon optics. Binoculars are purchased from Banshee and vary in zoom levels and functionality.

Additionally, a "hidden" feature that many players overlook is the ability to silence active headsets. By pressing the 'O' key while wearing electronic ear protection, you can dampen the deafening sound of helicopter rotors. This allows for better communication with your squad during transit without needing to adjust system volume settings.

### Binocular Progression
1. **Standard Binoculars:** Low zoom, wide field of view. Best for general scouting.
2. **High-Zoom Binoculars:** Narrower FOV. Useful for long-range target identification.
3. **Rangefinder Binoculars (Banshee Lvl 4):** Integrated distance tracking. Essential for snipers to calculate holdovers.

## Advanced Transportation and Extraction

Helicopter logistics have been streamlined in 2026 to reduce downtime. To minimize waiting at a Landing Zone (LZ), call your extraction when you are approximately 100 meters away. Calling from further distances triggers a longer arrival timer, often leaving you exposed for several minutes.

> **💡 Tip:** Once seated in the helicopter, hold the **'L' key** to expedite takeoff. This is critical when taking fire at an LZ, as it signals the pilot to depart immediately rather than waiting for other potential passengers.

Players also enjoy a 5-second invulnerability window upon landing at an LZ. This mechanic is designed to combat "LZ camping," giving you a brief moment to find cover or identify threats before taking damage.

## Boosting Trader Reputation Without Tasks

While missions are the primary way to level up traders, you can also gain reputation through commerce. This is particularly useful for traders like Artisan, Turncoat, and Banshee, who may have fewer available tasks in certain phases of the game.

The reputation gain is calculated as **Sale Amount / 100**. For example, selling a high-value military item worth $10,000 will grant you 100 reputation points. In high-tier loot areas like Midnight Sapphire, players can find 3x3 slot items worth upwards of $60,000, providing a massive 600-point boost to their standing with a specific vendor.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the most updated grayzone warfare wiki info for Vulture's location?**

A: Because Vulture rotates weekly, the best way to find him is to check Major COPs (triple-star icons) or use community-driven tools like "whereisvulture.com" which tracks his current position across all servers.

**Q: Why do my headshots feel inconsistent in the 0.4 update?**

A: This is usually due to a combination of the "neck" being part of the thorax hitbox and zeroing bugs. It is recommended to aim for the very top of the enemy's head/helmet to ensure a kill.

**Q: How do I drop items quickly while looting?**

A: You can use the **Delete key** to instantly drop a highlighted item. Note that this key is currently hard-bound and cannot be changed in the settings menu as of early 2026.

**Q: Is there a way to make the helicopters quieter?**

A: Yes, if you are wearing an active headset (electronic earpro), press the **'O' key** to toggle the noise-dampening feature. This specifically reduces environmental and vehicle noise.
